## II. Central Banks & Macro

### 3. April U.S. PCE: elevated year-on-year prints, softer monthly core
**Summary:** BEA data released May 28 showed headline PCE up 3.8% year over year in April — the highest since May 2023 and in line with expectations. Core PCE rose 3.3% year over year, also a multi-year high. On a monthly basis, core PCE increased 0.2%, below March’s 0.3% and beneath the 0.3% consensus; headline PCE rose 0.4% month over month versus a 0.5% forecast. Investopedia noted Iran-war-related energy prices remained a driver, though the monthly energy jump slowed from March. The 10-year Treasury yield eased to roughly 4.44% after the release.
