## III. Policy & Global Trade

### 3. U.S. Court of International Trade weighs in on 10% global duties under Trade Act Section 122
**Summary:** Multiple outlets reported that on May 7 the U.S. Court of International Trade issued a 2–1 decision challenging the Trump administration’s legal basis for broad import surcharges—commonly described as ~10% global-type tariffs—invoked under **Section 122** of the Trade Act of 1974. Coverage emphasizes statutory intent (narrow, balance-of-payments–linked emergency tools) versus how the administration framed deficits. For markets, the core issue is whether **enforcement narrows or the path shifts to appeals/alternative authorities**, changing baseline corporate tax-rate assumptions in models.
**Links:**
- [WION — US trade court rules against Donald Trump's 10% global tariffs under 1974 law](https://www.wionews.com/world/us-trade-court-rules-against-donald-trump-s-10-global-tariffs-under-1974-law-1778193364640)

**Commentary:** The equity read is not one-sided. A **dovish tariff interpretation** can ease imported inflation fears; an **uncertain policy response** (appeals, legislative fights, replacement statutes) can lift event risk and intra-week volatility. Favor watching import-intensive sectors and global supply-chain names for the largest sensitivities.
