### 4. ICCT: EU plug-in hybrids show "shocking" real-world CO₂ gap vs official figures (Research / Europe)
**Summary:** electrive.com on June 3, 2026 covered an ICCT study of ~8 million vehicles: EU new-car official CO₂ fell 28% from 2018–2023 while real-world emissions fell only 15%; PHEVs show the largest official vs on-road gap, and newer models are not clearly emitting less in reality. The Commission adjusted the PHEV "utility factor" in 2025 with another fix planned for 2027, but a preliminary European Parliament draft would suspend future corrections. ICCT Europe MD Peter Mock said even post-2025 fixes, on-road emissions remain poorly estimated.

**Links:**

- [electrive.com — ICCT study finds high CO₂ emissions from modern plug-in hybrids](https://www.electrive.com/2026/06/03/icct-study-finds-high-co%e2%82%82-emissions-from-modern-plug-in-hybrids/)
