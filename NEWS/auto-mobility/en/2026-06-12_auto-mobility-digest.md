### 1. VW, Stellantis, Renault push "Made in Europe" — 70% of vehicles must carry 70% local value (Policy / Europe)
**Summary:** ANSA and Reuters reported on June 12, 2026 that Volkswagen, Stellantis, and Renault — together accounting for roughly 60% of European car output — sent a joint proposal to the European Parliament calling for a simple "Made in Europe" framework: 70% of vehicles sold in the EU would need 70% local value content sourced within the 27 member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway, spanning engineering through manufacturing. The groups cited weak demand (about 3 million fewer annual sales than in 2019), high energy costs, and regulatory pressure, and urged battery localization support and regulatory flexibility for small cars to counter Chinese EV imports and stem industrial outsourcing. The letter stressed Europe is not closing itself off, but stopping further outsourcing of production to third countries.

### 2. India drops licensing for 77GHz radar and 5.9GHz V2X spectrum, clearing path for ADAS and autonomy (Policy / India)
**Summary:** CNA reported on June 12, 2026 that the Indian government issued notices on Thursday waiving separate spectrum licensing for crash-avoidance and self-driving systems: the 77GHz–81GHz band for radar sensors and the 5.9GHz band for vehicle-to-everything (V2X) communications. The move aligns India with U.S. and EU standards, allowing standardized off-the-shelf hardware rather than costlier local variants and accelerating advanced driver-assistance (ADAS) adoption. Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Maruti Suzuki, Tata Motors, Mahindra & Mahindra, and suppliers including Bosch, Continental, and Qualcomm stand to benefit. India recorded more than 177,000 road deaths in nearly half a million accidents in 2024; a 2023 government panel noted V2X fills gaps where sensors fail in blind-spot scenarios.

**Links:**

- [CNA — India clears way for self-driving, safety car tech to reduce road deaths](https://www.channelnewsasia.com/business/india-clears-way-self-driving-safety-car-tech-reduce-road-deaths-6178901)

**Commentary:** The world's third-largest car market used spectrum deregulation instead of slow legislation — one regulatory step may do more than subsidies to push ADAS from luxury cars into mass-market models.

## II. Autonomous Driving & ADAS

### 4. Belgium becomes fifth EU state to approve Tesla FSD Supervised; bloc-wide vote still absent from June 30 agenda (Autonomous / Europe)
**Summary:** newmobility.news updated on June 12, 2026 that Flemish Mobility Minister Annick De Ridder signed formal approval on June 10, making Belgium the fifth EU member to recognize Tesla Full Self-Driving (FSD) Supervised; under Belgium's federal structure, one regional authorization applies nationwide. The Dutch RDW type approval has triggered mutual recognition in Lithuania, Estonia, and Denmark. Owners must complete an in-car tutorial and pass a two-question quiz under UN Regulation 171 before first use. FSD remains SAE Level 2; the driver stays legally responsible. The EU Technical Committee on Motor Vehicles (TCMV) June 30 agenda lists only continued discussion on the Art. 39 authorization — no vote — while Germany, France, and Italy still prefer a coordinated EU decision. Tesla has replaced Europe's one-time FSD purchase with a €99/month subscription.

**Links:**

### 6. Swedish housing pilot uses V2G: Volkswagen and Vattenfall let EVs power homes (V2G / Europe)
**Summary:** The Straits Times reported on June 12, 2026 on a vehicle-to-grid (V2G) pilot in Hudiksvall, Sweden: housing association BRF Stenberg, Volkswagen, and utility Vattenfall let eight households use bidirectional chargers to charge cars off-peak and feed battery power back to homes during peak hours or outages, alongside rooftop solar and stationary storage for near self-sufficiency. Resident Filip Kiltorp said electricity bills are far lower than comparable homes; Gävle University demonstrated keeping a building powered for hours during a minister's speech mid-blackout. KTH professor Lina Bertling Tjernberg said the next step is bidirectional charging on every EV, though Sweden's EV penetration still trails Norway and Denmark.

**Links:**

- [The Straits Times — 'Battery on wheels': Sweden powers homes with EVs](https://www.straitstimes.com/world/europe/battery-on-wheels-sweden-powers-homes-with-evs)

**Commentary:** Unlike GM's fleet-wide V2G OTA activation, Sweden's pilot shows community-scale virtual power plants work in Nordic residential settings — the bottleneck remains bidirectional charging as standard equipment and grid rules.
