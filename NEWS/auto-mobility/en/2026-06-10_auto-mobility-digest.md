## I. Autonomous Driving & Technical Standards

### 1. Waymo Publishes "Reference Driver" Virtual Human Benchmark in Nature Communications (Autonomous Driving)
**Summary:** The Verge, TechCrunch, and Engadget reported on June 10, 2026, that Waymo and Delft University of Technology (TU Delft) published a cognitive model called "Reference Driver" (ReD) in Nature Communications. Built on an active inference framework, the model simulates how human drivers anticipate and decide in the seconds to minutes before a collision — not just last-second reactive maneuvers. Waymo likens it to a "behavioral crash test dummy" for batch-evaluating autonomous systems against a "careful and competent" human baseline in virtual environments, and is working with SAE and regulators toward industry consensus. Research code is open-sourced under an academic, non-commercial license.

- [The Verge — Waymo built a virtual driver to study how humans react to surprises on the road](https://www.theverge.com/transportation/947178/waymo-reference-driver-model-surprise-avoid-collision)

**Commentary:** AV competition is shifting from mileage narratives to quantifiable human benchmarks — if regulators adopt ReD, it could become a new yardstick for robotaxi safety claims.

## II. Policy & Markets

### 3. UK Government Launches Public EV Charging Cost Review; Report Due This Autumn (Policy / UK)
**Summary:** Auto Express reported on June 10, 2026, that the UK government has formally launched its promised review of public EV charging costs, addressing affordability pressure on drivers without home charging. Industry group ChargeUK says public charging prices have risen roughly 38% since 2021. The review will cover on-street slow charging, destination charging, and high-power en-route chargers, and assess cost trajectories through 2030 plus government and industry actions to reduce prices; scope is limited to public charging, excluding home charging. Advocate group EVA England expressed frustration that the review excludes the 20% VAT on public charging (vs. 5% on home energy).

**Links:**

- [Auto Express — Government launches review into electric car charging costs](https://www.autoexpress.co.uk/news/369768/government-launches-review-electric-car-charging-costs)

**Commentary:** The UK's electrification bottleneck has moved from charger availability to charger affordability — review outcomes will directly affect conversion among drivers without private parking.

**Summary:** Reuters via Global Banking and Finance on June 10, 2026: Renault Group CEO François Provost said in Brussels the EU should encourage Chinese automakers to source parts in Europe, not just assemble vehicles locally — auto parts account for roughly 95% of added value, so local sourcing benefits European jobs and suppliers. ### 7. Nio Helps Shape Singapore SS 722 Standard, First to Codify Battery Swap & Mobile Charging (Charging / Singapore)
**Summary:** CnEVPost reported on June 10, 2026, that Singapore Standard SS 722 for EV charging and battery swap systems was officially released, with Nio as a core contributor. As of the report date, Nio operated 3,904 swap stations and 5,056 charging stations in China.
