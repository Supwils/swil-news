## I. Policy & Regulation

### 1. Delhi Cabinet formally approves EV Policy 2.0 — ₹150 billion outlay, petrol two-wheeler ban from 2028 (Policy / India)
**Summary:** The Economic Times, car&bike, and Free Press Journal reported on June 29, 2026, that the Delhi Cabinet approved Electric Vehicle Policy 2.0, effective July 1 through March 31, 2030, with roughly ₹150 billion (₹15,000 crore) planned over four years. EVs priced up to ₹3 million ex-showroom receive 100% road tax and registration fee waivers; purchase incentives include up to ₹30,000 for e-two-wheelers, ₹50,000 for e-three-wheelers, and ₹1 lakh for N1 goods vehicles in year one; scrappage incentives of ₹5,000–₹1 lakh apply when replacing older polluting vehicles. Phased ICE bans: only electric three-wheelers and N1 trucks from January 1, 2027; only electric two-wheelers from April 1, 2028. Over 30,000 charging points are planned, with subsidies via direct benefit transfer. The government targets 95% electric new registrations by 2027; hybrid subsidy inclusion remains contested in the final text.

### 2. U.S. federal Section 30C home charger tax credit expires tomorrow — last federal EV incentive window (Policy / U.S.)
**Summary:** U.S. Code 26 USC §30C and IRS guidance (June 2026 edition) confirm the Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit terminates for qualified property placed in service after June 30, 2026. Homeowners installing residential chargers can claim 30% of installation costs, up to $1,000 per charging port, in eligible census tracts before the deadline; commercial equipment qualifies at 6%. Section 30D new-vehicle credits, Section 25E used-vehicle credits, and Section 45W commercial-vehicle credits already ended after September 30, 2025 — making 30C one of the few remaining federal EV-related benefits.

**Links:**

- [26 USC §30C — Alternative fuel vehicle refueling property credit](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section30C&num=0&edition=prelim)

**Summary:** Bloomberg, San Antonio Express-News, and TechCrunch reported June 26–29, 2026, that Tesla settled litigation with the family of a 2023 fatal crash victim; terms undisclosed. Tesla said on an April 2026 earnings call it replaced cameras on older vehicles and continues cooperating with regulators. The settlement ends civil litigation; the federal probe remains open with recall possible.

## III. Batteries & Charging

### 5. CATL joins BMW, Xiaomi, Google, and others to set global battery circular-economy standards; Battery Circular Design Guide due 2027 (Batteries / Global)
**Summary:** CarNewsChina reported June 29, 2026, that during London Climate Action Week, CATL — the world's largest cell manufacturer — joined BMW, Xiaomi, Google, Renault, Volvo, and others under Ellen MacArthur Foundation coordination to launch a unified battery circular-economy framework.
