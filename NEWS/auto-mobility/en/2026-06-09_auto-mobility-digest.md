## I. Policy & Geopolitics

### 1. US Defense Department adds BYD, NIO, and EV supply-chain firms to "Chinese military companies" list (Policy / US-China)
**Summary:** CNBC, Al Jazeera, and CnEVPost reported on June 9, 2026 that the US Department of Defense updated its Section 1260H "Chinese military companies" list, designating BYD, NIO Inc, Alibaba, Baidu, and multiple new-energy and autonomous-driving supply-chain companies. Battery makers CATL, CALB, and Eve Energy; lidar suppliers Hesai Group and RoboSense; and BOE Technology and Unitree Robotics were also listed. The roster grew from 134 firms in 2025 to 188. The designation does not automatically impose sanctions but bars direct DoD contracting starting later this month and third-party procurement of listed products or services from June 2027. BYD and NIO told the Hong Kong exchange the designation is "unjustified"; NIO said it will engage the DOD and reserves legal action. Former Trump White House official John McEntee criticized adding carmakers as "ridiculous."
