## I. Policy & Geopolitics

### 1. US Defense Department adds BYD, NIO, and EV supply-chain firms to "Chinese military companies" list (Policy / US-China)
**Summary:** CnEVPost and CarNewsChina reported on June 9, 2026 that the US Department of Defense, in a Federal Register notice scheduled for publication this week, designated BYD (HKEX: 1211), NIO Inc (NYSE: NIO), and multiple Chinese new-energy and autonomous-driving supply-chain companies as "Chinese military companies" under Section 1260H of the National Defense Authorization Act. Battery makers CATL, CALB, and Eve Energy; lidar suppliers Hesai Group and RoboSense; and BOE Technology were also listed. A version of the list briefly appeared in February before being withdrawn. The update extends scrutiny from traditional defense into EV batteries and AD sensors. The designation does not automatically impose sanctions but restricts US military contracting and research funding and sends a warning to investors. Former Trump White House official John McEntee criticized adding carmakers as "ridiculous," arguing Ford and GM would qualify under the same logic.

**Summary:** CnEVPost reported on June 9, 2026 that NIO issued a statement calling the Defense Department's inclusion "unjustified," denying it is a Chinese military company or military-civil fusion contributor, and reserving the right to take legal action.
