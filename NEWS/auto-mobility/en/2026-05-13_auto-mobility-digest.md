## II. Autonomous driving & smart mobility

### 4. Volvo × Aurora broaden supervised autonomous trucking with Dallas–Oklahoma City lane  
**Summary:** Volvo Autonomous Solutions and Aurora disclosed **May 4, 2026** activation of roughly a **200-mile** interstate freight corridor connecting **Dallas to Oklahoma City** using the **Volvo VNL Autonomous** integrated with the **Aurora Driver**. The program currently runs **five days per week in supervised autonomy** with direct delivery to Oklahoma City-area customer facilities aimed at simplifying drayage and hand-offs. Volvo reiterates an industrial roadmap building **“hundreds”** of autonomy-ready trucks targeted for production by **~2027**, while stressing final validation milestones toward supervised-to-driverless operations.  
**Links:**
- [Volvo Autonomous Solutions — Volvo and Aurora autonomous truck corridor press release](https://www.volvoautonomoussolutions.com/en-en/news-and-insights/press-releases/2026/may/volvo-and-aurora-launch-autonomous-truck-route-to-oklahoma-city.html)

### 7. India green-lights roughly 4.9 thousand public chargers under PM E-Drive alongside national interoperability ambition  
**Summary:** Reporting dated **May 13, 2026** states India approved **4,874** public chargers under **PM E‑Drive** funding of roughly **₹503.86 crore** (**about €44 million** per published conversion), incorporating proposals from **HPCL**, **BPCL**, **IOCL**, and several state governments still targeting **>72,000** chargers nationwide. Officials are also preparing **Unified Bharat eCharge**, a digital platform meant to unify locating, booking, and paying across heterogeneous networks. Separate ministerial talking points reference an **₹18,100 crore** production-linked incentive envelope for advanced chemistry cell manufacturing and cite **27,737** cumulative nationwide charger installations recorded by **March**, with not every site necessarily online simultaneously.

## IV. Supply chain & input-cost stress

### 10. West Asia disruptions propagate into India’s autos—executives quantify logistics drag & commodity shocks  
**Summary:** Published **May 10, 2026**, India’s Business Standard synthesizes quarterly commentary from **Bajaj Auto**, **Hero MotoCorp**, and **Ather Energy** alleging that escalating **West Asian** geopolitical risk is feeding metals inflation, tighter electronics supply, and freight insurance premia—with ripple effects across two-wheelers and EV startups alike. Bajaj Auto leadership estimates logistics and supply constraints constrained the company’s ability to service demand by roughly **10–15 %** during the referenced window, while warning commodity inflation could translate into a hit on the order of **3.5–4 % of revenue** in the coming quarter, only partly offset by staggered price increases. Hero discusses broad-based metal, rubber, and polymer inflation with roughly **2 %** list-price adjustments, while Ather cites rare-earth magnets, memory pricing, and lithium packs as pain points after cumulative hikes nearing **₹4,000** this calendar year, with potential for additional moves should costs persist.
**Links:**
