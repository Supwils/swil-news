## I. Policy & Markets

### 1. China's NEV retail falls 8% in first half of June; penetration holds near 64% (Market / China)
**Summary:** CnEVPost reported on June 17, 2026, that CPCA data showed China's passenger NEV retail reached 341,000 units from June 1–14, down 8% year on year — narrowing from a 14% drop in the first week — while rising 5% from the same period last month. NEV penetration stood at 63.9%, easing from a weekly high of 66.7% but still above year-ago levels. Overall passenger vehicle retail totaled 534,000 units, down 18% year on year; pure fuel light-vehicle production plunged 44% to 225,000 units. Wholesale was stronger: NEV wholesale hit 378,000 units, up 10% year on year, with a 67.9% penetration rate. The CPCA cited base effects, pre-college-exam caution, and the absence of last year's subsidy-driven panic buying; industry inventory at end-May stood at 3.48 million units, enough for roughly 66 days of sales.

### 2. Brookings releases US state EV policy scorecard as federal support retreats (Policy / US)
**Summary:** The Brookings Institution published "States at the wheel" on June 17, 2026, benchmarking state-level EV policy implementation on a 13-point scale (actual scores ranged 0–11). The report notes that since 2025, federal policymakers have accelerated the expiration of consumer tax credits, reduced charging deployment support, and expanded fossil-fuel subsidies; in 2026, the EPA moved to rescind the 2009 greenhouse gas endangerment finding and related vehicle standards. Northeastern and West Coast states scored highest; six states — mostly in the South — scored zero across all indicators. With federal support contracting, divergent state charging infrastructure and purchase incentives are becoming the key variables shaping US electrification pace.

**Links:**

- [Brookings — States at the wheel: A state policy scorecard on electric vehicle readiness](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/states-at-the-wheel-a-state-policy-scorecard-on-electric-vehicle-readiness/)

**Summary:** Reuters via Global Banking and Finance reported on June 17, 2026, that a VDA survey of 116 automotive suppliers (conducted May 12–24) found nearly one-third expect business conditions to worsen over the next year, while only 25% are optimistic.
