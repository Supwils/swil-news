### 1. Delhi EV Policy 2.0 Nears Finalization; Tesla Flags ₹30 Lakh Incentive Cap (Policy / India)
**Summary:** The Hindu BusinessLine, The Hindu, and NDTV reported on June 28, 2026 that Delhi's Electric Vehicle Policy 2.0 (2026–2030) is in its final review stage. The draft offers 100% road tax and registration fee waivers for battery-electric vehicles priced up to ₹30 lakh (about 3 million rupees), and 50% waivers for strong hybrids in the same bracket. It also bans new ICE three-wheeler registrations from January 2027 and new ICE two-wheeler registrations from April 2028, targeting 95% EV adoption by 2027. Tesla, through representatives and external consultants, told the Delhi government that the price cap would hinder premium EV growth — its Model Y starts at roughly ₹50.89 lakh and would receive no incentives. Industry stakeholders also disagree on whether strong hybrids should qualify for the 50% waiver and whether charging infrastructure can scale from roughly 5,000 to 18,000 public points on schedule.

### 2. UN WP.29 Approves First Global Autonomous Driving Technical Regulation (ADS GTR) (Regulation / Global)
**Summary:** UN News and Voi reported on June 24, 2026 that the UN Economic Commission for Europe's World Forum for Harmonization of Vehicle Regulations (WP.29) formally adopted the Automated Driving Systems Global Technical Regulation (ADS GTR), jointly led by China, the EU, the UK, the US, Canada, and Japan. The rules require manufacturers to implement audited safety management systems across the full ADS lifecycle, demonstrate performance at least matching a competent human driver through simulation, track, and road testing, and equip vehicles with automated-driving data recording systems for continuous monitoring. Roughly 90 existing UN regulations were also amended to remain applicable to vehicles without steering wheels or pedals. The framework is expected to enter into force in roughly one month.

**Links:**

- [UN News — New global rules clear the road for driverless vehicles](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/06/1167797)
- [Voi — Global Rules for Autonomous Cars Approved](https://voi.id/en/technology/582102)

**Commentary:** A decade after "autonomous driving is imminent" headlines, the world finally has a mutual-recognition compliance floor — fragmented national rules give way to shared validation methods, easing cross-market robotaxi scaling.

### 3. US Federal Section 30C Home Charger Credit Expires June 30; Purchase Credits Fully Gone (Policy / US)
**Summary:** GearUp Insights and AutoVerseUSA reported in June 2026 that federal Section 30D new clean vehicle credits (up to $7,500), Section 25E used EV credits, and Section 45W commercial credits all ended for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025. The only remaining federal EV-related incentive in June 2026 is Section 30C for home charging installation (30% of costs), but residential projects placed in service after June 30, 2026 no longer qualify. Meanwhile, the NEVI federal charging program resumed after a 2025 pause, with about 42 states approved for 2026 deployment plans as of April 2026. EPA also submitted additional California emissions waivers to Congress for review in June.

**Summary:** The Straits Times and Financial Times reported on June 28, 2026 that new Shenzhen rules take effect July 1, allowing the government to promote orderly robotaxi testing, demonstrations, and commercial pilots in selected zones or citywide. Drivers fear intensifying competition, while operators say robotaxis currently complement rather than replace existing mobility ecosystems.

### 5. NHTSA Proposes Removing Mandatory Brake Pedal for Fully Driverless Vehicles (Regulation / US)
**Summary:** CBT News, citing Bloomberg, reported in June 2026 that the US Department of Transportation's NHTSA proposed revising Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards to allow vehicles designed exclusively for driverless operation to omit manual brake pedals. The change would not apply to passenger vehicles that may still be human-driven. The proposal aligns with the Trump administration's broader effort to ease autonomous vehicle regulation and is especially relevant for Tesla's pedal-free Cybercab and purpose-built robotaxis from Waymo and Zoox. The Autonomous Vehicle Industry Association has also urged a fuller federal policy framework.

**Links:**

- [CBT News — NHTSA brake pedal rule could revolutionize the robotaxi market in 2026](https://www.cbtnews.com/nhtsa-brake-pedal-rule-autonomous-vehicles/)

**Commentary:** Removing the physical "must have pedals" requirement is a key step toward compliance for purpose-built robotaxis — but safety validation standards will not relax with it.
