### 1. China’s April retail NEV penetration tops 60% for the first time, even as headline NEV volumes slip YoY — BEV rebounds, PHEVs weaken
**Summary:** According to data attributed to China’s Passenger Car Association (CPCA) and echoed by trade media, nationwide passenger **NEV retail** reached **849,000 units** in **April**, down **around 6.8% YoY**, but the broader passenger market fell faster — roughly **21.5% YoY** on roughly **1.384 million retail units**, pushing monthly **NEV penetration to 61.4%**. **Battery electric vehicles (BEVs)** posted **approximately 579,000 retail units**, up roughly **2.4% YoY**, while plug-in hybrids (excluding range-extenders in separate line items cited by reporters) slid about **25.2% YoY** to roughly **192,000 units**. Exports staged a standout month: passenger exports were about **769,000 units**, roughly **80.7% higher YoY**; roughly **406,000** of those counted as **NEVs**, up about **112% YoY**, with **NEVs accounting for roughly 53% of outbound passenger volume** for what outlets describe as the first time past half.
