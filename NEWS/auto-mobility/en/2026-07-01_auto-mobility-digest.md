## I. Policy & Electrification

### 1. China's GB 38031/GB 18384 Mandatory Standards Take Effect Today — World's First "No Fire, No Explosion" Battery Rule (Policy / Battery / China)
**Summary:** CnEVPost and CarNewsChina reported on June 30, 2026, that two mandatory national standards — Safety Requirements for Power Batteries for Electric Vehicles (GB 38031-2025) and Safety Requirements for Electric Vehicles (GB 18384-2025) — take effect on July 1. GB 38031 upgrades the safety philosophy from a "5-minute warning before fire" to system-level "no fire, no explosion" after single-cell thermal runaway, and adds mandatory bottom-impact and post-300-fast-charge-cycle short-circuit tests. GB 18384 abolishes software-only power cut-off and mandates an independent physical one-touch high-voltage disconnect within one second. New models must comply immediately; existing models have until July 1, 2027. Industry estimates suggest battery system costs may rise 15%–20% (roughly RMB 3,000–5,000 per vehicle); leading makers such as CATL and EVE Energy say they prepared early, while low-end capacity faces accelerated exit pressure.

### 2. Delhi Electric Vehicles Policy 2026 Takes Effect Today — Full Tax Exemption for EVs Priced Up to Rs 30 Lakh (Policy / India)
**Summary:** The Economic Times and Times of India reported June 29–July 1, 2026, that the Delhi government has formally notified the Delhi Electric Vehicles Policy 2026, effective July 1 through March 31, 2030. The policy grants 100% road tax and registration fee exemptions for battery-electric cars with ex-showroom prices up to Rs 30 lakh; e-two-wheeler purchase subsidies up to Rs 30,000 in year one; and scrappage incentives up to Rs 1 lakh for replacing BS-IV and older vehicles (limited to the first 100,000 eligible applicants). Phased ICE restrictions include electric-only three-wheeler and N1 goods vehicle registrations from January 1, 2027, and electric-only two-wheeler registrations from April 1, 2028; strong hybrids were excluded from subsidies in the final cabinet version. The government plans roughly Rs 15,000 crore in investment over four years and more than 30,000 charging points, with subsidies disbursed via an Aadhaar-linked portal through Direct Benefit Transfer.

### 3. Bangladesh FY2026-27 EV Incentives Take Effect Today — Zero Import Duty on Electric Buses/Trucks, Charging Infrastructure Tax Wiped Out (Policy / South Asia)
**Summary:** Mongabay, The Daily Star, and bdnews24 reported in June 2026 that Bangladesh's FY2026-27 budget (starting July 1) introduces coordinated tax reforms for electric mobility. Electric bus and truck imports receive full tariff exemptions (except VAT) through June 30, 2030; imported passenger EV tax burdens fall from roughly 93% to 64% (up to $25,000) or 80% (up to $50,000); and customs duties and taxes on EV chargers and charging stations drop from 39.75% to 0%. Meanwhile, combined taxes on 1,200cc–1,600cc fossil-fuel vehicles rise from 132.36% to 155.88%, creating a structural "penalize oil, promote electric" spread. Government targets call for 25% electric buses and 30% electric trucks by 2035.

### 5. California AB 1777 Takes Effect Today — Police Can Issue "AV Noncompliance" Notices to Driverless Car Manufacturers (Autonomous Driving / US)
**Summary:** BBC, Car and Driver, and FOX 11 reported in May–July 2026 that implementing rules for California Assembly Bill 1777 take effect July 1. Officers can now issue Notices of Autonomous Vehicle Noncompliance to manufacturers when driverless vehicles commit traffic violations — regardless of whether a person is in the vehicle, as long as AV technology is engaged, liability rests with the manufacturer. Manufacturers must report notices to the DMV within 72 hours; provide 30-second emergency response lines; and deploy digital geofencing so vehicles exit emergency zones within two minutes of command. Prior incidents in San Francisco and San Bruno saw Waymo vehicles make illegal U-turns or stall during blackouts with no driver to cite.
