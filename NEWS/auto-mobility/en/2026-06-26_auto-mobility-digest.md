## I. Corporate Restructuring & Policy

### 1. Volkswagen weighs up to 100,000 job cuts and four German plant closures in historic overhaul (Automakers / Europe)
**Summary:** Manager Magazin, Reuters, and Electrek reported on June 26, 2026, that Volkswagen Group CEO Oliver Blume has proposed a radical restructuring: global headcount reductions could double from an existing 50,000 target to as many as 100,000, with production eventually ending at plants in Hanover, Zwickau, and Emden plus Audi's Neckarsulm site. Five-year investment plans would be cut roughly 15% to just over €13 billion, and the core VW passenger-car brand and parts operations could be spun into separate entities. Zwickau is the group's flagship EV plant; Emden builds the ID.4 and ID.7. The supervisory board is expected to discuss the plan on July 9; unions and IG Metall strongly oppose it, citing a late-2024 deal guaranteeing no German plant closures this decade. VW shares fell 3.4% to a 16-year low; the company declined to comment on confidential documents.

### 2. Polestar barred from selling new U.S. vehicles from MY2027; Commerce denies Connected Vehicle Rule waiver (Policy / U.S.)
**Summary:** electrive.com and Drive.com.au reported on June 26, 2026, that the U.S. Department of Commerce's Bureau of Industry and Security denied Polestar authorization to sell new vehicles in the United States from model year 2027 onward under the Connected Vehicle Rule, which bars connected-vehicle hardware and software with a sufficient nexus to China or Russia. Though Polestar 3 is assembled in South Carolina and Polestar 4 in South Korea, upstream Chinese ownership and software ties triggered the denial; sister brand Volvo received an exemption. Polestar will clear existing Polestar 3/4 inventory and maintain service; 94% of its Q1 2026 retail volume came from outside America, and the company plans to intensify its European focus.

### 3. NHTSA proposes dropping manual brake-pedal mandate for purpose-built autonomous vehicles (Autonomous Driving / U.S.)
**Summary:** Insurance Journal and TechCrunch reported on June 25–26, 2026, that the U.S. Department of Transportation's National Highway Traffic Safety Administration proposed revisions to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard No. 135 that would remove the manual brake-pedal requirement for vehicles designed exclusively for automated driving and never operated by a human. ADS-equipped vehicles retaining steering wheels or pedals must still meet all existing standards; all applicable vehicles must pass equivalent stopping-distance tests. The proposal is now open for a 30-day public comment period, primarily benefiting players like Tesla's Cybercab and Amazon's Zoox that lack traditional driver controls. NHTSA also withdrew a Biden-era voluntary ADS evaluation framework and is separately developing real-world ADS safety-performance requirements.

**Links:**

- [Insurance Journal — NHTSA Proposes to Drop Brake Pedal Requirements for Self-Driving Vehicles](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/national/2026/06/26/875366.htm)
- [TechCrunch — Trump administration proposes axing brake-pedal requirement for AVs in a boost for Tesla](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/trump-admin-proposes-axing-brake-pedal-requirement-for-avs-in-a-boost-for-tesla/)

**Commentary:** Regulation is shifting from a human-driver assumption to machine-only design — dropping the brake pedal is a necessary step toward robotaxi production, but real-world safety standards remain the harder gate.

## III. Safety & Recalls

### 7. Toyota, Lexus, Subaru recall ~21,000 MY2026 e-TNGA EVs over battery ECU software fault (Safety / Global)
**Summary:** USA Today and Electrek reported on June 25, 2026, that NHTSA campaign 26V393000 covers approximately 20,991 model-year 2026 vehicles: 11,495 Toyota bZ, 4,739 Lexus RZ, and 4,757 Subaru Solterra models sharing the e-TNGA platform. A battery electronic control unit (ECU) software memory fault can unexpectedly shut down the electric drive system while driving, increasing crash risk; Toyota says 100% of affected vehicles carry the defect. Dealers will update ECU software free of charge; owner notification letters are expected by August 3, 2026.
