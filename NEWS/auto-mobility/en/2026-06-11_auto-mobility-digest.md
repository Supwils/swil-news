## I. Autonomous Driving & Driver Assistance

### 1. Belgium Approves Tesla FSD as Fifth EU State; Bloc-Wide Vote Likely Slips to Early 2027 (Autonomous Driving / Europe)
**Summary:** Automotive World reported on June 11, 2026, that Belgium became the fifth EU member state to approve Tesla's Full Self-Driving (FSD) Supervised software. Flemish Mobility Minister Annick De Ridder announced the clearance on X after in-country testing; approval by any of Belgium's three regions is valid nationwide. The Netherlands (April), Lithuania, Estonia, and Denmark had previously recognized Dutch RDW type approval, creating a country-by-country mutual-recognition chain — currently limited to Hardware 4 vehicles (sold from 2023) running the European FSD v14 branch. Meanwhile, the EU Technical Committee on Motor Vehicles (TCMV) June 30 agenda lists only a "continuation of discussions" on the Netherlands' Art. 39 authorization request, with no vote scheduled. Bloc-wide clearance requires 55% of member states and 65% of EU population; Germany, France, and Italy have not moved, and industry trackers now expect the earliest vote in autumn 2026, with full EU recognition potentially slipping into early 2027.

**Commentary:** Small-market approvals and major-market holdouts are moving in parallel — Tesla is executing a salami-slice EU rollout, but without Germany, France, or Italy, FSD's commercial impact remains modest.

## III. Batteries, Charging & Energy

### 3. GM Activates V2G for 250,000 Existing EVs; Bets on Sodium-Ion Grid Storage (V2G / US)
**Summary:** TechTimes reported on June 11, 2026, that GM Chief Product Officer Sterling Anderson announced at the GM Empower 2026 event in San Francisco that more than 250,000 bidirectional-capable EVs already on U.S. roads have received over-the-air V2G activation — no new hardware required — spanning models from the Chevrolet Equinox EV to the Cadillac Escalade IQ. Pilots are underway with PG&E and DTE Energy, with an open invitation to utilities nationwide. GM also disclosed a sodium-ion grid-storage R&D partnership with Peak Energy (prototype cells by end-2026, trial production targeted for 2028), LFP production via Ultium Cells with LG Energy Solution expected in July 2026, second-life battery deployments already serving a Crusoe AI data center in Nevada (12 MW/63 MWh), and a unified public charging interface called Energy Pass. GM projects 130,000 of its EVs in PG&E territory by 2030, with more than 52,000 providing peak grid-balancing services.
