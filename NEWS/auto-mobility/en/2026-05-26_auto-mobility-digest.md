## 1. EVs & Product Launches

### 1. Ferrari unveils Luce, its first all-electric production car, in Rome: four-door five-seater, 1,050 cv, €550,000 (new model)
**Summary:** On **May 26, 2026** in Rome, Ferrari officially unveiled **Luce**, its first all-electric production car, billed as a 2027 model-year vehicle. The four-door, five-seat sedan packs a four-motor system delivering roughly **1,050 cv**, a **122 kWh** battery, **530+ km** WLTP range, **~2.5 s** 0–100 km/h, and a top speed above **310 km/h**. Both interior and exterior were co-designed with **LoveFrom**, the team led by Jony Ive and Marc Newson. Price is **€550,000 (~$640,000)**, with deliveries starting in Q4 2026. CEO Benedetto Vigna called it "the result of five years of work"; Luce is only the brand's second-ever four-door model and its first five-seater.

## 2. Autonomous Driving & Robotaxis

### 4. Waymo's flood-related service pause expands to multiple cities after May 21 Atlanta incident reveals failed software patch (autonomy)
**Summary:** Per TechCrunch and Bloomberg, a Waymo robotaxi drove into a flooded street in Atlanta on **May 21**, getting stuck for **about an hour**; the incident came less than two weeks after Waymo had pushed a "puddle/standing-water" software patch to its full **3,791-vehicle** fleet. Waymo subsequently suspended service in Atlanta, Dallas, Houston and San Antonio, with various outlets reporting additional pauses extending to Austin and Nashville, affecting at least 4–6 cities, largely in Texas. NHTSA said it "is aware and in communication with Waymo, and will take appropriate action if necessary." With Texas weather still unstable around May 26, no firm restart date has been set.
**Links:**

- [TechCrunch — Waymo expands pause to four cities as robotaxis keep driving into floods](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/21/waymo-pauses-service-in-four-cities-as-robotaxis-keep-driving-into-floods/)
- [Bloomberg — Waymo Halts Atlanta Robotaxi Service After Vehicle Stuck in Flooded Road](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-21/waymo-suspends-service-in-atlanta-as-robotaxis-stumped-by-floods)

**Commentary:** "Patched and still failed" is the script ADS regulators least want to see — it escalates a one-off incident into a systemic question of whether iteration speed can match ODD expansion; if NHTSA starts requiring pre-OTA filings and graceful-degradation plans, the entire robotaxi expansion curve will be reset.

## 4. Europe Policy & Industry

### 8. European automakers lobby Brussels en masse for a "calibration" — not a repeal — of the 2035 fleet CO2 target (policy)
**Summary:** Per Bloomberg, ACEA and Transport & Environment, **May 2026** saw the **European Parliament** rapporteur's report on the revision of light-duty CO2 rules published — viewed by industry as a step toward "a more pragmatic, real-world framework." That same month, executives from Volkswagen, BMW and Mercedes met EU officials in Brussels, citing slowing BEV growth, subsidy roll-back and charging-infrastructure gaps to push for a "calibration" of the 2035 target. The Commission's current proposal preserves a hard requirement of **90% tailpipe-emissions reduction for new fleets from 2035**, with the remaining 10% offsettable via EU-made low-carbon steel, e-fuels or biofuels.
**Links:**

- [Bloomberg — European Carmakers Renew Push for Softer EU CO2 Rules in Brussels Talks](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/european-automakers-press-brussels-for-further-co2-reprieve)
- [ACEA — European automakers welcome step towards more pragmatic CO2 framework](https://www.acea.auto/press-release/european-automakers-welcome-step-towards-more-pragmatic-co2-framework/)

**Commentary:** This "calibration" fight is really about European OEMs refusing to pay twice for the post-IRA China–US squeeze: any material softening of the 2035 framework would benefit German luxury brands still earning fat ICE margins, and disadvantage local EV-only suppliers and startups.
