## I. Electric vehicles & exports (North America / Southeast Asia)

### 1. Lotus ships first batch of 18 Eletre SUVs to Canada under revised Canada–China EV trade rules (policy / exports)
**Summary:** CleanTechnica reports that Lotus has delivered the first 18 units of the Chinese-built Eletre to Canada, described as the first confirmed export delivery under Ottawa’s revised framework that replaced a punitive 100% surtax on Chinese-made EVs with a roughly 6.1% tariff within a quota system cited as covering up to about 49,000 vehicles annually. The story notes a Canadian launch price from about CAD $119,900 and an 800 V electrical architecture, and discusses how Geely may use global marquee brands as a channel into politically sensitive Western markets.
**Links:**
- [CleanTechnica — Lotus sent its first batch of 18 EVs to Canada](https://cleantechnica.com/2026/05/10/lotus-sent-its-first-batch-of-18-evs-to-canada-is-it-paving-the-way-for-geely/)
