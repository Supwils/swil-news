## I. Policy & Markets

### 1. EU Prepares Countervailing Duties on Chinese PHEVs, Closing BEV Tariff Loophole (Policy / Europe)
**Summary:** electrive.com and Reuters (citing Handelsblatt) reported on June 19, 2026, that the European Commission is preparing countervailing duties on plug-in hybrid vehicles imported from China to close a regulatory gap left when additional tariffs on battery-electric vehicles took effect in October 2024. Handelsblatt cited three sources saying an anti-subsidy investigation is ready to proceed once a majority of EU member states approve; targeted manufacturers include BYD, Chery, and SAIC (MG). BYD became Germany's best-selling PHEV brand for the first time in May 2026 with 4,290 registrations — a strategy Chinese brands have used to avoid BEV duties of 17%–35.3%. The Commission denied hybrid tariffs as recently as January; rates are expected to be manufacturer-specific and, on average, lower than BEV tariffs because batteries account for a smaller share of PHEV value.

### 2. China's BEV Sales Surpass ICE for First Time; NEV Penetration Above 60% for Two Months (Market / China)
**Summary:** NetEase and Guancha reported on June 20, 2026, that China's auto market has reached a milestone: industry data show pure-electric vehicle sales have exceeded traditional gasoline and diesel models for the first time in 2026, marking a shift from "growth phase" to "dominant phase" for electrification. CPCA data put new-energy retail penetration at a record 66.7% in the first week of June (June 1–7); May NEV passenger-car retail penetration hit 62.9%, above 60% for a second consecutive month. No ICE models ranked in China's top 10 best-sellers in May; only four ICE models remained in the top 20. The Middle East energy crisis has been cited as an external factor boosting NEV demand, though overall retail volumes remain under pressure amid subsidy phase-down.

### 3. China's "Reverse Market": ICE Deep Discounts vs. NEV Price Tightening (Market / China)

Observers note this is the first collective "price repair" across 15+ brands since 2024 — the price-war margin is fading as profits and chip costs begin flowing downstream.

### 6. Mars Auto and LX Pantos Expand US Autonomous Freight Route to 7,000 km Round-Trip (Autonomous Driving / Logistics)
**Summary:** Seoul Economic Daily reported on June 20, 2026, that South Korean autonomous-trucking startup Mars Auto and global logistics firm LX Pantos expanded their US transcontinental autonomous freight route from roughly 3,500 km one-way to more than 7,000 km round-trip — breaking the record for the world's longest fixed autonomous transport route. By securing eastbound return cargo, the partners cut the empty-mile (deadhead) rate from an industry average of 16.7% to about 5%. Mars Auto operates on its camera-based Vision E2E "Mars Pilot" system (no lidar, no HD maps) and has accumulated more than 20 million km of driving data. CEO Park Il-soo said the round-trip route validates safer, more economical autonomous expansion within the North American logistics ecosystem.
