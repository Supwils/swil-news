## II. Autonomous Driving & ADAS

### 4. Saudi Arabia unveils comprehensive AV legal framework — liability rules for driverless operation (Policy / Middle East)
**Summary:** Gulf News reported June 13, 2026 that Interior Minister Prince Abdulaziz bin Saud bin Naif approved traffic-regulation amendments published in Umm Al Qura, creating one of the Gulf's most systematic legal frameworks for autonomous vehicles. Fully autonomous vehicles place compliance liability on the registered owner; systems requiring human oversight keep liability with the driver. New admin rules require authority approval before deregistering an AV, and AVs must follow existing traffic obligations including signs, signals, right-of-way, and emergency-vehicle procedures, with standard penalties applying. The General Directorate of Traffic said the framework covers varying automation levels.

**Links:**

- [Gulf News — Saudi Arabia introduces legal framework for self-driving vehicles](https://gulfnews.com/world/gulf/saudi/saudi-arabia-introduces-legal-framework-for-self-driving-vehicles-1.500573087)

**Commentary:** The Middle East is leading with liability clarity before long pilots — in the robotaxi race, legal certainty is infrastructure as important as sensor cost.

### 5. Baidu Apollo Go "AmiGo" wins Swiss L4 permit; open-road trials from June 1 in eastern Switzerland (Robotaxi / Europe)
**Summary:** Baidu announced June 12, 2026 that AmiGo — its on-demand AV service with PostBus — received a FEDRO special operating permit for Level 4 autonomy.
