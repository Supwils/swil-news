### 2. NHTSA launches rulemaking to drop manual brake-pedal mandate for purpose-built autonomous vehicles (Autonomous Driving / U.S.)
**Summary:** Bloomberg and NHTSA reported on June 25, 2026, that the U.S. Department of Transportation's National Highway Traffic Safety Administration commenced the fifth update to Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS), proposing revisions to FMVSS No. 135 that would remove the manual brake-pedal requirement for vehicles designed exclusively for automated driving and never operated by a human. ADS-equipped vehicles retaining steering wheels or pedals must still meet all existing standards; all applicable vehicles must pass equivalent stopping-distance tests. NHTSA calls the move a "commonsense" step in its Automated Vehicle Framework to clear hardware barriers for players like Waymo and Tesla, while retaining defect-investigation and recall authority and developing separate real-world ADS safety-performance requirements.

**Links:**

- [Bloomberg — US Moves to Drop Brake Pedal Rule for Driverless Vehicles](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-25/us-set-to-end-brake-pedal-requirement-for-driverless-vehicles)
- [NHTSA — Commonsense Updates to Brake Pedal Requirements for AVs](https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/fmvss-updates-brake-pedal-requirements)

**Commentary:** Regulation is shifting from a human-driver assumption to machine-only design — dropping the brake pedal is a necessary step toward robotaxi production, but real-world safety standards remain the harder gate.

### 3. UN adopts first global ADS technical regulation (ADS GTR), co-led by China, U.S., EU, Japan, and others (Autonomous Driving / Global)
**Summary:** UN News, The Straits Times, and China Daily reported around June 25, 2026, that the UN Economic Commission for Europe's World Forum for Harmonization of Vehicle Regulations (WP.29) adopted the world's first unified regulatory framework for Automated Driving Systems (ADS) in Geneva on June 24, comprising a UN Regulation under the 1958 Agreement and a Global Technical Regulation (ADS GTR) under the 1998 Agreement. Co-led by China, the EU, the United States, the UK, Canada, and Japan, the rules require ADS performance at or above a competent human driver, audited lifecycle safety management, documented safety cases, simulation and on-road validation, continuous performance monitoring, and data storage. China's MIIT said it will accelerate alignment between domestic mandatory standards and the ADS GTR core content.

**Links:**

- [UN News — New global rules clear the road for driverless vehicles](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/06/1167797)
- [China Daily — UN approved first global technical regulation on automated driving systems, co-led by China](https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202606/25/WS6a3cf7e7a310986e2b461ebc.html)

**Commentary:** A decade in the making, global ADS rules may ease fragmented national regimes — but with the U.S. outside 1958 Agreement mutual recognition, implementation will still run on dual tracks.
