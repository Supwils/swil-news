### 1. Audi unveils Nuvolari hybrid supercar: 1,001 PS, limited to 499 units (Product / Global)
**Summary:** NetEase, Auto Express, and others around June 5, 2026 reported that Audi officially revealed the Nuvolari — its first high-performance hybrid supercar — in Antibes, France. Limited to 499 units, it delivers roughly 1,001 PS (~986 bhp), a top speed above 350 km/h, and 0–100 km/h in about 2.6 seconds. The mid-engine layout pairs a twin-turbo 4.0L V8 with three electric motors, carbon bodywork, an Audi Space Frame, quattro predictive ride, and active aerodynamics. Pricing starts around £500,000, with deliveries planned for the first half of 2027. Audi positions it as an F1-tech halo car, not a direct R8 replacement.

**Summary:** Carnewschina on June 5, 2026 reported that Mercedes-Benz officially opened pre-orders in China for the all-new electric GLC (GLC L EV), starting at 349,000 yuan (~$51,300), with a July 2026 market launch. Mercedes China sales have fallen YoY for 12 straight months — the GLC EV is a key turnaround bet.

### 10. Scania demonstrates heavy-duty V2G via MCS: bidirectional power up to 750 kW / 1,000 A (Charging / Europe)
**Summary:** PR Newswire and Transport and Energy (May 26–June 1, 2026) reported Scania completed one of the world's first heavy-duty vehicle-to-grid demonstrations using the Megawatt Charging System (MCS), reaching up to 1,000 A and 750 kW bidirectional flow with real-time communication among truck, charger, and energy management systems — enabling peak shaving, frequency balancing, and onsite solar use.
