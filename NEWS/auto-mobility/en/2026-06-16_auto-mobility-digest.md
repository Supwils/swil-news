## I. Policy & Market

### 1. BNEF Electric Vehicle Outlook 2026: global passenger EV sales may exceed 23 million, but long-term forecasts cut for China and the US (Policy / Global)
**Summary:** BloombergNEF released its annual Electric Vehicle Outlook (EVO 2026) on June 16, 2026, projecting more than 23 million global passenger EV sales (BEV and PHEV) this year, up 11% year-on-year and representing roughly 27% of global new-car sales (versus 9% five years ago), with EVs reaching 52% of sales by 2035. China accounted for 63% of global EV sales in 2025 and is expected to remain above half through 2030. However, BNEF cut its outlook for the second consecutive year: tighter Chinese incentive eligibility and market maturation are slowing growth there, while the US — with federal fuel-economy targets rolled back and Inflation Reduction Act support scaled back — is projected to see a 19% sales drop in 2026, with EVs making up only 24% of the US fleet by 2040. The global EV fleet consumed 367 TWh in 2025, rising to over 2,700 TWh by 2040, requiring more than $800 billion in grid investment.

### 2. EU BEV share of new registrations rose to 20.6% in April; Middle East oil spike further lifts electrification intent (Market / Europe)
**Summary:** The European Commission's Directorate-General for Climate Action published an analysis on June 16, 2026, citing ACEA data showing fully electric vehicles (BEVs) accounted for 20.6% of new EU registrations in April 2026, up from 15.7% in April 2025 and above the 2025 full-year average of 17.4%; the BEV share has grown tenfold since 2019. The report attributes part of the recent acceleration to Middle East instability pushing oil prices higher, while emphasizing EU climate policy and global clean-transport momentum as long-term drivers. The IEA notes that at $100-per-barrel oil, EV drivers enjoy fuel savings 35% higher than a year ago; EU EV uptake has already cut oil demand by an estimated 140,000 barrels per day (4.5% of car-related oil use), saving roughly €4.5 billion annually in fossil-fuel imports.

## II. Autonomous Driving & ADAS

### 4. Baidu Apollo Go and Swiss PostBus win Eastern Switzerland Level 4 permit for AmiGo; regular operations targeted for 2027 (Robotaxi / Europe)
**Summary:** Electrek reported on June 16, 2026, that AmiGo — an on-demand autonomous mobility service jointly developed by Baidu's Apollo Go and Swiss Post's PostBus — has received a Federal Roads Office (FEDRO) special operating permit for Level 4 operations in Eastern Switzerland (Baidu announced June 12). Since June 1, Apollo Go RT6 all-electric robotaxis with safety operators have been testing across roughly 80 km² in the cantons of St. Gallen, Appenzell Ausserrhoden, and Appenzell Innerrhoden. The next phase is a closed-group user trial, followed by rides without safety operators once all safety evidence is submitted, with regular bookable service via the AmiGo app targeted from 2027 — potentially Europe's largest planned automated public-transport operation. Each RT6 carries up to three passengers with 30+ sensors; steering wheels are designed for removal in fully driverless mode.

### 12. Consumer Reports: median 50% price gap for identical Uber and Lyft trips requested at the same time (Mobility platform / North America)
**Summary:** NBC News and Consumer Reports released a months-long investigation on June 16, 2026, finding that volunteers requesting identical routes at the same time in cities including Austin and Kansas City often saw dramatically different Uber and Lyft quotes, with a median gap of about 50% between lowest and highest price groupings — in some cases within the same minute. The report raises questions about "surveillance pricing" based on user data; Uber denies personalized pricing, attributing differences to real-time supply, driver distribution, and minor GPS variance; Lyft also denies surveillance pricing. Connecticut and Maryland have banned certain forms of personalized pricing, and the US House Oversight Committee is investigating related practices.
