### 1. CCTV June 7 report: China pushes slimmer EVs as mandatory energy-consumption standard takes effect Jan 1 (Policy / China)
**Summary:** The Straits Times on June 7, 2026 cited a China Central Television report that Beijing wants leaner electric vehicles after years of bigger batteries, more cabin space, and feature stacking made passenger cars significantly heavier and wider. China's average passenger car weighed 1,704 kg in 2024 — roughly one-third more than in 2012. Many SUVs and MPVs now approach or exceed 2 m in width; one vehicle measured nearly 2.3 m against a standard parking-space width of 2.4 m. Long-range marketing (some claims up to 1,000 km, battery packs up to ~800 kg) and "mobile living space" configurations (including in-car toilets) were highlighted. A mandatory national energy-consumption standard effective January 1, 2026 bars production, sale, or registration of new EV models that fail to comply.

### 12. Federal IRS Section 30C home EV charger tax credit expires June 30, 2026 (Policy / U.S.)
**Summary:** IRS guidance and industry trackers confirm federal Clean Vehicle Credits (30D/25E — up to $7,500 new / $4,000 used) expired September 30, 2025. The remaining federal EV incentive is the Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit (30C): 30% of qualifying home Level 2 charger installation costs, up to $1,000, for equipment placed in service before July 1, 2026, in eligible low-income or non-urban census tracts. State programs — including Oregon (up to $7,500), California, and Illinois — may still stack on top.
