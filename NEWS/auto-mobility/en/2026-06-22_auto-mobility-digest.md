**Summary:** BitAuto and Gelonghui reported on June 22, 2026, that the latest CPCA survey shows top-tier automakers — accounting for roughly 70% of market volume — targeting about 10% month-on-month retail growth in June as they push for half-year targets. ### 2. BEV stands alone: range-extenders and PHEVs down 23%+ and 28%+ YoY in May; BEVs are 67% of NEV sales (Market / China)
**Summary:** Shuidi Auto App and NetEase reported on June 22, 2026, that May NEV penetration hit a record 62.9%, but the mix is splitting sharply: range-extended and plug-in hybrid sales fell more than 23% and 28% YoY respectively, while BEV sales rose 3.9% YoY and now account for 67% of NEV volume. In the large-SUV segment, BEVs have led range-extenders for nine straight months; NIO ES8 sold 11,472 units in May, its seventh consecutive month as China's top-selling large SUV above RMB 400,000, with cumulative deliveries exceeding 120,000 units in 275 days. The Leapmotor L80 sold 5,949 units in its first half-month, setting a record for a new large five-seat BEV; the five-seat ES8 pre-sale opens June 28.

**Links:**

- [NetEase — ICE collapse, range-extender plunge, BEV carries the load](https://www.163.com/dy/article/L022QE3H0527E3LS.html)

**Commentary:** "New energy" with a fuel tank is being abandoned by consumers — as range and charging anxiety ease, one-step BEV adoption is beating transitional powertrains.

## II. Autonomous Driving & Robotaxi

### 4. Xiaomi YU7 GT completes world's first claimed full autonomous Nürburgring lap in 10:29.483 (Autonomous Driving / China)
**Summary:** CnEVPost and CarNewsChina reported on June 22, 2026, that Xiaomi announced its high-performance BEV SUV YU7 GT completed what it calls the world's first production-car fully autonomous lap of the Nürburgring Nordschleife in 10 minutes 29.483 seconds with no human intervention. That is about 3 minutes 7 seconds slower than the 7:22.755 production-SUV lap record set by a professional driver in the same model in May. The YU7 GT launched May 21 from RMB 389,900, with a 897V SiC platform, 101.7 kWh ternary lithium pack, and peak 1,003 hp; YU7 series deliveries were 8,736 units in May, down for a fifth consecutive month.

### 13. Strait of Hormuz disruption continues to hit global auto supply chains and energy costs (Supply Chain / Global)
**Summary:** Automotive Manufacturing Solutions and Automotive Logistics reported in June 2026 that US–Israel conflict with Iran has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, with roughly 170 container ships (~450,000 TEU) trapped at one point. Japan imports ~90%, South Korea ~70%, and India nearly half of crude via the strait, exposing Toyota, Honda, Nissan, Hyundai, Kia, and export-heavy Chinese makers like BYD to energy and logistics shocks. European OEMs also fear Middle East premium demand erosion — VW Group CEO Oliver Blume warned the conflict could weaken regional sales; aluminum giant Alba has declared force majeure and cut output.
