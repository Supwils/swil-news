## I. Batteries & Volkswagen Platform Strategy

### 1. Samsung SDI reportedly wins Volkswagen Unified Cell supply deal; Hungary Göd plant becomes multi-OEM hub (Battery / Europe)
**Summary:** The Elec, TechTimes, and others on June 4–6, 2026 reported that Samsung SDI has secured a supply agreement — not yet officially confirmed by Volkswagen — to manufacture side-terminal prismatic Unified Cells at its Göd, Hungary plant. Two lines are being converted, with mass production expected in 2027 at an estimated double-digit GWh scale. If confirmed, Samsung would become the third Unified Cell supplier after PowerCo (Salzgitter) and Gotion (Hefei), extending its VW Group footprint from premium marques to volume brands including Volkswagen, Škoda, and SEAT/CUPRA. Samsung SDI has invested over €1.5 billion in Göd since 2016; it already supplies BMW 46-series cylindrical cells, Mercedes-Benz prismatic cells (April 2026 deal, industry estimates ~$6.8B), and Hyundai Motor Europe models. Samsung SDI's Q1 2026 earnings call targeted lifting Hungary utilization above 70% in H2 2026.

### 2. Volkswagen ID.Polo and CUPRA Raval enter production at Martorell — first mass-market Unified Cell vehicles (Product / Europe)
**Summary:** The Elec and TechTimes on June 3–6, 2026 reported that Volkswagen Group started series production of the CUPRA Raval and Volkswagen ID.Polo on June 3 at its Martorell plant in Spain — the first mass-market models carrying the Unified Cell. Early cells come from PowerCo's Salzgitter plant and Gotion's Hefei facility; Samsung SDI's Hungary lines are expected to join in 2027. The standardized 256×106×24.8 mm prismatic format is intended to underpin roughly 80% of group EVs by 2030, with chemistries spanning LFP, sodium-ion, and solid-state over time.

## II. Autonomous Driving & Robotaxi

### 3. Tesla Robotaxi covers entire Austin metro (~245 sq mi) — unsupervised fleet still ~20 vehicles (Robotaxi / U.S.)
**Summary:** Electrek, TechTimes, and Teslarati on June 3–5, 2026 reported Tesla confirmed on X that unsupervised Robotaxi service now covers the full Austin metropolitan area — roughly 245 square miles, including suburbs Pflugerville and Manor, the I-35 corridor, Gigafactory Texas, and Austin-Bergstrom International Airport. This is Tesla's fifth Austin geofence expansion and is framed internally as evidence FSD reliability can support broader commercial deployment.

### 4. Reuters investigation: Tesla FSD safety stats called "apples-to-oranges"; labelers say they wouldn't ride (Autonomous / U.S.)
**Summary:** Reuters on May 28, 2026 published an investigation drawing on nine former Tesla data labelers, one former self-driving engineer, and 11 traffic-safety researchers. The report alleges Tesla inflated FSD safety claims by comparing airbag-deployment crashes in its fleet against federal tow-away crash data — a broader, less severe category. University of Michigan researcher Marco Benedetti's apples-to-apples recalculation cut the claimed advantage from "10× safer" to roughly 3×, with further distortion from fleet age (Tesla avg. ~4.1 years vs. U.S. fleet ~12.8 years). Seven of nine labelers said they would not ride with FSD driving; 10 of 11 researchers viewed the statistics as marketing rather than rigorous safety analysis. The report also described extensive pre-mapping of Robotaxi zones, tension with Musk's "no laborious local mapping" narrative.

**Links:**

- [Electrek — Tesla FSD safety stats misleading, Reuters investigation](https://electrek.co/2026/05/28/tesla-fsd-safety-stats-misleading-reuters-investigation/)
- [Yahoo Autos — Reuters says flawed math boosted Tesla FSD safety claims](https://autos.yahoo.com/policy-and-environment/articles/reuters-says-flawed-math-boosted-002500234.html)

**Commentary:** As Austin's geofence widened, FSD's safety narrative faced a methodology reckoning — scaling Robotaxi requires proving safety and fleet density in parallel, not expanding the map alone.

### 10. Wallbox deploys first Supernova PowerRing public DC fast chargers in Sitges, Spain (Charging / Europe)
**Summary:** Electrek on June 5, 2026 reported Wallbox installed its first public Supernova PowerRing chargers at Port de Sitges near Barcelona — the architecture's first live deployment since late 2025. PowerRing uses intelligent power sharing: three stalls currently share up to 240 kW (80–240 kW per vehicle); the design can scale to 720 kW shared capacity and up to 400 kW to a single capable EV, helping operators maximize throughput where grid connections are constrained. Co-founder Eduard Castañeda called it the start of PowerRing's broader European rollout.
