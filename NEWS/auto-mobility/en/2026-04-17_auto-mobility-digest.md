### 1. Geely Recalls 1,473 Polestar 4 Vehicles in China Over 86kWh Battery Thermal Runaway Risk
**Summary:** Geely Automobile filed a recall with China's SAMR covering 1,473 Polestar 4 vehicles equipped with 86kWh battery packs, citing thermal runaway risk under specific charging conditions. The Polestar 4 is manufactured at Geely's Hangzhou facility and had been commercially available in China since early 2025.

The software update path (versus physical replacement) will be closely watched.

## III. Policy & Regulation

### 7. EU PHEV Utility Factor Tightening; "Made in EU" ~70% EV Component Content Requirement
**Summary:** The EU Commission advanced two parallel EV policy measures: tightening the "utility factor" for plug-in hybrid vehicles in emissions calculations (reducing the real-world emissions benefit attributed to PHEV mode) and a proposed "Made in EU" content requirement for EVs sold in Europe, targeting approximately 70% local value content for battery and powertrain components to qualify for regulatory preferences. The latter was part of the EU Industrial Accelerator Act framework.
**Links:**
- [European Commission — PHEV utility factor](https://ec.europa.eu/)
- [Reuters — Made in EU EV content requirements](https://www.reuters.com/)
**Commentary:** The PHEV utility factor tightening is long overdue — independent testing has consistently shown PHEVs achieving far less electric mode driving than regulatory credit calculations assumed, inflating their official CO₂ performance. The 70% Made in EU content proposal is a direct industrial policy instrument that will test whether EU battery manufacturing can reach competitive cost parity fast enough.
