## I. Regulation and Policy

### 1. SEC Staff Statement on "Covered User Interfaces": Broker-Dealer Registration Not Required Under Strict Conditions (Impacts Wallets and DeFi Frontends)
**Summary:** The SEC's Division of Trading and Markets released a staff statement on April 13 clarifying that for websites, browser extensions, or apps ("Covered User Interfaces") that help users prepare and submit "crypto asset securities" transactions through self-custodied wallets under Section 15(a) of the Securities Exchange Act — staff would not object to such providers foregoing broker-dealer registration if they met specific conditions: no custody, no solicitation of specific trades, user-configurable default parameters, neutral objective-standard liquidity connection, fixed fees, and full disclosure. The statement is staff-level, not a Commission rule, and includes a sunset provision treating it as withdrawn five years from April 13, 2026 if no further Commission action. Commissioner Peirce endorsed the direction but called for more durable rulemaking to clarify the "broker-dealer" definition.
**Links:**
- [SEC — Staff Statement Regarding Broker-Dealer Registration of Certain User Interfaces](https://www.sec.gov/newsroom/speeches-statements/staff-statement-regarding-broker-dealer-registration-certain-user-interfaces-utilized-prepare-staff-statement-regarding-broker-dealer-registration-certain-user-interfaces-utilized)
- [Pillsbury — SEC Staff Statement on Crypto Trading Interfaces: Key Takeaways](https://www.pillsburylaw.com/en/news-and-insights/sec-staff-statement-crypto-trading-interfaces.html)
**Commentary:** This provides a workable "interim map" of compliance boundaries for US non-custodial frontends and aggregation routers, but the conditions are strict and the five-year sunset limits certainty. Product fee and routing strategy design will directly determine whether a given product falls within the safe harbor.
