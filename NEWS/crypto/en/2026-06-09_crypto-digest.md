## I. Regulation & Policy

### 1. FinCEN and OFAC Close Public Comment Period on GENIUS Act Stablecoin AML/Sanctions Rules
**Summary:** The U.S. Treasury's Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) and the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) jointly proposed rules on April 8, 2026, to implement anti-money laundering, counter-terrorist financing, and sanctions compliance requirements for Permitted Payment Stablecoin Issuers (PPSIs) under the Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins Act (GENIUS Act). Public comments were due **June 9, 2026**, via the Federal eRulemaking Portal (Docket FINCEN-2026-0100). The proposal classifies PPSIs as financial institutions under the Bank Secrecy Act, mandates effective sanctions compliance programs for the first time for this category, and grants technical capabilities to freeze and block impermissible transactions. A final rule is expected by July 18, 2026, with a 12-month implementation window after publication. Law firms including Arnold & Porter and Mayer Brown note that industry may comment on nearly 60 specific questions, including secondary-market obligations and foreign issuer scope.

### 2. Russia's Deputy Finance Minister Chebeskov Signals Fees on USDT, BNB as "Unfriendly" Assets

The full framework is set to take effect **July 1, 2026**, permitting Bitcoin and stablecoins for cross-border trade while banning domestic crypto payments.
