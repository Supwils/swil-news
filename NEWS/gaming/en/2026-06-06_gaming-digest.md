## IV. Regulation & Streaming

### 11. California's Protect Our Games Act (AB 1921) clears the Assembly, heads to the Senate

**Summary:** The California Assembly passed AB 1921—branded the Protect Our Games Act—43–16, requiring digital game operators to notify players 60 days before ceasing services needed for a game's "ordinary use," and to provide offline patches, alternate versions, or full refunds upon shutdown. It applies to paid digital games first sold or rereleased on or after January 1, 2027, excluding free and subscription-only titles. Stop Killing Games advised on drafting; the bill now moves through the Senate pending gubernatorial signature.

**Links:**

- [Engadget — California game-access bill passes Assembly](https://www.engadget.com/2185206/a-california-bill-that-preserves-access-to-video-games-achieves-its-first-victory/)
- [California Legislature — AB 1921 full text](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB1921)

**Commentary:** In the wake of Ubisoft's *The Crew* shutdown controversy, California is drafting the first state-level template tying delisting to refunds or offline preservation.
