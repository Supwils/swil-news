### 1. Xbox Reevaluates Project Helix: Exploring "Radically Different" Console Business Models Amid RAM Crisis

**Summary:** On June 10, The Verge and others reported that Xbox CEO Asha Sharma and chief strategy officer Matthew Ball said publicly during Summer Game Fest week that Microsoft is reevaluating next-gen console Project Helix and exploring "radically different" business models in response to the global memory and storage shortage known as "RAMageddon." Sharma said mass audiences can no longer afford "thousands of dollars" per console generation and predicted unprecedented models will emerge later this year. Ball stressed Microsoft remains committed to shipping Helix but must rethink affordability and flexibility. Speculation ranges from ad-subsidized hardware and OEM-branded Xbox devices to subscription-heavy, services-first structures. Separate leaks describe a PC-console hybrid with 32GB RAM in Game Mode and 24GB in Windows Mode, with online features potentially tied to subscription by mode.
