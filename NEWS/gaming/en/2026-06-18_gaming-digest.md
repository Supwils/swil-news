## I. Major Studios & Industry Shakeup

### 1. Xbox "Reset" Lands: Ninja Theory Confirmed for Closure, ~130 Jobs at Risk

**Summary:** On June 18, multiple outlets reported that Microsoft-owned Xbox is shutting down Cambridge studio Ninja Theory (developer of the *Hellblade* series). According to the *Cambridge Independent*, roughly 130 staff were informed on Monday, June 16; the studio is actively seeking an independent buyer or a staff buyout (MBO) to avoid a full shutdown. The move follows last week's "reset" memo from new CEO Asha Sharma and Chief Content Officer Matt Booty — Xbox acknowledged spending roughly $20 billion on studio acquisitions over five years (excluding the $69 billion Activision Blizzard deal) while "over-extending" and "not adequately funding" its teams, with annual revenue down nearly $500 million. Ninja Theory had just announced *Senua*, the next *Hellblade* entry (targeting 2027), at the Xbox Games Showcase on June 7; observers questioned whether Microsoft pushed the reveal knowing closure was imminent to make a sale more attractive. Microsoft has not officially commented.

### 2. Double Fine and Compulsion Games Also Face Closure or Spinoff Talks as Xbox Refocuses on Flagship IP

**Summary:** On June 18, Bloomberg, Kotaku, and others reported that beyond Ninja Theory, Psychonauts maker Double Fine and *South of Midnight* studio Compulsion Games are also facing shutdown or divestiture from Microsoft. Tim Schafer's team at Double Fine is negotiating to buy the studio back; Compulsion leadership is fighting to keep the team intact. Even if buybacks succeed, sources say significant layoffs would still follow. More Xbox Game Studios teams are reportedly in similar negotiations, with employees cleared to seek outside work. Industry analysis points to Xbox concentrating resources on flagship franchises like *Halo* and *The Elder Scrolls* — underperformance of *Senua's Saga: Hellblade II* and *South of Midnight* is cited as context. Rumors also persist that Microsoft is evaluating spinning Xbox into a subsidiary or joint venture. Xbox Game Studios head Craig Duncan departed on June 15; studios now report to Matt Booty, with larger layoffs expected after the fiscal year ends June 30.

### 7. Philippines PAGCOR Launches "PAGCOR Guarantee" Verification Portal, Mandates National Gambling Helpline Ads
