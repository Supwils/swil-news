### 3. California Assembly passes Protect Our Games Act, forcing offline patches or refunds
**Summary:** TechRaptor and legislative records show California's Assembly passed AB 1921 on May 29 by a 43–16 vote, sending the Protect Our Games Act to the State Senate. For server-connected games sold in California on or after January 1, 2027, publishers must give at least 60 days' notice before shutting necessary online services and then provide an offline-capable version, an independent patch, or full refunds. Free-to-play titles, subscriptions, and games with permanent offline downloads at purchase are exempt. The bill stems from the global Stop Killing Games movement.

**Links:**

- [TechRaptor — California State Assembly Passes 'Protect Our Games Act'](https://techraptor.net/gaming/news/california-state-assembly-passes-protect-our-games-act)

### 4. India's Supreme Court upholds 28% GST on full bet value, capping real-money gaming sector
**Summary:** Outlook Respawn and The Economic Times report that on May 27 India's Supreme Court ruled stake-based online activities — including fantasy sports — are betting subject to 28% GST on the full face value of deposits, with retrospective exposure estimated near INR 2.5 lakh crore. PROGA already banned online money games effective May 1, 2026; platforms including Dream11 and MPL suspended operations and cut thousands of jobs. The tax ruling leaves former operators facing historic liabilities on revenue they can no longer earn.
