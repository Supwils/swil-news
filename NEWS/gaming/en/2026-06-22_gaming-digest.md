### 1. Ubisoft Co-Founder Claude Guillemot Dies in Plane Crash at 69

**Summary:** On the afternoon of Friday, June 19, Ubisoft co-founder and Guillemot Corp. chairman Claude Guillemot died in a twin-engine Cessna 421 crash near the French coastal resort of La Baule; he and a flight instructor from Rennes were both killed. Claude was 69. French authorities have opened an investigation into involuntary manslaughter; the aircraft went down shortly before landing at La Baule-Escoublac Airport. Claude and his four brothers founded Ubisoft in 1986, overseeing operations and Asian markets while building the Thrustmaster PC peripherals brand. Ubisoft issued brief condolences on June 21–22 and said it would not comment further.

## II. Regulation & Industry Shifts

### 5. India Gameskraft GST Ruling Aftermath: Promoter Personal Liability Still Unresolved

**Summary:** On June 22, Pitch and others reported that while India's Supreme Court Gameskraft GST landmark ruling directs authorities to reassess tax under Rule 31B — linking taxable value to player deposits rather than individual bets, and excluding recycled winnings — it did not strike down show-cause notices targeting founders, directors, and senior executives. The industry previously faced potential liability of roughly ₹2.5 lakh crore; the court gave companies eight weeks to respond before adjudication proceeds on whether personal liability applies. Valuation methods gained partial clarity, but promoter liability remains the sector's biggest uncertainty.
