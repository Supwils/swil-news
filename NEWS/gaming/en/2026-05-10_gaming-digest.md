### 1. Atari Acquires Full Rights to the First Five *Wizardry* Games; Drecom Pushes Back on “Whole-Trademark Sale” Narrative
**Summary:** Anime News Network, citing statements from Atari and Drecom, reports that Atari announced it acquired the complete exclusive rights and underlying foundational-universe intellectual property for the first five *Wizardry* titles (1981–1988), outlining long-term plans for remasters, collections, and new releases. **Summary:** Law.asia summarizes that the *Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Act, 2025* and the operational *Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Rules, 2026* took effect on May 1, 2026, distinguishing prohibited online money gaming from permissible social games and esports, standing up the Online Gaming Authority of India (OGAI), introducing registration, mandatory safety features, penalties, and appeals, and linking coordination with financial institutions and law enforcement. The article points to official PDF releases and press materials on India’s Ministry of Electronics and IT channels.

### 3. Philippines PAGCOR Caps Online Rebate/Cashback Programs; May 7 Memorandum Requires Prior Approval and No Stacked Promotions
**Summary:** Inside Asian Gaming reports that the Philippine regulator’s Electronic Gaming Licensing Department, in a memorandum dated May 7, 2026, caps cash rebate and cashback schemes for slots, electronic bingo, numeric games, and sports betting at either 1.5% of player turnover or deposits, or alternatively up to 15% of net losses, depending on the program design. Categories outside the memo require case-by-case RTP review; unapproved incentive programs risk regulatory action; rebates cannot be booked as deductible gaming losses against GGR, and operators may not combine cash promos to exceed permitted totals.
**Links:**
- [Inside Asian Gaming — PAGCOR caps player rebate and cashback](https://asgam.com/2026/05/10/pagcor-imposes-new-caps-on-player-rebate-and-cashback-programs-to-level-online-gaming-playing-field/)
