## II. Regulation, Regional Frameworks, and China Market Headlines

### 4. PEGI expands interactive risk criteria; new submissions rated under the rules from June 2026 (European ratings)
**Summary:** In a March 12, 2026 press release, PEGI states that starting in June 2026 newly submitted games will be classified with additional criteria covering time- or quantity-limited offers, paid random items (default PEGI 16, sometimes 18), play-by-appointment mechanics, and unrestricted online communication. The organization positions the update as strengthening online-safety guidance for families, developed with reference to Germany’s USK experience.
**Links:**
- [PEGI — PEGI expands age rating criteria with interactive risk categories](https://pegi.info/news/pegi-expands-age-rating-criteria-interactive-risk-categories)
**Commentary:** Once loot boxes, passes, and chat move from footnotes to rating drivers, storefront copy and parental tooling must be re-localized for a more compliance-heavy era.
