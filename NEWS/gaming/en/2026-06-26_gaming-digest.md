### 7. Korea's Loot-Box Rules Push Roblox to Disclose Item Odds Worldwide

**Summary:** On June 26, TechTimes reported Roblox revised its paid random-item policy to comply with South Korea's Game Industry Promotion Act — requiring disclosure of exact percentage odds for loot boxes including indirect purchases — and rolled the changes out globally. Players worldwide now see precise drop rates before purchase, not just in Korea. Korea's Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism and Game Rating and Administration Committee previously investigated Roblox and helped shape the policy; reports say ~60% of regulatory corrective actions target overseas operators. Korean lawmakers are pushing to raise fine caps from 20 million won toward 3% of sales.
