## 1. Big-Publisher M&A and Litigation

### 1. Microsoft Settles With Former Activision Blizzard Shareholders Over $68.8B Buyout for $250M (Litigation)
**Summary:** Financial and legal outlets reported on and around May 26 that Microsoft has agreed to pay roughly **$250 million** to resolve a nearly four-year class action brought by former Activision Blizzard shareholders. Led by Swedish pension fund **Sjunde AP-Fonden**, the plaintiffs alleged that the board, headed by then-CEO **Bobby Kotick**, breached its fiduciary duties by rushing into Microsoft's $68.7 billion takeover at $95 per share — a deal that also locked in roughly $400 million in change-of-control benefits for Kotick. The settlement works out to about **$0.30 per share**; Microsoft is funding 40% directly, with the rest covered by directors-and-officers (D&O) insurance. The agreement still requires final approval from a Delaware judge.
**Links:**

- [Claims Journal — Activision Shareholders Reach $250M Settlement Over Microsoft Buyout](https://www.claimsjournal.com/news/national/2026/05/26/337788.htm)
- [Law.com — Microsoft Agrees to $250M Settlement With Activision Blizzard Shareholders](https://www.law.com/delbizcourt/2026/05/22/microsoft-agrees-to-250m-settlement-with-activision-blizzard-shareholders/)

**Commentary:** The largest tail risk on a $68.8B transaction is being settled with insurance carrying the bulk — a sign that "shareholder-suit cost" on mega gaming M&A is being actuarialized, which in turn will embolden boards to accept richer take-out premiums once antitrust clears.

## 4. Labor and Organization

### 7. Wizards of the Coast Declines to Recognize Magic: The Gathering Arena Union; NLRB Vote in June (Labor)
**Summary:** More than **100** developers on **Magic: The Gathering Arena** at Renton, WA-based Wizards of the Coast previously organized under the **Communications Workers of America (CWA)**, seeking collective bargaining over remote work, layoff protections, generative-AI usage, and overtime. They set a **May 1** deadline for voluntary recognition. **Hasbro** and Wizards declined to recognize the union by that date, instead sending employees letters urging them to reconsider; the next step is a formal **NLRB** vote in **June**.
**Links:**
