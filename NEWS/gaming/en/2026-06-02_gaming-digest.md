## III. Regulation, Streaming & Entertainment M&A

### 8. California Protect Our Games Act clears Assembly, heads to Senate

**Summary:** California's Assembly passed AB 1921—the Protect Our Games Act—43–16 in late May; VGC reported June 1 that it advanced to the State Senate. The bill would require operators to give at least 60 days' notice before shutting servers on paid online-dependent games and to provide offline alternatives, patches, or full refunds. If enacted as written, it applies to games released on or after January 1, 2027, excluding subscriptions and free-to-play titles. The push aligns with Europe's Stop Killing Games campaign after controversies such as Ubisoft's *The Crew* shutdown.

**Links:**

- [VGC — US bill requiring paid games to be playable after shutdown passes initial vote](https://www.videogameschronicle.com/news/us-bill-requiring-paid-games-to-be-playable-after-they-shut-down-has-passed-initial-vote-and-moved-to-the-senate/)
