### 2. Fox to Acquire Roku for $22 Billion: Merging Tubi and The Roku Channel into Free-Streaming Powerhouse

**Summary:** On June 15, Fox Corporation and Roku announced a definitive agreement for Fox to acquire Roku at $160 per share (cash plus Fox Class A common stock), valuing the company at approximately $22 billion in enterprise value. The deal combines Fox's sports, news, and entertainment content plus Tubi with Roku's connected-TV platform, The Roku Channel, and first-party data from 100M+ global streaming households; pro forma, the combined entity becomes the third-largest U.S. television player by viewing share. The deal requires regulatory approval; Fox shares fell roughly 11% in pre-market trading.

## II. Regulation & Policy

### 3. UK Announces Under-16 Social Media Ban, Extending to Stranger Chat and Livestreaming in Games

**Summary:** On June 15, UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced from Downing Street that the government will ban access to major social platforms — Snapchat, TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook, and X — for users under 16, with transitional rules for 17-year-olds to avoid a "cliff edge." Starmer also announced "world-leading" online gaming measures: developers must block under-16s from text and voice communication with strangers in games like Fortnite and Roblox, and restrict livestreaming gameplay to strangers. The government acknowledges enforcement difficulty and has tasked Ofcom with studying age verification; regulations are expected passed by end-2026, effective early 2027. Messaging apps like WhatsApp and Signal are excluded.

### 4. Netherlands Plans Full Online Gambling Ad and Bonus Ban, Cross-Platform Deposit Caps

### 5. Brazil Attorney General Seeks Supreme Court Suspension of Rio Grande do Sul Betting Ad Law

**Summary:** On June 15, Brazil's Attorney General's Office (AGU) filed with the Supreme Federal Court (STF) to suspend Rio Grande do Sul Law No. 16.508/2026 — imposing strict local betting advertising limits, set for full effect August 25. In unconstitutionality case ADI 7971, AGU argues commercial advertising authority is federal-exclusive and the state law conflicts with the 2023 federal betting framework and Finance Ministry regulations, creating regulatory fragmentation. The state law also bans ads near schools and medical facilities and empowers PROCON–RS to fine, block sites, and suspend licenses.

**Links:**
