## I. Regulation & Courts (India)

### 1. India’s Supreme Court upholds the 28% GST framework on full wager value for online money-gaming; large retrospective challenges dismissed
**Summary:** Major Indian business outlets report that, on May 27 (local time), the Supreme Court upheld the central government’s framework to levy a 28% Goods and Services Tax (GST) on the full face value of bets and stakes flowing through online gaming, fantasy sports, casino-style arrangements, and related “actionable claims,” treating key amendments as clarificatory and retrospectively applicable. The court dismissed a batch of industry challenges to massive legacy tax demands; published estimates of aggregate sector exposure vary by outlet, with recurring reporting bands around roughly ₹1.2 lakh crore to about ₹1.5 lakh crore. Commentators widely expect sharper cash-flow, compliance, and valuation pressure—layered atop a stricter legislative backdrop for real-money formats.

- [Zeebiz — Supreme Court upholds 28% GST on online gaming, rejects retrospective tax challenges worth Rs 1.5 lakh crore](https://www.zeebiz.com/india/news-supreme-court-upholds-28-gst-on-online-gaming-rejects-retrospective-tax-challenges-worth-rs-15-lakh-crore-396234)

**Commentary:** This further aligns “real-money online games” with a taxable betting/gambling narrative under Indian tax law—an overhang that may persist for digital entertainment startups and investors.

### 2. “Skill” does not automatically immunize a format under GST if money rides on uncertain outcomes (legal readout)
**Summary:** Medianama’s analysis of the combined judgment emphasizes the court’s GST framing: whether conduct is betting/gambling does not depend solely on “skill vs chance,” but on whether players stake money on uncertain future contingencies. The piece also walks through procedural implications, including the treatment of prior high-court relief (for example, issues tied to the Gameskraft show-cause trajectory as reported).

**Links:**

- [Medianama — Game of skills is gambling if money is on 'uncertain outcomes': SC](https://www.medianama.com/2026/05/223-game-of-skills-gambling-money-supreme-court/)

**Commentary:** The ruling pushes the industry debate from slogans about “skill games” to the structural question of stake, consideration, and taxable supply—tightening the compliance story for fantasy formats.
