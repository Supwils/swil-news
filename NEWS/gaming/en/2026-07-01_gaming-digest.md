### 1. Xbox Ends Project Fantasy Partnership with IO Interactive; Studio Announces Layoffs

**Summary:** On July 1, multiple outlets reported that IO Interactive — developer of *007 First Light* and *Hitman* — is laying off an undisclosed number of staff after an "external partner" ended its relationship on the in-development online fantasy RPG *Project Fantasy*. The studio said on X that it must adapt to a new reality and face short-term consequences including staffing decisions, while stressing it remains "100% committed" to the IP. The news lands amid reports of a broader Xbox business reset and studio closures/spin-offs; Avalanche's *Contraband* and other projects were previously halted after Xbox pulled funding.

### 2. Wemade Founder Park Kwan-ho Sells Full Stake to NeoPulse in ~₩920B Deal

**Summary:** On July 1, Korean and industry outlets reported that Wemade founder Park Kwan-ho agreed to sell his entire 39.33% stake to NeoPulse, a Korea-based subsidiary of Hong Kong's Shengsong Investment, in a deal worth about ₩920 billion (~$590 million) — roughly triple the current share price — with closing expected October 30, after which NeoPulse would hold 40.25%. The buyer cited *The Legend of Mir* IP recognition in China and Wemade's MMORPG expertise; both sides plan to expand Mir through Chinese IT and game partnerships and push AI-assisted development, next-gen graphics, and live-service operations. Herald Business noted this marks Chinese capital taking outright control of a Korean game company for the first time at nearly ten times the scale of Shanda's 2004 Actoz Soft acquisition (~₩96.1 billion), renewing concerns over IP and management sovereignty in Korea's game industry.

### 6. Korea FTC Opens Sanctions Against Google Over Game Exclusivity Deals; Fine Up to ~₩849.6B

**Summary:** Seoul Economic Daily reported on July 1 that Korea's Fair Trade Commission has launched sanction proceedings against Google over contracts with game companies. Examiners found Google significantly reduced developers' incentives to enter rival app markets such as One Store, constituting a "very serious" abuse of market dominance and obstruction of competitors; related Google revenue in Korea was calculated at about ₩141.6 trillion (~$92.18 billion), with a maximum penalty of 6% — roughly ₩849.6 billion — if illegality is confirmed in final deliberation. This remains examiners' opinion; Google may submit a written defense within eight weeks, with final penalties decided by the full commission.

### 8. Iowa Sweepstakes Casino Enforcement Authority Takes Effect July 1

**Summary:** Lines.com reports that Iowa Senate File 2289, signed by Governor Kim Reynolds on May 15, took effect July 1, amending the state's core gambling statute to grant the Iowa Racing and Gaming Commission cease-and-desist power and court-injunction authority over unlicensed dual-currency sweepstakes casino operators. Platforms such as McLuck, WOW Vegas, and Chumba Casino still accepted Iowa players as of May with no public exit announcements before July 1; IRGC has not named initial enforcement targets. At least five U.S. states passed sweepstakes-casino legislation in 2026; Iowa is among the few pursuing enforcement authority rather than outright prohibition alone.
