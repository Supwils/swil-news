## III. Regulation, Gambling & Regional Markets

### 7. Singapore Intensifies World Cup Illegal-Betting Crackdown Ahead of Kickoff

**Summary:** On June 9, Singapore's Ministry of Home Affairs and Ministry of Social and Family Development announced stepped-up enforcement against illegal gambling and football betting during the FIFA World Cup (June 11–July 19). Police will issue Online Criminal Harms Act directions requiring platforms to restrict illegal gambling services and ads, terminate local numbers used for promotion, and block linked bank accounts and card payments; the National Council on Problem Gambling launched its "Every bet costs something" campaign. Outside Singapore Pools, all gambling is illegal — offenders face fines up to S$500,000 and up to seven years' imprisonment.

### 9. New Zealand Publishes Online Casino Rules: Effective July 3, Up to 15 Licenses

**Summary:** New Zealand's government published implementing regulations for the Online Casino Gambling Act 2026, effective July 3 alongside a competitive licensing process.
