## I. Industry Shake-Up & Organizational Contraction

### 1. Xbox Launches "100-Day Reset": 3% Margin, Major Layoffs Expected After Fiscal Year Close

**Summary:** On June 11, Xbox CEO Asha Sharma and Chief Content Officer Matt Booty published a memo titled "Next 100 Days: XBOX Reset" on Xbox Wire, openly acknowledging severe challenges facing Microsoft's gaming business. The memo disclosed an accountability margin of roughly 3% for the fiscal year — well below industry averages and Microsoft's 30% target. Excluding Activision Blizzard King, Xbox spent over $20 billion on content, platform, and hardware subsidies over the past five years while annual revenue declined nearly $500 million. Sharma wrote that "this cannot continue," citing five key challenges: a hardware component crisis (storage costs potentially 5× higher than two years ago), an overextended studio system, overly complex platform infrastructure reliant on vendors, underfunded first-party IP, and a need to reassess exclusivity, windowing, and AI strategy. Bloomberg, The Verge, and others reported that Xbox is planning major layoffs and marketing budget cuts shortly after the June 30 fiscal year close; Polygon also noted Microsoft is moving away from the "Microsoft Gaming" brand label toward "We are Xbox."

## II. Regulation & Legislation

### 3. U.S. Senate Marks Up NO FAKES Act on June 11; ESA Warns of Impact on Game Development

**Summary:** On June 11, the U.S. Senate Judiciary Committee held a markup session on S.4591, the NO FAKES Act of 2026 — federal legislation establishing a consent-based framework against AI deepfakes and unauthorized digital replicas, backed by SAG-AFTRA, the RIAA, Google, OpenAI, and others. The video game industry, via the Entertainment Software Association (ESA), formally wrote to Chairman Chuck Grassley and Ranking Member Dick Durbin (letter dated June 9), warning the bill's broad "digital replica" definition fails to distinguish harmful deepfakes from legitimate fictional game characters. The ESA argued this could expose studios to frivolous lawsuits over background NPCs that coincidentally resemble real people, and inadvertently restrict multi-purpose avatar creation tools. Legis1 analysis noted committee passage would advance the bill toward a full Senate floor vote, with platform safe-harbor provisions still under negotiation.

**Links:**

- [ESA — Letter to Senate Judiciary Committee urging NO FAKES Act changes (Jun 9)](https://www.theesa.com/esa-sends-letter-to-u-s-senate-judiciary-committee-urging-changes-to-no-fakes-act/)
- [Outlook Respawn — Game industry pushback ahead of Jun 11 markup](https://respawn.outlookindia.com/gaming/gaming-news/video-game-industry-pushes-back-on-no-fakes-act-draft)

**Commentary:** Performer rights and game creation freedom collide at a critical legislative node — the ESA isn't opposing anti-deepfake goals but demanding a carve-out for fictional worlds, or U.S. AAA pipelines face new compliance and litigation costs.
