## I. Regulation & Platforms

### 1. Dutch Consumer Group Seeks €220 Million from Valve Over Alleged Steam Monopoly Pricing

**Summary:** On June 12, the Stichting Consumenten Competition Claims (CCC) launched collective proceedings under the name GameClaim against Valve, seeking more than €220 million in damages. The foundation alleges that Valve holds roughly 85% of the PC digital distribution market and uses Most-Favoured Nation (MFN) clauses to prevent developers from offering lower prices on rival stores such as Epic, keeping game prices artificially elevated for Dutch consumers since 2013. Copenhagen Economics estimates collective harm exceeding €220 million — about €130 per Dutch Steam account across roughly two million accounts. The case is currently in a pre-action settlement phase; Valve denies monopolistic behavior and argues its market position stems from platform quality and user experience.

### 2. India Online Gaming GST Fallout Continues: June 12 Media Deep-Dive on Supreme Court Ruling

**Summary:** On June 12, The Economic Times and other Indian outlets continued analyzing the Supreme Court's May 27 judgment in the Gameskraft GST case and its impact on real-money gaming. The court confirmed that 28% GST applies to the full face value of every bet, not just platform commission, and upheld the retrospective clarificatory effect of 2023 GST amendments — exposing the industry to potential tax liabilities near ₹92,000 crore. With the Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Act, 2025 already shrinking the real-money segment, platforms including Dream11, Gameskraft, and MPL face revived show-cause notices; listed gaming stocks such as Delta Corp and Nazara remain under pressure.
