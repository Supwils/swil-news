**Summary:** Reporting on June 7 cites Sharma's recent statements that Microsoft Gaming is undergoing a sweeping overhaul: stepping back from aggressive multi-platform expansion and controversial AI features, refocusing on console exclusivity and human-first development. Generative AI is now strictly banned from auto-generating creative content or replacing writers, artists, and designers; features like Gaming Copilot have been discontinued. The hardware team is stabilizing current-gen consoles while preparing 2027's next-generation "Project Helix."

## IV. Regulation & Industry

### 9. India's Supreme Court Online Gaming Rulings Explained: 28% Retrospective GST, State Bans Upheld

**Summary:** The Hindu published an explainer on June 7 covering the Supreme Court's May 27, 2026 twin rulings: a retrospective 28% GST on the full face value of online gaming bets is constitutional, and state laws prohibiting online betting/wagering are valid. The court held that when money is staked on uncertain outcomes, the skill-versus-chance distinction no longer applies. Industry estimates put back taxes at up to $14 billion sector-wide, with firms pivoting overseas or to social gaming. Separately, the 2025 Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming Act bans most real-money online games (with exceptions for esports, education, and social play); the Supreme Court will hear constitutional challenges on November 4.
