### 5. Star Citizen: Squadron 42 Buybacks Intentionally Cancelled to Prevent Gray Market Abuse
**Summary:** Cloud Imperium Games announced that Star Citizen's Squadron 42 package buyback program had been intentionally disabled following detection of systematic gray market abuse — where buyers were purchasing packages through buybacks at below-market prices and reselling them on secondary markets at a premium. CIG stated the cancellation was temporary and that controls were being implemented to allow legitimate buybacks while blocking gray market arbitrage.
**Links:**
- [CIG — Squadron 42 buyback cancellation announcement](https://robertsspaceindustries.com/)

## IV. Regulation & Markets

### 10. India PROGA: Non-Monetary Games May Receive "Deemed Approved" Status; Formal Rules Expected May–June 2026
**Summary:** India's Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) signaled that under the Proposed Regulation for Online Gaming Apps (PROGA) framework, non-monetary games — those not involving real-money wagering — may receive "deemed approved" registration status without individual review, substantially reducing the compliance burden for game publishers. Formal regulatory rules were expected in May–June 2026.
**Links:**
- [MeitY — PROGA framework update](https://www.meity.gov.in/)
**Commentary:** "Deemed approved" for non-monetary games is a practical recognition that reviewing every game title individually would create a multi-year backlog that would effectively prohibit new game publishing. The demarcation between monetary and non-monetary games — particularly for games with in-app purchases — will be the contested regulatory boundary.
