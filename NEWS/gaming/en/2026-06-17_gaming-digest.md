## I. Major Studios & Engine Ecosystem

### 1. Epic State of Unreal 2026: UE6 Roadmap Unveiled, Fortnite Skins to Work Across Games

**Summary:** On June 17, Epic Games outlined its vision for Unreal Engine 6 at the State of Unreal keynote during Unreal Fest Chicago. UE6 will merge Unreal Engine 5 and Unreal Editor for Fortnite (UEFN) into a unified editor, targeting Early Access by late 2027 and a full release roughly 12–18 months later (around mid-2029). The new Scene Graph gameplay framework is built on the Verse programming language, focusing on how games are made rather than pure graphical leaps. On interoperability, players will be able to bring Fortnite skins into third-party games that implement support, and developers can build skins that work with Fortnite — though each studio must integrate the systems on its own. Epic also announced that UEFN creators have been paid over $1 billion cumulatively, mobile UEFN playtime has doubled over the past year, and iteration times have dropped by an average of 40%.

### 7. India's Supreme Court: 28% GST on Full Stake Value, Retroactive to 2017 — ~₹2.5 Lakh Crore at Stake

**Summary:** On June 17, India's Supreme Court ruled that online gaming, fantasy sports, and casinos owe 28% Goods and Services Tax on the full face value of stakes, with 2023 amendments deemed "clarificatory" and effective retroactively from July 2017. Most operators had been calculating tax on platform fees or gross gaming revenue; cumulative industry liability is estimated at roughly ₹2.5 lakh crore. Markets fear retrospective enforcement will hit foreign investment attracted between 2017 and 2023 and undermine fiscal predictability; investors are watching how firms absorb the liabilities.
