### 7. Crossover trial: resonance breathing aids post-concussion symptoms and autonomic function
**Summary:** Per **Frontiers in Neuroscience** on **June 17**, a Turkish team ran a **3–1–3** week crossover in **26** rugby athletes with persistent post-concussion symptoms, comparing **resonance breathing (RB)** and **pelvic-floor resonance breathing (PRB)**. Both improved **Rivermead** symptom scores, **HRV**, and select **EEG** bands; RB showed stronger effects on **LF/HF** ratio and skin sympathetic activity. Authors support breathing interventions as non-pharmacological concussion rehab tools.

**Links:**

- [Frontiers in Neuroscience — Respiratory modulation in sports-related concussion (June 17, 2026)](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnins.2026.1825070/full)

**Commentary:** Concussion care is expanding beyond rest toward neuro-autonomic modulation; collision-sport athletes need graded return protocols, not guesswork.

### 8. ACTIV-6 trial: early metformin in acute COVID cuts long COVID risk ~50%
**Summary:** Per Pharmacy Times on **June 17**, **Clinical Infectious Diseases** published **ACTIV-6** results: among **2,983** outpatient adults **≥30** with mild-to-moderate COVID started within **7** days of symptom onset, metformin reduced clinician-diagnosed long COVID at **6** months to **0.56%** vs. **1.17%** on placebo — roughly **50%** relative risk reduction (RR **0.495**; posterior efficacy probability **0.96**).
