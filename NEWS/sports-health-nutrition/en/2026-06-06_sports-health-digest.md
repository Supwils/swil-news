### 5. ACSM Position Stand Update: Evidence-Based Resistance Training Prescription
**Summary:** Per **Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise** (April **2026**), the American College of Sports Medicine updated its **2009** resistance-training guidance by synthesizing **137** systematic reviews (>**30,000** participants). RT improved strength, hypertrophy, power, endurance, and gait speed vs. controls. Key findings: loads **≥80% 1RM**, full range of motion, **2–3 sets**, and **≥2 sessions/week** favor maximal strength; hypertrophy benefits from higher volume (**≥10 sets/week**) and eccentric overload; power training favors **30%–70% 1RM** loads with fast concentric phases.

**Links:**

- [ACSM — Resistance Training Prescription Position Stand (MSSE, 2026)](https://www.ovid.com/jnls/acsm-msse/fulltext/10.1249/mss.0000000000003897~american-college-of-sports-medicine-position-stand)

**Commentary:** A practical evidence framework for "what and how much to lift" — consistency and form beat chasing exotic training variables.

**Summary:** Per Medical News Today on **June 6**, a **Nature Communications** study (**May 28**) in preclinical models found **phytic acid (InsP6)** in whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds activates **HDAC3**, helping maintain intestinal tight junctions and reduce "leaky gut" risk. **Links:**
