## II. Health & Medicine

### 7. S-LiTE Trial: Exercise Outperforms GLP-1 Alone for Vascular Health in Weight Maintenance
**Summary:** Per **Nature Metabolism** (published **June 24**) and the **ADA 2026** Scientific Sessions, a prespecified secondary analysis of the **S-LiTE** trial followed **130** adults with obesity through **8 weeks** of low-calorie diet-induced weight loss (≥**5%**), then **52 weeks** of exercise, **liraglutide**, or both. Exercise alone or combined reduced carotid intima-media thickness and inflammatory cytokines (**IL-6, IFN-γ**); combination therapy also improved endothelial biomarkers (**sICAM-1, sVCAM-1, tPA**), while **liraglutide alone** showed no such vascular gains. ADA experts noted exercise plus GLP-1 may help patients **gain lean mass**; protein targets of **1.2–1.6 g/kg/day** were cited as effective.

**Links:**

- [Nature Metabolism — Exercise and liraglutide on vascular health in S-LiTE trial (June 24, 2026)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42255-026-01554-4)
- [AJMC — GLP-1 Therapies in 2026: Beyond Blood Sugar and the Scale](https://www.ajmc.com/view/glp-1-therapies-in-2026-beyond-blood-sugar-and-the-scale)

**Commentary:** Weight-loss drugs cannot replace resistance plus aerobic training; users should prioritize protein and strength work to preserve muscle and vascular resilience.

**Summary:** Per mindbodygreen on **June 25**, a study in the **American Journal of Clinical Nutrition** compared **20-year** cardiovascular risk across U.S. adults on low-fat, Mediterranean, and AHA-2020 dietary patterns. Versus low-fat eating, the **Mediterranean diet** was associated with **21%** lower relative CVD risk; AHA-2020 goals also outperformed low-fat. Heart protection hinges on **fat quality and overall dietary pattern** — olive oil, nuts, fatty fish, vegetables, legumes — not simply restricting total fat.

**Links:**

## IV. Lifestyle

### 14. Chinese Scientists Identify Tryptamine–GPR139 Pathway as Sleep-Pressure Signal and Insomnia Target
**Summary:** Per ScienceNet (June 19) and **Nature Neuroscience**, teams led by the Chinese Academy of Sciences Center for Excellence in Brain Science found cerebrospinal **tryptamine (TrpA)** tracks **sleep pressure** — accumulating with wakefulness and clearing during sleep. Wake-active monoaminergic neurons release TrpA in an activity-dependent manner; it acts via hypothalamic preoptic **GPR139** to promote sleep. **GPR139** agonists extended sleep duration and depth in animal models; human genetics also link the pathway to sleep-disorder risk.

**Links:**

- [ScienceNet — Chinese scientists find potential new insomnia target (June 19, 2026)](https://news.sciencenet.cn/htmlnews/2026/6/566893.shtm)
- [Nature Neuroscience — Tryptamine regulates sleep homeostasis (June 19, 2026)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41593-026-02332-x)

**Commentary:** From "catch-up sleep" to future drugs, sleep-homeostasis mechanisms are being mapped; fixed schedules, less blue light, and regular exercise remain first-line habits.

### 15. Asia's First Digital CBT-I Trial: Youth Depression Incidence Down 42%
**Summary:** Per CU Medicine and **PLoS Medicine**, **708** youths aged **15–25** with insomnia and subclinical depression were randomized to **12 months** of smartphone **digital cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia (Digital CBT-I)** or control. The intervention group showed nearly **60%** insomnia remission at one year; depression incidence was **10%** vs **18%** in controls — a **42%** relative reduction. **84%** of intervention participants completed all sessions; no related adverse events were reported.

**Links:**
