### 5. 30-Year Cohort: 90–119 Weekly Minutes of Resistance Training Cuts All-Cause Mortality 13%
**Summary:** Per MedicalXpress and Sina Finance (citing the **British Journal of Sports Medicine**, published **June 2, 2026**), Harvard-led researchers followed **147,374** participants for up to **30 years** and found **90–119 minutes/week** of resistance training linked to a **13%** lower all-cause mortality risk, **19%** lower cardiovascular mortality, and **27%** lower neurological disease mortality; no further benefit appeared above **120 min/week**. Combining **30–44 MET-hours/week** of aerobic activity with **60–119 min/week** of resistance training was associated with up to a **45%** mortality reduction.

## II. Health & Medicine

### 6. MIT Unveils Blueberry-Sized Ingestible Sensor for Core Body Temperature
**Summary:** Per MIT News and MedicalXpress on **June 15**, MIT engineers reported in **Nature Electronics** a swallowable sensor measuring **6 mm** in diameter and **4 mm** in height, accurate to **0.01°C**, drawing ~**10 nanowatts** and transmitting once per second via backscatter to an external antenna. Smaller than existing ingestible thermometers, it lowers GI obstruction risk; animal tests under anesthesia and while awake were accurate. Potential uses include infection fever monitoring, peri-anesthetic hypothermia alerts, and athlete heat-stress tracking.

**Links:**

- [MIT News — A tiny ingestible sensor can measure temperature from inside the body (June 15, 2026)](https://news.mit.edu/2026/tiny-ingestible-sensor-can-measure-temperature-inside-body-0615)
- [MedicalXpress — Blueberry-size capsule tracks core temperature (June 15, 2026)](https://medicalxpress.com/news/2026-06-blueberry-size-capsule-tracks-core.html)

**Commentary:** Continuous core temperature beats single-point forehead or oral readings; clinical and sports applications are promising, but widespread human use remains years away.

### 7. FDA Approves Pembrolizumab Plus Belzutifan for High-Risk Kidney Cancer After Surgery
**Summary:** Per MedicalXpress on **June 15**, the **FDA** approved **pembrolizumab** (anti–PD-1) plus **belzutifan** (HIF-2α inhibitor) as adjuvant therapy for adults with intermediate-high or high-risk **ccRCC** after nephrectomy. First approval for belzutifan in earlier-stage ccRCC and first PD-1/HIF-2α combination regimen.

### 12. 27-Year Danish Cohort: Nitrate Source Matters More Than Dose for Dementia Risk
**Summary:** Per NaturalNews on **June 15**, **Edith Cowan University** and Danish Cancer Research Institute researchers tracked **54,000+** Danish adults for **27 years** (**Alzheimer's & Dementia**), finding vegetable-derived nitrate linked to lower dementia risk (~one cup baby spinach/day equivalent), while nitrate from **red/processed meat** and **drinking water** (even at **5 mg/L**—one-tenth the EU limit of **50 mg/L**) linked to higher risk. Antioxidants in vegetables may promote beneficial nitric oxide; meat lacks them and may favor N-nitrosamine formation.

### 14. Sleep Lab Study: Pink Noise May Cut REM by ~19 Minutes; Earplugs More Effective
**Summary:** Per mindbodygreen on **June 15**, a controlled sleep-lab study in healthy young adults found pink-noise nights averaged ~**19 fewer minutes** of REM vs quiet control; deep sleep was not significantly disrupted.
