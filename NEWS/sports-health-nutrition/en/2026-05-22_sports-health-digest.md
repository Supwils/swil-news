### 5. Systematic review with network meta-analysis: HIIT leads reductions in visceral adipose tissue among people with overweight/obesity, with dose–response clues
**Summary:** A May 7, 2026, open-access article in *Nutrition & Metabolism* pooled 61 randomized trials (4,136 participants) and used pairwise, network, and dose–response meta-analyses to compare modalities for visceral adipose tissue (VAT) loss. The authors report all exercise arms beat control; high-intensity interval training (HIIT) showed the largest standardized effect, followed by aerobic plus resistance training, aerobic-only, and resistance-only training. Nonlinear dose–response analyses link total exercise volume to VAT change and provide modality-specific minimum effective dose ranges expressed in MET·min/week (see the paper for exact thresholds and uncertainty).
**Links:**
- [Nutrition & Metabolism — Unveiling the perfect workout: exercise modalities and dosages to combat visceral adipose tissue…](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12986-026-01129-w)
**Commentary:** For anyone trying to “target belly fat” with evidence, HIIT plus aerobic and strength work is a prescription scaffold—but it still must be individualized around joint history, cardiovascular risk, and sustainable intensity.

### 6. *PLOS One*: Morning caffeine brings neuromuscular performance in healthy females toward evening-like levels
**Summary:** In a randomized, single-blind, crossover trial published May 7, 2026, healthy females completed evening, morning placebo, and morning caffeine (6 mg·kg⁻¹) trials with intermittent isometric contractions to task failure. The authors conclude that caffeine ingested ~45 minutes pre-exercise partially offsets circadian morning decrements in neuromuscular performance, with patterns suggesting a predominantly central rather than thermoregulatory peripheral mechanism.
**Links:**
- [PLOS One — Caffeine ingestion improves morning neuromuscular performance to evening levels in healthy females](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0336199)
**Commentary:** For early-morning training or competition, caffeine remains an evidence-backed acute ergogenic aid—within boundaries of individual tolerance, sleep, and cardiovascular safety.

## III. Nutrition & Diet

### 11. *Nature Communications*: Whole-food plant-based diet lowers LDL cholesterol versus a typical American diet in adults with heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia
**Summary:** In a randomized, two-period, two-treatment, crossover, fully controlled feeding trial published May 20, 2026, 50 adults with genetically confirmed heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (HeFH) who were not taking lipid-lowering drugs consumed, in random order, four weeks each of a whole-food plant-based diet (WFPB) and a standard American diet (SAD) under isocaloric conditions. The authors report a clinically meaningful LDL-C reduction with WFPB relative to SAD (about −17.9%; see the paper for confidence intervals and P values) and discuss implications for diet as a cornerstone of HeFH care.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — Impact of a whole food, plant-based diet on LDL-cholesterol and cardiovascular risk factors in adults with heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia…](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-73468-4)
**Commentary:** This places “what plant-predominant eating can do” in a genetically high-risk population under controlled feeding—useful context for athletes with familial hyperlipidemia when discussing lifestyle layers with clinicians.
