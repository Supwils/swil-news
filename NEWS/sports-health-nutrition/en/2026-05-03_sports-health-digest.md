### 3. Exercise Physiology · Female Rat Model · Meteorin-like protein and reproductive axis signaling **Sports · Health**

**Summary:** An open-access paper in *Scientific Reports* (with article metadata indicating publication on May 3, 2026) from collaborating Turkish institutions examines meteorin-like protein (metrnl) versus moderate-intensity swimming (about 30 minutes/day, five days/week for roughly 19 days) in female rats with regular estrous cycles. The authors report metrnl administration reduced sexual-motivation-related behaviors and altered hypothalamic GnRH expression, reproductive hormones (including FSH and AMH), and ovarian follicle counts in patterns resembling effects attributed to the swimming protocol—leading them to propose exercise-induced metrnl as a candidate exerkine tied to female reproductive regulation under heavy training-like stimuli.

**Links:**
- [Nature — Meteorin-like protein may mediate exercise-related reproductive disorders in female rats](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-50646-4)

**Commentary:** These findings are mechanistic animal evidence—not clinical guidance—but they add biological context to conversations about RED-S risk and menstrual health in athletes.

### 7. Packaged Foods · Sodium and nutrient-quality gaps among Chinese ready foods sold online **Nutrition**

**Summary:** A cross-sectional study in *Nutrition & Diabetes* analyzes nutrient declarations for 2,087 ready-food SKUs drawn from four major Chinese e-commerce platforms. Applying a Health Star Rating–style scoring pipeline with proxy nutrients where labels were incomplete, the authors report a mean score near 2.7 (SD ~0.9) with only about 32.9% of items clearing a ≥3.5 “healthier” cutoff; abstract-level compliance statistics indicate roughly one-quarter of items align with estimated meal-level energy guidance (≈25.3% in the abstract) while more than about 85.6% exceed meal-level sodium thresholds aligned with Chinese dietary guidance—motivating reformulation, labeling, and consumer-education strategies.

**Links:**
- [Nature — Nutritional contents of ready foods in China](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41387-026-00413-1)

**Commentary:** As convenience meals scale, front-of-pack literacy plus pairing lower-sodium mains with home-prepared vegetables and whole grains is an actionable harm-reduction playbook.
