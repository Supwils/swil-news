## 2. Health & Medicine

### 4. USC team in npj Drug Discovery: selective cPLA2 inhibitors may ease brain inflammation in APOE4 carriers (Neuroscience)
**Summary:** A USC-led study published in Nature's **npj Drug Discovery** in late May identified small molecules that selectively inhibit **calcium-dependent phospholipase A2 (cPLA2)**, an enzyme whose role in brain inflammation surfaced via work on carriers of **APOE4** — the strongest known genetic risk factor for Alzheimer's. In vitro work showed a lead compound blunted Aβ42 oligomer-driven cPLA2 activation in human-derived cells; in mouse models it crossed the blood-brain barrier and modulated neuroinflammatory pathways. The team also secured roughly **$8 million** in NIH funding to advance development.
**Links:**

- [USC Today — Promising target for Alzheimer's-linked brain inflammation found](https://today.usc.edu/usc-scientists-identify-promising-new-target-for-alzheimers-linked-brain-inflammation/)
- [ScienceDaily — USC scientists discover a hidden Alzheimer's trigger and a possible way to shut it down](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260525000504.htm)

**Commentary:** Beyond anti-amyloid monoclonals, neuroinflammation is becoming the AD second front; an APOE4-targeted "precision anti-inflammatory" — if safely BBB-permeable — has a better clinical runway than blanket immunosuppression.

### 5. University of Lancashire RCT: 100 µL peppermint oil twice daily for 20 days cuts systolic BP by 8.5 mmHg on average (Cardiovascular)
**Summary:** A placebo-controlled randomized trial published in **PLOS ONE** (online April 23, continuing to circulate in late May) by the University of Lancashire enrolled **40** adults aged 18–65 with prehypertension or Stage 1 hypertension. The intervention arm received **100 microliters** of peppermint (Mentha × piperita) oil twice daily for **20 days**, versus a peppermint-flavored placebo. Systolic BP fell by **8.5 mmHg** on average in the intervention arm — comparable to starting the DASH diet, regular aerobic exercise, or low-dose antihypertensives.
**Links:**

- [Medical Xpress — Peppermint oil can lower blood pressure, clinical trial finds](https://medicalxpress.com/news/2026-05-peppermint-oil-blood-pressure-clinical.html)
- [PLOS ONE — Effects of peppermint oil on cardiometabolic outcomes in patients with pre- and stage 1 hypertension](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0344538)

**Commentary:** A 40-person sample and short duration make this a preliminary signal, but low cost and high adherence make peppermint oil worth testing in the grey zone between lifestyle and pharmacotherapy — for now it remains adjunctive, not substitutive.

## 3. Nutrition & Diet

### 7. University of Cologne in Nature Cell Biology: leucine shields outer mitochondrial membrane proteins by suppressing SEL1L, boosting cellular energy (Nutritional Biochemistry)
**Summary:** Led by Prof. Thorsten Hoppe at the University of Cologne and disseminated via ScienceDaily in late May, the **Nature Cell Biology** paper "Leucine inhibits degradation of outer mitochondrial membrane proteins to adapt mitochondrial respiration" shows that the essential amino acid **leucine** suppresses **SEL1L**-driven degradation of outer mitochondrial membrane proteins, protecting energy-producing machinery and raising cellular respiration efficiency. Animal models showed that disrupted leucine metabolism impairs mitochondrial function and fertility; related mutations also appear in some human lung cancer cell lines.
**Links:**

- [ScienceDaily — Scientists discover the nutrient that can supercharge cellular energy](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260520233221.htm)
- [PMC — Leucine inhibits degradation of outer mitochondrial membrane proteins to adapt mitochondrial respiration](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12611767/)

**Commentary:** This adds cellular-level evidence for "high-protein + leucine-rich BCAA" approaches in recovery and metabolic disease — but don't read it as "just take more BCAA supplements"; whole-protein dietary sources with complete amino acid profiles remain first-line.

### 8. MIT team in Nature: cysteine-rich diet activates CD8 T cell IL-22 pathway to drive intestinal repair (Nutrition & Immunity)
**Summary:** An MIT-led mouse study in **Nature** in late May found that of 20 amino acids tested, **cysteine** stood out for stimulating small-intestine regeneration. Mechanistically, absorbed cysteine is converted into **CoA**, taken up by **CD8 T cells**, which then secrete **IL-22**, a signaling protein supporting stem cell-driven repair. Cysteine-rich diets also helped repair radiation-induced intestinal damage; unpublished data suggest similar regenerative effects after **5-fluorouracil** chemotherapy.
**Links:**

- [ScienceDaily — MIT scientists discover amino acid that helps the gut heal itself](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260520233223.htm)
- [MIT News — A cysteine-rich diet may promote regeneration of the intestinal lining, study suggests](https://news.mit.edu/2025/cysteine-rich-diet-may-promote-intestinal-lining-regeneration-study-suggests-1001)

**Commentary:** A flagship case for "nutrition × immune cell metabolism": future oncology supportive care may move from empirical to evidence-based "chemotherapy + targeted amino-acid/micronutrient" protocols — though cross-species dose translation needs caution.
