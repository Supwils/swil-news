## III. Nutrition & Diet

### 9. Sports nutrition: *PLOS One* trial—morning caffeine (6 mg·kg⁻¹) lifts neuromuscular performance in young women toward evening levels **Nutrition**

**Summary:** Singh and Hesketh published May 7, 2026, in *PLOS One* a single-blind crossover in thirteen healthy, low-habit caffeine females comparing evening placebo, morning placebo, and morning caffeine at 6 mg·kg⁻¹ before isometric knee-extension testing. Morning caffeine increased peak force and time to exhaustion versus morning placebo, narrowing gaps with evening performance; higher EMG RMS with limited median-frequency shifts supports a predominantly central mechanism. Testing was scheduled within defined menstrual-cycle days without hormonal contraception.

**Links:**
- [PLOS One — Caffeine ingestion improves morning neuromuscular performance to evening levels in healthy females](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0336199)

**Commentary:** Early-start competitions may discuss timed, individualized caffeine use, but sleep, anxiety, pregnancy contraindications, and CYP1A2 variability matter—self-directed megadosing is unsafe.

## IV. Lifestyle

### 10. Sleep & mood: *Scientific Reports* finds U-shaped link between weekend catch-up sleep and depressive symptoms in China and U.S. youth **Lifestyle**

**Summary:** Leng et al., online May 5, 2026, pooled 1,440 Chinese young adults (2024 fieldwork) and 892 U.S. NHANES 2021–2023 young adults, defining elevated depressive symptoms with PHQ-9 ≥ 10 and categorizing weekend catch-up sleep. Versus none, about 0–1 hour of catch-up associated with lower odds in both countries; spline and threshold analyses suggested nonlinear inflection near ~0.58 h (China) and ~1.01 h (U.S.), with effect modification by sex and alcohol use in subgroup models.

**Links:**
- [Scientific Reports — Weekend catch-up sleep and depressive symptoms in young adults](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-50383-8)

**Commentary:** For chronically under-slept trainees, banking entire weekends in bed may not optimize mood; stable bedtimes plus aligned training intensity usually beat irregular crash weeks.
