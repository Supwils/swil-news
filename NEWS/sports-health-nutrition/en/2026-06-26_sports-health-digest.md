### 7. Meta-Analysis: Variable Resistance Training Yields Small but Significant PAPE Gains
**Summary:** Per **Sports Medicine - Open** (published **June 17**), a three-level meta-analysis of **13** studies and **95** effect sizes found acute **variable resistance training (VRT)** produced a small but statistically significant positive effect on subsequent performance (**g = 0.27**), especially for **jumping** and **Tier-3 athletes**. Multi-set protocols and equal-intensity VRT designs showed stronger effects (**g = 0.43 / 0.39**). Authors suggest VRT as a practical **conditioning activity (CA)** before competition, though evidence for top-tier athletes remains limited.

**Links:**

- [Sports Medicine - Open — Variable resistance training and PAPE meta-analysis (June 17, 2026)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40798-026-01028-7)

**Commentary:** Bands or chains can prime vertical jump and sprint work, but individualize load and monitor fatigue so activation doesn't backfire.

### 9. UT Southwestern: Time-Restricted Feeding Extends Mouse Healthspan; Humans Advised 12-Hour Morning Window
**Summary:** Per UT Southwestern and **Nature Aging** (reported **June 25**), **528 C57BL/6J** mice on standard chow showed slower gains in weight, body fat, frailty, and age-related disease under **8-** or **12-hour** nightly feeding windows; **8-hour** males lived roughly **12%** longer. Lead author **Joseph Takahashi** recommends humans eat within a **12-hour window starting in the morning**, aligned with circadian rhythm; human trials remain mixed and need further validation.

**Links:**

- [UT Southwestern — Eating at the right time is important for health (June 25, 2026)](https://www.utsouthwestern.edu/newsroom/articles/year-2026/june-health-eat.html)

**Commentary:** *When* you eat matters as much as *what* you eat; try fixing dinner time and cutting late-night snacks before reassessing body composition.
