## Nutrition & Diet Patterns

### 5. *Nature Medicine* RCT: six‑hour time‑restricted eating matches daily calorie restriction for PCOS weight loss · Chrononutrition
**Summary:** A **2026 *Nature Medicine*** randomised controlled trial (**N = 76 women with PCOS**, **ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05629858**) compared **six‑hour TRF (13:00–19:00 feeds, no calorie tracking)**, standard **daily caloric restriction (−25 % energy)**, and **no‑intervention controls** over **six months**. **Primary outcome—percentage body‑weight change:** TRF **−4.32 % (95 % CI −6.20 to −2.44, P < 0.01)** vs control; CR **−4.66 % (95 % CI −7.13 to −2.19, P < 0.01)**; **no significant difference between TRF and CR (Δ 0.34 %, P = 0.79)**; **zero serious adverse events** reported across arms. Electronic publication metadata lists **accepted 2 Mar 2026 / published 27 Mar 2026**—still clinically salient amid May’s PMOS terminological rollout.  
**Links:**
- [*Nature Medicine* — TRF RCT in women with PCOS](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04316-7)
- [ClinicalTrials.gov record — NCT05629858](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05629858)
**Commentary:** For patients fatigued by continuous calorie tallying, a structured midday feeding window appeared **non‑inferior** to explicit CR—yet drug timing, occupational schedules, and circadian phenotype still merit personalised vetting.
