### 14. UK Biobank: plant protein lowers joint disease risk; animal protein raises OA risk
**Summary:** Per **Nutrition & Metabolism** (**June 17**), over **12–13 years** of UK Biobank follow-up with **23,298** incident OA and **2,070** RA cases, **vegetable protein** correlated with lower OA (**HR 0.89**) and RA (**HR 0.76**) risk, while **animal protein** linked to higher OA risk (**HR 1.07**); **CRP** partially mediated associations. Whole grains, legumes, cheese, and yogurt associated with lower joint-disease risk; **red meat** with higher RA risk.

**Links:**

- [Nutrition & Metabolism — Dietary protein sources and chronic joint diseases risk (June 17, 2026)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12986-026-01140-1)

**Commentary:** Protein **source** may matter more than total grams; active adults can favor legumes, whole grains, and fermented dairy while moderating red meat.
