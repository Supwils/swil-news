## II. Health & Medicine

### 6. JACC cohort: ≥2 hours/week resistance training linked to 44% lower MI risk in women
**Summary:** Per **JACC** (published **June 17**) and Medical News Today (**June 25**), Harvard researchers followed **117,025** U.S. nurses for a mean **14.5 years**. Women doing **≥2 hours/week** of resistance training had **20%** lower major CVD risk and **44%** lower myocardial infarction risk (HR **0.56**); each extra hour/week was associated with **14%** lower MI risk. Greatest benefit came from combining resistance work, **150 min/week** aerobic activity, and **<2 hours/day** sedentary TV viewing.

**Links:**

- [JACC — Resistance training and CVD risk in U.S. women (June 17, 2026)](https://www.jacc.org/doi/10.1016/j.jacc.2026.04.036)
- [Medical News Today — 2 hours strength training cuts heart attack risk 44% (June 25, 2026)](https://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/2-hours-strength-training-a-week-may-lower-womens-heart-attack-risk)

**Commentary:** Women's cardiovascular prevention should not stop at aerobic mileage — two structured strength sessions per week belong in standard advice.

### 14. Pea protein preload before carbs beats co-ingestion for postprandial glucose control
**Summary:** Per **European Journal of Nutrition** RCT (**June 11**), **10** healthy adults in a crossover trial: pea protein **30 minutes before** a high-carb meal (PrePP) lowered postprandial glucose excursion more than taking protein with the meal (**0.46 vs 1.125 mmol/L**), with delayed peak; diastolic BP at **180 min** was **9.2 mmHg** lower vs carbs alone. Authors suggest PrePP may match whey preload strategies for glycemic management.

**Links:**

- [European Journal of Nutrition — Pea protein preload improves postprandial glucose (June 11, 2026)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00394-026-03971-3)

**Commentary:** On training days, shift part of your protein shake **20–30 minutes before** the main carb meal to stabilize glucose and satiety.
