### 5. HIIT acutely raises BDNF and improves executive function more than moderate or low-intensity training
**Summary:** Per **Scientific Reports** published **June 12**, a Turkish team compared low-intensity continuous training (LICT), moderate-intensity continuous training (MICT), high-intensity interval training (HIIT), and rest in **12** healthy young men using a crossover design with **7-day** washouts. HIIT produced significantly greater post-exercise increases in serum **BDNF** and blood lactate than all other conditions, and **only HIIT** significantly shortened Stroop test completion times. Authors suggest HIIT may be an effective acute neurotrophic stimulus for brain health.

**Links:**

- [Scientific Reports — HIIT modulates acute BDNF and cognitive responses (June 12, 2026)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-56739-4)

**Commentary:** "Brain training" need not mean puzzles alone; short HIIT may deliver metabolic and cognitive benefits — but progression and cardiovascular screening matter.

### 6. RCT: Percussive massage gun restores muscle strength within 24 hours vs. 48 hours for static stretching
**Summary:** Per **Frontiers in Sports and Active Living** published **June 3**, researchers compared fascia gun percussion, static stretching, and passive recovery for lower-limb exercise-induced muscle fatigue in athletes. The fascia gun group returned to baseline for RPE, thigh circumference, and muscle strength within **24 hours**; static stretching required **48 hours**, and passive recovery did not fully recover within **48 hours**. Percussive massage appeared more effective than traditional stretching for reducing swelling and accelerating strength recovery.

**Links:**

- [Frontiers — Fascia gun vs static stretching for exercise-induced muscle fatigue (June 3, 2026)](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sports-and-active-living/articles/10.3389/fspor.2026.1831667/full)

**Commentary:** Recovery tools are evolving; massage guns can supplement — but not replace — sleep, hydration, and progressive load management.

### 7. Systematic review: Micro-exercises significantly ease neck/shoulder pain in sedentary workers
**Summary:** Per **Scientific Reports** published **June 15**, a systematic review and meta-analysis of **19** RCTs (**n = 2,732** sedentary workers) found micro-exercises significantly reduced combined neck/shoulder pain (**g = −0.79**), neck pain (**g = −1.36**), and neck disability (**g = −0.45**), and improved quality of life; no significant effect on muscle activity; work absenteeism data were limited. Authors see office-based fragmented movement as scalable.

**Links:**

- [Scientific Reports — Micro-exercises for neck/shoulder pain in sedentary workers (June 15, 2026)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-56061-z)

**Commentary:** **2–3 minutes** of neck-shoulder micro-exercises per hour is a zero-cost desk prescription; pairing with standing and walking breaks may extend benefits.

## IV. Lifestyle

### 17. Swedish study: Women sleep better objectively yet report poorer sleep quality than men
**Summary:** Per News-Medical on **June 16**, Karolinska Institutet researchers published in **Sleep Advances** after monitoring **238** women and **238** men (ages **29–85**) with one night of home polysomnography. Women had fewer awakenings per hour, longer total sleep time, higher sleep efficiency, and more deep sleep — yet rated sleep quality lower than men. With age, men's deep sleep declined and awakenings increased more; women's objective metrics deteriorated less, but the subjective gap persisted. One-night data may not reflect long-term patterns.

**Links:**

- [News-Medical — Women track nighttime disruptions more accurately than men (June 16, 2026)](https://www.news-medical.net/news/20260616/Women-track-nighttime-disruptions-more-accurately-than-men-new-data-shows.aspx)

**Commentary:** Sleep complaints cannot rely on duration alone; women appear more sensitive to micro-awakenings — clinical assessment should combine subjective and objective measures.
