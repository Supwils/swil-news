### 7. Loma Linda Cohort: Five or More Eggs Weekly Linked to 27% Lower Alzheimer's Risk
**Summary:** Per Loma Linda University Health and Yahoo Health in **June**, a study in the *Journal of Nutrition* analyzed ~**40,000** participants in Adventist Health Study-2 over **15.3 years** on average. Adults **65+** eating **≥5 eggs per week** had up to a **27%** lower Alzheimer's risk versus never eating eggs; even 1–3 times monthly or 2–4 times weekly showed **17%–20%** reductions. Researchers cite choline, lutein, and omega-3s; the study received partial funding from the American Egg Board.

**Links:**

### 8. eBioMedicine Trial: High-Dose DHA Reaches Brain but Does Not Improve Cognition
**Summary:** Per Keck Medicine and News-Medical in **June**, a USC team reported in *eBioMedicine* a **2-year** double-blind RCT of **365** adults aged **55–80** with low fish intake and dementia risk factors. Daily **2000 mg DHA** raised cerebrospinal-fluid DHA by **17%** at **6 months**, confirming CNS delivery; yet at **24 months** there were no significant gains in memory, cognitive scores, or hippocampal volume versus placebo (~**47%** carried APOE4). Authors suggest omega-3s may work better within Mediterranean-style diets than as isolated supplements.

**Links:**

- [Keck Medicine — Fish oil supplements may not prevent Alzheimer's decline (2026)](https://news.keckmedicine.org/fish-oil-supplements-may-not-prevent-alzheimers-related-decline/)
- [News-Medical — High-dose DHA reaches brain but fails to protect memory (June 25, 2026)](https://www.news-medical.net/news/20260625/High-dose-DHA-reaches-the-brain-but-fails-to-protect-memory.aspx)

**Commentary:** Biological target engagement ≠ clinical benefit — brain-health strategies should prioritize whole-food diets, exercise, and sleep over fish-oil capsules alone.

### 11. Pea Protein Preload Significantly Blunts Post-Meal Glucose Spikes
**Summary:** Per *European Journal of Nutrition* on **June 11**, a randomized double-blind crossover trial in **10** healthy adults compared pea protein taken **30 minutes before** a carb-rich meal (PrePP) versus with the meal (PP). Versus carbs alone, PrePP cut postprandial glucose excursion from **1.89 mmol/L** to **0.46 mmol/L**, delayed the peak beyond **60 minutes**, improved satiety, and lowered diastolic blood pressure. Authors say pre-meal plant protein may match whey for glycemic control.

**Links:**

- [European Journal of Nutrition — Pea protein preload improves postprandial glucose (June 11, 2026)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00394-026-03971-3)

**Commentary:** Glycemic control need not rely on animal protein — vegetarians or lactose-intolerant people can try a small pea-protein preload **20–30 minutes** before meals.

### 12. UK Biobank: Plant Protein Lowers OA Risk; Red Meat Raises RA Risk
**Summary:** Per *Nutrition & Metabolism* on **June 17**, over **12–13 years** of UK Biobank follow-up, researchers documented **23,298** incident osteoarthritis (OA) and **2,070** rheumatoid arthritis (RA) cases. C-reactive protein partly mediated protein-source associations with OA.

**Links:**

- [Nutrition & Metabolism — Dietary protein and chronic joint diseases risk (June 17, 2026)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12986-026-01140-1)

**Commentary:** Joint care for active people extends beyond weight and training load — favor plant proteins and limit red meat within an anti-inflammatory eating pattern.

### 13. 100,000-Person UK Study: Sleep Schedule Consistency Matters More Than Duration Alone
**Summary:** Per *BMC Public Health*, analysis of ~**100,000** UK Biobank participants found most averaged **7–9 hours** of sleep yet only kept a stable **~4.8-hour** nightly sleep window. Stabilizing sleep across a **7-hour** routine at ~**8 hours** average could reduce future mental-disorder incidence by **23%** (HR **0.79**); **>25%** disrupted weekly routines on weekend nights, raising disorder risk by **10%**. Lower-income and minority groups showed poorer sleep regularity, highlighting health inequities.

**Links:**

- [BMC Public Health — Inconsistent sleep routines threaten mental health in 100,000 UK Biobank participants](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12889-025-24794-7)

**Commentary:** Avoid weekday discipline with weekend sleep revenge during competition prep — fixed bed and wake times are low-cost mental-health interventions.

**Summary:** Per mindbodygreen on **June 12**, research presented at **SLEEP 2026** surveyed **442** adults on chronotype, anxiety, loneliness, and mental health. Researchers advise night-oriented people to protect social connection — morning light exposure and relatively stable schedules — rather than forcing a morning-person identity.

**Links:**
