## II. Health & Medicine

### 1. NEJM Phase 3: Oral Daraxonrasib Nearly Doubles Median Survival in Treated Metastatic Pancreatic Cancer
**Summary:** Per CBS News, NEJM, and TPR on **May 31, 2026**, the **RASolute 302** trial (Revolution Medicines–funded) randomized **500** patients with previously treated **mPDAC** to daily oral **daraxonrasib** (RAS-targeting) vs chemotherapy. Median overall survival was **13.2 months** vs **6.6–6.7 months** (hazard ratio ~**0.40**, P<0.001); median progression-free survival ~**7.2–7.3** vs **3.5–3.6** months. Grade ≥3 adverse events were somewhat lower with daraxonrasib; treatment-related discontinuation was **1.2%** vs **11.2%**. The FDA plans expedited review and expanded access for eligible patients. Results were presented at **ASCO 2026** in Chicago.

**Links:**

- [NEJM — Daraxonrasib or Chemotherapy in Previously Treated mPDAC (published May 31, 2026)](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2605555)
- [CBS News — New drug nearly doubles survival in some pancreatic cancer patients](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/pancreatic-cancer-treatment-daraxonrasib-survival-rate/)

**Commentary:** A once-"undruggable" KRAS pathway now has an oral option with meaningful survival and tolerability gains—a genuine paradigm shift in pancreatic oncology.

**Summary:** Per Medical News Today, EurekAlert, and IBTimes **May 30–31**, University of Rochester neuroscientist **Maiken Nedergaard**'s **May 26** *Science* review argues that chronic stress, depression, cardiovascular disease, fragmented sleep, and aging may all disrupt synchronized neuromodulator rhythms during deep sleep, impairing **glymphatic** clearance of amyloid-β and tau. **Summary:** Per PsyPost, Bordeaux Neurocampus, and PubMed, NutriNeuro-led work in *Brain, Behavior, and Immunity* found ~**60%** of male volunteers showed fructose malabsorption (breath test), with higher plasma **LPS**, **STAI** anxiety scores, and distinct microbiota vs normal absorbers (~**30 g/day** fructose intake on average). **Glut5-KO** mice on 5% fructose diet showed anxiety-like behavior, microbiota shifts, and microglial inflammatory gene changes.
