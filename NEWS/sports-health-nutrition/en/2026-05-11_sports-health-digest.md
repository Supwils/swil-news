## III. Nutrition & Dietary Science

### 11. COSMOS substudy · Multivitamin–multimineral modestly brakes select epigenetic clocks—cocoa arms neutral  
**Summary:** A *Nature Medicine* COSMOS ancillary analysis (~958 older adults tracked at baseline plus 12/24 months with five methylation clocks) reports daily Centrum Silver–class multivitamin–multimineral supplementation produced small but statistically significant yearly attenuations versus placebo—notably roughly **−0.113 yr/yr on PCGrimAge** (**P≈0.017**) and **−0.214 yr/yr on PCPhenoAge** (**P≈0.032**)—with amplified signals among participants who showed accelerated aging at baseline; cocoa extract arms showed null clock shifts. Editors caution magnitude is modest relative to lifelong lifestyle exposures and hard clinical endpoints remain unanswered.  
**Sources:**
- [Nature Medicine — Multivitamins, cocoa extract, COSMOS epigenetic clocks](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-026-04239-3)  
**Commentary:** Supplement stacks cannot replace diversified whole-food patterns; clinicians should prioritize deficiency correction guided by labs before cosmetic anti-aging dosing.

### 12. Cardiac rehabilitation · Network meta-analysis positions supervised HIIE atop endothelial-function gains  
**Summary:** Coverage of a European Journal of Preventive Cardiology network meta-analysis (37 RCTs / **6818** adults with coronary disease or chronic heart failure) states high-intensity interval exercise most consistently amplified flow-mediated dilation versus usual care—and outperformed moderate continuous aerobic modalities—via recurrent shear-mediated nitric oxide dynamics. Combined high-intensity aerobic-plus-resistance programs showed the largest point estimates yet drew from thinner evidence strata. Investigators reaffirm stringent clinical screening prior to prescribing HIIT doses.  
**Sources:**
- [News-Medical — HIIT excels for endothelial dysfunction in CVD cohorts](https://www.news-medical.net/news/20260504/High-intensity-interval-exercise-is-most-effective-for-heart-patients.aspx)  
- [European Journal of Preventive Cardiology — Network meta-analysis (OUP)](https://academic.oup.com/eurjpc/advance-article/doi/10.1093/eurjpc/zwag118/8490220)  
**Commentary:** HIIT prescriptions belong in medically supervised pipelines first; social-media “killer week” challenges remain contraindicated for anyone with unexplained exertional symptoms.
