### 3. *Frontiers in Physiology*: a theoretical model of acute aerobic effects on weightlifters’ rate of force development
**Summary:** On May 8, 2026, West Virginia University researchers published an original article modeling how 2–60 minutes of acute aerobic exercise might blunt rate of force development (RFD) inferred from isometric mid-thigh pull force–time slopes. Under default simulation parameters, the model predicts steep RFD losses for shorter aerobic bouts (on the order of ~70% at about 10 minutes) with diminishing marginal declines thereafter. The authors stress the framework is not yet empirically validated and is intended as a coach-facing scenario tool, with open code and a small web app companion.
**Links:**
- [Frontiers in Physiology — Acute aerobic exercise and RFD in weightlifters (model)](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physiology/articles/10.3389/fphys.2026.1785061/full)
- [GitHub — RFDsimulator (companion code)](https://github.com/PistilliLab/RFDsimulator)
**Commentary:** It reframes concurrent-training “interference” as tunable math for programming conversations—while still begging for athlete-level validation.

## III. Nutrition

### 7. *Scientific Reports*: 2023 Nutri-Score dietary profiles linked to C-peptide and CRP in EPIC
**Summary:** A cross-sectional analysis published online May 13, 2026, in *Scientific Reports* studied 1,103 EPIC participants with biomarker data, deriving 2023 Nutri-Score–based dietary profiles from food-frequency style questionnaires. Multivariable models showed associations between poorer nutritional-quality profiles and higher C-peptide and C-reactive protein concentrations—markers aligned with insulin resistance and chronic inflammation—while underscoring cross-sectional limits on causality.
**Links:**
- [Scientific Reports — Nutri-Score profiles and metabolic biomarkers in EPIC](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-51357-6)
**Commentary:** Front-of-pack policy tools gain credibility when they correlate with blood-based risk axes, not just consumer surveys.
