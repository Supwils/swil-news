## II. Health & Medicine

### 1. ASCO five-year data: Moderna personalized mRNA melanoma vaccine plus Keytruda halves recurrence risk
**Summary:** Per NBC News (June 1, 2026), at **ASCO 2026** in Chicago, investigators reported five-year outcomes for **mRNA-4157 (V940)** plus pembrolizumab (Keytruda) after resection of high-risk stage III melanoma, published in the *Journal of Clinical Oncology*: among 157 patients, ~**70%** of the vaccine arm remained recurrence-free at five years vs. ~**49%** on standard therapy; recurrence risk fell by about half and metastasis risk by nearly **60%**. The vaccine is neoantigen-tailored; a larger phase 3 (~1,000 patients) is awaited.

**Links:**

### 2. Oral daraxonrasib: median survival 13.2 months in pretreated metastatic pancreatic cancer vs. ~6.7 on chemo
**Summary:** Per ScienceAlert and the *NEJM* (widely reported June 1, 2026, after ASCO weekend release), the RASolute 302 phase 3 trial randomized 500 patients with previously treated metastatic pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma to daily **daraxonrasib** (RAS-targeting) vs. chemotherapy. Median overall survival was **13.2 months** vs. **6.6–7 months** (HR ~**0.40**); grade 3+ adverse events were somewhat lower; the FDA plans expedited review and limited expanded access.

### 3. Global cohort study: social frailty accelerates progression to multimorbidity
**Summary:** Per *BMC Medicine*, in 24 countries (7,119 middle-aged/older adults free of baseline physical/psychological/cognitive conditions, ~7-year follow-up), **10.9%** were socially frail at baseline; socially frail participants had higher risk of progressing to any condition (HR 1.30) and **multimorbidity** (HR 2.10), with ORs >2 for several combined trajectories. Authors call for early identification, especially in general resources and basic social needs domains.

**Links:**

- [BMC Medicine — Social frailty and risk of progression to multimorbidity](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12916-026-04761-8)

**Commentary:** Community participation, social support, and check-ups are "anti-aging" investments; loneliness and social isolation should be treated as modifiable health risks.
