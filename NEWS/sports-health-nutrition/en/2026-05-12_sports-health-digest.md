### 6. Network meta-analysis argues high-intensity interval exercise optimises endothelial function in CAD/CHF · Cardiac rehabilitation
**Summary:** NewsMedical summarises peer-reviewed findings in *European Journal of Preventive Cardiology*: a synthesis of 37 studies (6 818 CAD or chronic-heart-failure adults) observed high-intensity interval exercise outperforming moderate continuous aerobic modalities for ultrasound-based measures of endothelial function, prompting calls for medically supervised tailoring in rehab.  
**Links:**
- [News-Medical.net — HIIT most effective endothelial stimulus in analysed CVD cohorts](https://www.news-medical.net/news/20260504/High-intensity-interval-exercise-is-most-effective-for-heart-patients.aspx)
- [*Eur J Prev Cardiol* — Advance article landing (Oxford Academic)](https://academic.oup.com/eurjpc/advance-article/doi/10.1093/eurjpc/zwag118/8490220)
**Commentary:** Intensity-structure refinements complement—but cannot replace—baseline risk appraisal, wearable telemetry where available, and staged progression.

### 10. Secondary analysis of crossover RCT contrasts low-fat vegan vs Mediterranean patterns on emissions and cardiometabolic markers · Sustainable diets
**Summary:** An AAAS EurekAlert release tied to *BMJ Nutrition, Prevention & Health* explains that, among 62 adults with overweight in a randomised crossover design, a low-fat vegan phase cut food-related greenhouse gas emissions ~57 % versus ~20 % for Mediterranean, along with favourable shifts in weight, insulin sensitivity, and blood lipids; animal-source food removal drove most environmental delta.  
**Links:**
- [EurekAlert! — Vegan diet trial environmental + metabolic outcomes](https://www.eurekalert.org/news-releases/1126563)
- [*BMJ NPH* — Article record](https://nutrition.bmj.com/content/early/2026/04/30/bmjnph-2025-001482)
**Commentary:** Pairing personal metabolic endpoints with planetary accounting is increasingly how nutrition evidence will be judged by funders and food systems planners.
