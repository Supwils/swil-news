### 2. BJSM 30-year cohort: 90–120 weekly minutes of strength training may be mortality "sweet spot"
**Summary:** Per MedicalXpress, Healthline, and EurekAlert (June 2–3, 2026), a **30-year** analysis in the **British Journal of Sports Medicine** of roughly **147,000** U.S. adults found that **90–119 minutes/week** of resistance training was linked to a **13%** lower all-cause mortality risk, **19%** lower cardiovascular death, and **27%** lower neurological death; benefits amplified when combined with aerobic activity, with **no further mortality gain above 120 minutes/week**. Bodyweight and band work count toward effective training volume.

**Links:**

- [MedicalXpress — 90–120 weekly minutes of strength training may be optimal (June 2, 2026)](https://medicalxpress.com/news/2026-06-analysis-weekly-minutes-strength-optimal.html)

**Commentary:** Evidence supports roughly **2–3 sessions of 30–40 minutes** weekly; consistency and movement quality matter more than stacking hours.

### 6. Exercise science: lifelong endurance training improves metabolism but not muscle strength in older men

**Links:**

- [Dove Medical Press — Lifetime endurance physical activity and cardiometabolic profile (June 2, 2026)](https://www.dovepress.com/lifetime-endurance-physical-activity-is-related-to-better-cardiometabo-peer-reviewed-fulltext-article-CIA)

**Commentary:** "Runners who never lift" risk sarcopenia later in life; endurance and resistance training should be combined, not traded off.

### 2. EPIC cohort (450,112 people): +30 g/day processed meat linked to higher stomach and esophageal cancer risk
**Summary:** Per Fox News and the latest **EPIC** analysis, researchers followed **450,112** Europeans for a mean **14.1 years**, identifying **876** gastric and **215** esophageal adenocarcinoma cases: each extra **30 g/day** of **processed meat** was associated with **9%** higher gastric cancer risk (95% CI 2–17) and **13%** higher esophageal adenocarcinoma risk (95% CI 0–27).
