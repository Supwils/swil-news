### 6. Exercise science: Meta-analysis finds variable resistance training modestly boosts jump performance
**Summary:** A **June 17** systematic review and three-level meta-analysis in **Sports Medicine – Open** pooled **13** studies and **95** effect sizes, quantifying acute **variable resistance training (VRT)** on **post-activation performance enhancement (PAPE)**. Overall effect **g = 0.27** (**p = 0.0016**) favored improved jumping; **3-set** protocols and equal-intensity VRT designs showed stronger effects (**g = 0.43** and **0.39**). Tier-1 elite-athlete data remain insufficient for firm conclusions.

**Links:**

- [Sports Medicine – Open — Variable resistance training and PAPE meta-analysis (June 17, 2026)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40798-026-01028-7)

**Commentary:** One to three moderate squat or deadlift sets with bands or chains before testing may help amateurs "prime" lower-body power without excessive fatigue.

### 8. Nature: Deep learning discovers new ECG biomarker for sudden cardiac death risk
**Summary:** A **June 24** **Nature** study linked all ECGs in a Swedish region to death certificates and trained a deep-learning model to flag sudden-death risk. Roughly **2.2%** of the cohort was high-risk with a **7.0%** annual sudden-death rate — higher than reduced-LVEF patients (**4.6%**); **86.1%** of model high-risk patients were missed by LVEF alone. High-risk patients with implanted defibrillators were **54.4%** less likely to die than expected. External validation succeeded in a U.S. health system and a Taiwanese hospital registry.

### 9. JAMA cohort: Consistent midlife resistance training cuts type 2 diabetes risk by 42%
**Summary:** A **June 22** **JAMA Network Open** study led by **Tianyue Zhang** (Zhejiang University School of Medicine) pooled **143,715** U.S. adults aged **40–60** across three cohorts, mean follow-up **19.2** years, with **10,038** incident type 2 diabetes cases. vs. no resistance training, **≥2 h/week** was linked to **27%** lower risk; **stable long-term** training in midlife (>30 min/week sustained) cut risk **42%** — intermittent training showed no benefit. Meeting **≥1 h/week** resistance, **≥15 MET-h** aerobic activity, and **<2 h/day** TV viewing together lowered risk up to **62%**; upper-body-only training (**25%** reduction) outperformed lower-body-only work.

## III. Nutrition & Diet

### 10. JAMA study: Anti-inflammatory diet linked to 21%–29% lower dementia risk even with Alzheimer's blood markers
**Summary:** Per **JAMA Network Open** (interpreted by **Medical News Today** in **June**), Sweden's **SNAC-K** cohort followed **1,865** adults **≥60** for up to **15** years; **240** developed dementia. Researchers compared Mediterranean, healthy-eating-index, and **anti-inflammatory diet (rEDII)** patterns. Among those with elevated **p-tau217, NFL, or GFAP** biomarkers, **only the anti-inflammatory pattern** consistently correlated with lower dementia risk — **21%–29%** relative reductions; Mediterranean and general healthy patterns helped more when biomarkers were lower. Anti-inflammatory eating emphasizes vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, tea, and coffee while limiting red/processed meat, refined grains, and sugary drinks.

**Links:**

- [Medical News Today — Anti-inflammatory diet may lower dementia risk up to 29% (June 2026)](https://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/anti-inflammatory-diets-lower-dementia-risk-even-alzheimers-early-signs)
- [Popular Science — A healthy diet may still make a difference for people at higher risk of dementia (2026)](https://www.popsci.com/health/a-healthy-diet-may-still-make-a-difference-for-people-at-higher-risk-of-dementia/)

**Commentary:** Those with family history or cognitive concerns should prioritize an anti-inflammatory dietary pattern over chasing single superfoods; observational data cannot prove causation, but dietary shifts carry low risk and plausible brain benefit.

### 11. Cell Metabolism: Low-protein, methionine-supplemented "longevity diet" extends healthy lifespan in mice
**Summary:** Per **Valter Longo**'s USC team in **Cell Metabolism** on **June 23**, a **low-protein longevity diet with methionine supplementation (LDMM)** modeled on Mediterranean/Okinawan patterns extended **healthspan**, cut fat mass and frailty, and improved cardiometabolic markers in **20-month-old** mice; Western high-fat/sugar and ketogenic diets increased fat and frailty. LDMM lowered **IGF-1** while raising **growth hormone, GLP-1, and FGF21**; **4-day fasting-mimicking diet** cycles every two months further improved metabolism. Human data linked highest animal-protein intake to higher obesity and diabetes prevalence despite lower calories.

**Links:**

- [USC Today — Low-protein amino acid-supplemented longevity diet (June 2026)](https://today.usc.edu/low-protein-amino-acid-supplemented-longevity-diet-linked-to-longer-healthy-lifespan-lower-frailty-risk-better-metabolic-health/)
- [Cell Metabolism — Methionine-supplemented longevity diet (2026)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1550413126002251)

**Commentary:** Longevity nutrition may hinge on plant-forward eating with fish and precise essential amino acids — not blind high protein or extreme keto; human trials are pending, so balanced Mediterranean-style eating remains the prudent default.

## IV. Lifestyle

### 12. Sleep specialist: Chronic exhaustion may signal one of 80+ sleep disorders, not just stress
**Summary:** Per the **Daily Herald** on **June 26**, sleep-medicine experts warn that months or years of fatigue unresponsive to standard sleep-hygiene advice may reflect one of **80+** clinical sleep disorders — not personality or willpower. Red flags include waking unrefreshed, feeling "tired but wired" at bedtime, daytime concentration and memory problems, and restless legs. Paths differ by diagnosis: **CBT-I** remains first-line for insomnia; sleep apnea may need weight loss, alcohol limits, and **PAP** therapy; circadian disorders may respond to micro-dose melatonin or bright-light therapy rather than forcing an early chronotype.

**Links:**

- [Daily Herald — Sleep doctor zeroes in on signs you have a real problem (June 26, 2026)](https://www.dailyherald.com/20260626/health-and-fitness/bedtime-exhaustion-sleep-doctor-zeroes-in-on-signs-you-have-a-real-problem/)

**Commentary:** Athletes whose recovery lags despite rising training load should seek sleep-specialist referral instead of stacking caffeine; quality sleep is the hidden supplement for adaptation and immunity.
