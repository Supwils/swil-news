### 6. BJSM 30-year cohort: 90–120 weekly minutes of strength training may be mortality "sweet spot"
**Summary:** Per NetEase citing Medscape/BJSM coverage (June 5, 2026), a **30-year** analysis in the **British Journal of Sports Medicine** (published online **June 2, 2026**) of roughly **147,000** U.S. adults found **90–119 minutes/week** of resistance training linked to **13%** lower all-cause mortality, **19%** lower cardiovascular death, and **27%** lower neurological death; benefits amplified with aerobic activity, with **no further mortality gain above 120 minutes/week**. Bodyweight, bands, and home exercises count toward effective volume.

**Links:**
