### 5. British Journal of Sports Medicine: network meta-analysis on exercise modalities and 24-hour ambulatory blood pressure (Exercise science)
**Summary:** On May 12, 2026, Medical Xpress summarized a British Journal of Sports Medicine network meta-analysis that screened randomized trials from November 2024 to August 2025, requiring structured training lasting at least four weeks. ## III. Nutrition & Diet

### 9. PREDIMED-Plus: energy-restricted Mediterranean diet plus exercise/support cuts six-year diabetes incidence (Nutrition)
**Summary:** ScienceDaily on May 19, 2026, summarizes the Spanish PREDIMED-Plus trial randomizing 4,746 adults aged 55–75 with overweight/obesity and metabolic syndrome but without baseline diabetes or cardiovascular disease. Over ~six years, an intervention bundling roughly 600 kcal/day caloric reduction from a Mediterranean pattern, moderate exercise (e.g., brisk walking plus strength and balance elements), and professional weight-loss support reduced type 2 diabetes incidence by about 31% versus an ad libitum Mediterranean control, alongside larger average weight and waist reductions. The European Research Council also published a plain-language summary of the same trial news line.
**Links:**
- [ScienceDaily — Lower-calorie Mediterranean diet with exercise cuts diabetes risk](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260519003103.htm)
- [ERC — Mediterranean diet with calorie reduction and exercise lowers diabetes risk by 31%](https://erc.europa.eu/news-events/news/mediterranean-diet-combined-calorie-reduction-and-exercise-lowers-diabetes-risk-31)
**Commentary:** “Diet pattern + executable exercise prescription + behavioral support” beats slogan-level “eat less, move more” for real-world prevention.
