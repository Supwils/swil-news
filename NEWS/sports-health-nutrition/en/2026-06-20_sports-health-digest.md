### 7. Phase 3 Trial: Pancreatic Cancer Pill Daraxonrasib Doubles Median Survival
**Summary:** Per AOL on **June 20**, a **500-patient Phase 3** trial published in **The New England Journal of Medicine** found oral KRAS inhibitor **daraxonrasib** for metastatic pancreatic cancer delivered median overall survival of **13.2 months** vs. **6.7** with chemotherapy; **31.6%** of patients saw tumors shrink or disappear vs. **11.2%** on chemo. Patients reported less pain and fatigue and better quality of life; common side effects include rash and GI issues. The FDA granted early access in **May** for some failed-line patients; full approval is pending.

**Links:**

- [AOL — New Pancreatic Cancer Pill Doubles Survival Rate (June 20, 2026)](https://www.aol.com/news/pancreatic-cancer-pill-doubles-survival-000600842.html)

### 11. BMJ 205,000-Person, 40-Year Study: Three Weekly Fries Servings Raise Diabetes Risk 20%
**Summary:** Per UDN on **June 20** citing **The BMJ**, tracking **205,000+** people over nearly **40** years found **3 weekly servings** of french fries (roughly **113–170 g** each) raised **type 2 diabetes** risk by **20%**; baked, boiled, or mashed potatoes showed no significant association. Replacing **3** potato servings with whole grains cut diabetes risk by **8%** overall and **19%** when replacing fries; substituting white rice increased risk.

**Links:**

- [UDN — 200,000-person study on potato preparation and diabetes (June 20, 2026)](https://udn.com/news/story/7266/9577597)

**Commentary:** Risk tracks frying, salt, and fast-food patterns — not potatoes alone; steam or bake at home and prioritize whole grains as staples.
