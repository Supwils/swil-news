**Summary:** Per News-Medical.net's report on a paper published online March 19, 2026, a team from the University of Queensland, University of Southern Queensland, and Stanford University examined how a 9-week Nordic hamstring exercise program changes the muscle-tendon mechanics of the biceps femoris long head — the most commonly injured muscle in sprinting. **Links:**

### 6. Communications Medicine: 650,000+ IBS Adults Over ~20 Years — Long-Term Use of Certain Common Medications Associated with Higher Mortality Risk (Observational; Not Causal)
**Summary:** ScienceDaily reported Cedars-Sinai's April 16 study: the team analyzed long-term records of over 650,000 U.S. adults diagnosed with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), finding that long-term antidepressant use was associated with elevated mortality risk (cited at approximately 35%), with certain antidiarrheal agents (such as loperamide and diphenoxylate categories) also reporting similar signals. Clinical takeaway is prompting physicians and patients to jointly review long-term medication benefit-risk and alternative management strategies.

### 8. Cell Reports: FGF21 Reverses Obesity in Mice via Hindbrain Parabrachial Neuron Population; Partially Overlaps with GLP-1 Mechanism but Differs
**Summary:** ScienceDaily reported a University of Oklahoma team's April 16 paper: natural hormone FGF21 (fibroblast growth factor 21) in obese mouse models can promote energy expenditure and improve obesity phenotype by activating a parabrachial-projecting neuron circuit in the hindbrain. The paper emphasized anatomical overlap with GLP-1 receptor agonists in "hindbrain energy regulation hotspots" but noted this is not the same appetite-suppression mechanism — suggesting future metabolic disease intervention may offer combination or stratified target opportunities. Research remains in animal studies; clinical translation will take time.
