### 2. ACSM Updates Resistance Training Position Stand with "Overview of Reviews": Consistency Over Complexity
**Summary:** The **American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM)** published an updated position stand — ***Resistance Training Prescription for Muscle Function, Hypertrophy, and Physical Performance in Healthy Adults: An Overview of Reviews*** — in the **April 2026** issue of ***Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise***, synthesizing evidence from over **100 systematic reviews** and more than **30,000 participants**. Key findings: in healthy adults, **adherence and executability** often matter more than elaborate periodization schemes; **concentric velocity, relative load, and weekly set volume** are the most stable "primary levers" for strength, hypertrophy, and power, while questions like **training to failure** and **free weights vs. machines** are less determinative for many populations than previously emphasized.
**Links:**
- [ACSM — Updated Resistance Training Guidelines (2026)](https://acsm.org/resistance-training-guidelines-update-2026/)
- [PubMed — ACSM Position Stand abstract](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41843416/)
**Commentary:** For coaches and advanced trainees, this means channeling programming away from "complex periodization" toward a **sustainable prescription**: clear objectives, stable frequency, and maximizing the primary variables within joint-allowed range.
