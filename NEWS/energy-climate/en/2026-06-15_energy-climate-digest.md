### 1. LNG carrier *Disha* transits Strait of Hormuz on June 15 as shipping resumes after US–Iran preliminary deal (oil & gas)
**Summary:** Firstpost reported on **June 15, 2026** that the Maltese-flagged LNG tanker *Disha* successfully crossed the Strait of Hormuz, among the first commercial vessels to resume transit after a preliminary US–Iran diplomatic breakthrough. Chartered by India's Petronet LNG, the vessel had loaded at Qatar's Ras Laffan in **March** and was stranded in the Gulf before sailing with **62,370** metric tonnes of LNG to Dahej, Gujarat, expected to arrive **June 18**. India's Ministry of Ports, Shipping and Waterways said **10** India-flagged and **5** foreign vessels have crossed the strait so far, with the last in the current group also due **June 18**. The chokepoint normally carries roughly **one-fifth** of global oil and LNG seaborne trade; traffic had been largely halted since late-February escalation. European gas fell as much as **5.8%** in early Asian trading on June 15 and Brent crude dropped more than **4%**, though shippers still await mine clearance and deal details.

### 2. QatarEnergy moves a ninth known LNG cargo on June 15; "going dark" transits persist since Hormuz crisis (oil & gas)
**Summary:** Energy News Beat on **June 15, 2026** cited Bloomberg's Stephen Stapczynski reporting that QatarEnergy's tanker *Al Daayen* went dark on AIS tracking over the weekend and reappeared east of Hormuz in the Arabian Sea heading for China. Since US–Israeli strikes on **February 28** and Qatar's **March 4** force-majeure halt, only **nine** known LNG cargoes have exited the Gulf—far below the pre-war pace of roughly one cargo per day. Pre-war Qatar exported about **77 million tonnes** of LNG annually; Iranian attacks damaged roughly **17%** of Qatar's LNG capacity with repairs estimated at **3–5 years**; private war-risk markets largely withdrew coverage and the IRGC established a strait authority charging transit fees. Northeast Asia's structural deficit is widening, US exporters are benefiting, and European prices remain elevated as "going dark" becomes standard practice.

## III. Clean Power & Storage

### 6. CHN Energy completes 400 MW solar–hydrogen–storage integrated project in Rudong; media highlight on June 15 (clean power)
**Summary:** pv magazine on **June 15, 2026** reported China Energy Investment Corp (CHN Energy) completed its Guohua Rudong integrated project on tidal flats near Yangkou Port, Nantong, Jiangsu, with system-wide joint commissioning finished **June 10** and performance meeting design specs. The site includes **400 MW** coastal PV, **60 MW/120 MWh** battery storage, a **1,500 Nm³/h** green-hydrogen unit (designed for **482 tonnes** of high-purity hydrogen annually), a **220 kV** shore substation, and **500 kg/day** hydrogen refueling. PV is expected to generate about **468 GWh** per year, enough for nearly **200,000** households; the hydrogen plant remains in final commissioning, targeted for **August 2026**. A dedicated submarine cable can feed the electrolyzer directly from surplus solar without passing through the public grid; storage smooths PV fluctuations. The project is part of China's third batch of large wind and solar bases and includes **2.9 km²** of tidal-flat ecological restoration.
