### 1. China releases 15th Five-Year New Energy System Plan; non-fossil power to become the main electricity source by 2030 (Policy)
**Summary:** Xinhua and the National Development and Reform Commission reported on **June 25, 2026** that NDRC and the National Energy Administration have issued the *Plan for Building a New Energy System During the 15th Five-Year Period*, targeting a preliminary clean, low-carbon, secure, and efficient energy system by **2030**. Key goals include comprehensive energy production capacity of **5.8 billion tonnes** of standard coal equivalent; peaking coal and oil consumption; a **25%** share of non-fossil energy in final consumption; wind and solar exceeding **50%** of installed power capacity; and non-fossil sources supplying **50%** of electricity generation. The plan lays out six major system-building tasks, a nationally coordinated spatial layout with five non-fossil growth zones, and explicit language on compute–power coordination. Cailian Press cited China Electricity Council data showing wind and solar at roughly **47.9%** of capacity as of end-May—close to the **2030** **50%** target—while the non-fossil generation share remains about **10** percentage points below the **2030** benchmark.

### 2. Paris court orders TotalEnergies to include customer indirect emissions within six months — first climate application of corporate duty-of-vigilance law (Carbon Markets)
**Summary:** AP and Bloomberg reported on **June 25, 2026** that a Paris court ruled TotalEnergies must revise its climate plan within **six months** to account for Scope **3** greenhouse gas emissions from customers' use of its oil and gas products. Judges held the company can influence value-chain emissions through portfolio and energy-mix choices. The court did not grant NGOs' demands to force production cuts and scheduled a January **2027** review of the updated assessment. This is the first time France's **2017** corporate duty-of-vigilance law has been applied to climate change. TotalEnergies said it will update its policies and noted a **28%** reduction in operational emissions since **2015**. The ruling landed amid a record heat wave in France, against a backdrop of recent climate rulings from the European Court of Human Rights and the International Court of Justice.

**Links:**

**Commentary:** Scope **3** is now a legal obligation, not a voluntary disclosure—oil majors' climate ledgers are being stretched from the smokestack to the fuel tank, even if production-cut lines remain unbroken.

### 5. Record European heat wave persists; France temporarily shuts two nuclear reactors over river temperatures (Climate & Disasters)
**Summary:** UN News and WMO reported on **June 25, 2026** that a record-breaking heat wave is gripping much of Europe, with more than **100 million** people facing temperatures above **35°C**. ### 6. MIT Technology Review on June 25 analyzes Europe's heat-wave "triple squeeze" on the power grid (Climate)
**Summary:** MIT Technology Review published on **June 25, 2026** that the European heat wave is squeezing grids from both supply and demand: cooling load is surging, transmission and generation efficiency is falling, and some thermal and nuclear plants are derating or shutting because cooling water is too warm or scarce. Bruegel senior fellow Simone Tagliapietra describes a "triple squeeze"—rising cooling demand, lower plant and grid efficiency, and output cuts at thermal and nuclear units. European grids historically peak in winter because electric heating is widespread, but planned spring–summer outages overlapping heat-driven shutdowns are amplifying summer risk. An London Climate Action Week event on extreme heat was itself cancelled because of the heat.

**Links:**

- [MIT Technology Review — What Europe's heat wave means for the power grid](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/25/1139690/europe-heat-wave-grid/)

**Commentary:** Grid design still assumes winter peaks—in a June record heat wave, Europe's seasonal power playbook needs rewriting.
