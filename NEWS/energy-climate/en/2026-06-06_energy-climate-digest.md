### 1. Seven Northeastern US States Sue Over ~$1B TotalEnergies Offshore Wind Lease Cancellation (Policy)
**Summary:** Per ABC News and Courthouse News (June 2–6, 2026), New York Attorney General Letitia James leads Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, New Jersey, New York, Rhode Island, and Vermont in suing the Trump administration in federal court in Washington, D.C. The suit challenges a March 2026 Interior Department agreement with TotalEnergies subsidiary Attentive Energy: cancellation of offshore wind leases ~47 miles off New York and two leases off North Carolina, with ~$795M–$928M reimbursed from the Judgment Fund, in exchange for fossil fuel investments in Texas and a pledge not to pursue new US offshore wind. States argue violations of the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act and the Judgment Fund Act. Attentive Energy One was expected to power New York City, create 1,700+ jobs, and support at least 5 GW of offshore wind by 2040. Governor Hochul called it a "pay-not-to-play" abuse of taxpayer dollars.

### 2. New York Legislature Passes Nation's First Statewide One-Year Data Center Permit Moratorium (Policy)
**Summary:** The Register and WinBuzzer (June 5–6, 2026) report that the New York Legislature passed the Responsible Data Center Development Act (S10642/A11560): a one-year permit pause for new data centers with peak load of 20 MW or more, plus a statewide environmental impact report within 18 months. The bill creates a dedicated electricity rate class requiring large data centers to fully bear grid upgrade costs, and mandates one-third renewable power by 2030–2034, two-thirds by 2035–2039, and 90% from 2040 onward. Senate Energy Chair Kristen Gonzalez cited 28 grid-interconnection projects totaling ~9,682 MW of additional demand. The bill awaits Governor Kathy Hochul's signature by December; she has previously favored municipal control over siting.

**Links:**

- [The Register — New York advances one-year datacenter permit moratorium](https://www.theregister.com/legal/2026/06/05/new_york_advances_one-year_datacenter_permit_moratorium/5251911)

**Commentary:** AI power demand is shifting from a tech issue to state energy politics — the first statewide moratorium could trigger a chain reaction in data center siting rules nationwide.

### 3. China Issues Unified Non-Fossil Power Consumption Accounting Guidelines (Policy)
**Summary:** Per CCTV and NEA (June 1–2, 2026), five ministries including NDRC and NEA issued the Trial Guidelines for Non-Fossil Energy Electricity Consumption Accounting (NDRC Energy [2026] No. 622) — China's first nationwide unified rules. The guidelines define physical, transactional, and allocation-based recognition paths, with green certificate trading as the primary basis, and stipulate that each kilowatt-hour can be claimed by only one entity to prevent double counting. NEA notes ~95% of China's non-fossil energy consumption is electricity; the guidelines align with the non-fossil share indicator in the carbon peaking/neutrality comprehensive assessment framework, with next steps to improve green certificate issuance and trading systems.

**Links:**

### 4. India Launches E85 Ethanol Fuel on June 5, Targeting 500 Stations by Year-End (Policy)
