### 1. Investors Managing €12 Trillion Urge EU to Keep ETS Strong, Reject Carbon Market Dilution (Carbon Market)
**Summary:** Euronews and IIGCC reported on **June 10, 2026** that **46** major European investors representing **€12 trillion** in assets have written to EU leaders warning that private capital for industrial decarbonisation requires a strong, predictable Emissions Trading System (ETS) at the core of economic strategy. The letter comes ahead of EU Council talks on **June 18–19** on the ETS future and a legislative review scheduled for **July 15**, explicitly opposing calls from Italy, Germany and heavy industry to weaken or dismantle the carbon market. Investors argue Europe's competitiveness problems stem from high electricity prices, grid constraints and limited access to affordable clean power—not excessive carbon pricing—and should be addressed through complementary policies on grids, electrification and clean-tech finance. The European Commission's recent four-year plan estimates roughly **€660 billion** annually is needed to cut fossil-fuel dependence, rising to **€695 billion** between 2031 and 2040.

### 2. U.S. Federal Court Vacates IRS Notice, Restores 5% Safe Harbor for Wind and Large Solar Tax Credits (Policy)
**Summary:** Daily Energy Insider reported on **June 10, 2026** that the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia ruled on **June 6** that the IRS acted arbitrarily and capriciously in Notice 2025-42 by eliminating the **5%** safe harbor for wind and solar projects above **1.5 MW**, vacating the notice nationwide. Developers may again qualify for **45Y/48E** clean electricity tax credits by incurring **5%** of total project costs before the physical-construction test deadline. The court cited industry reliance interests built over more than a decade; as of **June 6** the government had not appealed. Developers unable to meet physical-construction requirements before **July 4, 2026** may revisit the **5%** pathway, though appeals or new rules remain a risk.

### 3. China Expands Green Power Direct Connection from Single-User to Multi-User "Cluster Sharing" (Policy)
**Summary:** People's Daily (via Science and Technology Daily) reported on **June 10, 2026** on NDRC and NEA guidance (发改能源〔2026〕688号) allowing renewable plants to supply multiple users in industrial parks and zero-carbon zones via dedicated lines—upgrading green power direct connection from one-to-one "single-point binding" to one-to-many "cluster sharing." Projects must follow a "load determines source" principle, with annual self-consumed generation at least **60%** of total available output and at least **30%** of total consumption (**35%** before **2030**). Expert Su Xiaoling noted complex multi-user ownership and uncertain source-load profiles require coordinated storage, flexible loads and grid-forming equipment. With CBAM and other carbon barriers tightening, traceable green power via direct connection is becoming a practical compliance path for export-oriented firms.

**Links:**

**Commentary:** Scaling green direct connection depends on whether storage and dispatch can keep pace with the flexibility gap created by dedicated-line physical isolation.
