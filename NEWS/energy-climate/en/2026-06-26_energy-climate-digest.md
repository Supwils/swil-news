## I. Policy & Carbon Markets

### 1. EU energy ministers reach general approach on European Grids Package — June 26 (Policy)
**Summary:** The Council of the EU and INSIGHT EU reported on **June 26, 2026** that energy ministers meeting in Luxembourg adopted a negotiating position (general approach) on the European Grids Package, covering a revised Trans-European Energy Infrastructure (TEN-E) Regulation and a new Permitting Directive. The package aims to boost cross-border interconnection, electrification, and decarbonization through coordinated planning across electricity, hydrogen, and gas networks, and reinvestment of congestion income in cross-border projects — starting at **10%** from **January 2028**, rising **5** percentage points annually to **25%** by **2031**. Ministers backed simplified permitting, digital application portals, and treating electricity and renewable projects as overriding public interest unless proven otherwise, plus a new priority category for security and resilience of existing grids. German Energy Minister Katherina Reiche called grids the "operating system of the energy transition"; EU Energy Commissioner Dan Jørgensen said grids are the biggest bottleneck to lower prices, decarbonization, and independence, with over **€1 trillion** needed by **2040**. Negotiations with the European Parliament follow, targeting final agreement in **2026**.

### 3. Washington state signs linkage agreement to join California–Quebec carbon market by 2027 (Carbon market)

**Summary:** Semafor and MIT Technology Review reported on **June 26, 2026** that sustained heat stresses power systems on both supply and demand: AC load surges as solar output falls in the evening; anticyclonic still air limits wind; French nuclear units shut or derate as cooling water overheats.
