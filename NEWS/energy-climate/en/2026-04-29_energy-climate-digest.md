### 3. EU April Infringements Package: Electricity-Market Reform and Renewables Directive Enforcement
**Summary:** On April 29, 2026 the European Commission published energy-related infringement decisions: it sent reasoned opinions to Croatia, Poland, and Portugal for failing to transpose the revised electricity market design Directive (EU) 2024/1711, arguing full implementation would stabilize consumer prices, better reflect low marginal costs of renewables, and strengthen consumer protections; it sent Hungary a reasoned opinion on Energy Charter Treaty investor–state arbitration compliance; and it referred Greece, Malta, and Portugal to the Court of Justice over incomplete transposition of the strengthened renewables promotion Directive (EU) 2023/2413, seeking fines to accelerate clean power and emissions cuts.
**Links:**
- [European Commission DG ENER — April infringements package: key decisions on energy](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/news/april-infringements-package-key-decisions-energy-2026-04-29_en)
- [European Commission — Press corner (package context)](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/inf_26_720)
**Commentary:** “Affordable energy” in the EU is not just rhetoric but litigable law—transpose too slowly and courts and penalty risk expose the gap between retail power politics and Brussels climate statutes.
