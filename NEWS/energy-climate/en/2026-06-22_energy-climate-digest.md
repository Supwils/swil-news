## I. Policy & Carbon Markets

### 1. China releases dual-track renewable "consumption + curtailment" rules, effective Aug 1 (Policy)
**Summary:** Xinhua and China's National Energy Administration reported on June 22, 2026, that the NDRC and NEA have issued the Implementation Measures for Minimum Renewable Energy Consumption Share Targets and Renewable Power Consumption Responsibility Weighting (Joint Order No. 42 of four ministries), effective August 1, 2026. The rules impose obligations at both the provincial level and among key energy-consuming industries: provinces face renewable power consumption responsibility weights (total and non-hydro, measured by physical generation consumed), while major industrial users face minimum shares for both power and non-electric renewable consumption. Non-electric consumption is included in the consumption assessment for the first time. Enterprises that miss targets must make up the gap within three months via green certificates or face talks, public notices, and credit penalties; provinces can also buy green certificates or carry deficits forward. The NEA said 2030 weight targets will be issued alongside 2026 targets to strengthen long-term guidance, and obsolete provisions such as "excess consumption credits" and "incentive indicators" have been removed.

### 4. FERC ordered six grid operators on June 18 to reform large-load rules within 60 days; June 22 coverage calls it an "interventionist" turn (Policy)
**Summary:** Claims Journal reported on June 22, 2026, that FERC Chairman said moves to accelerate AI data center grid hookups reflect a new, more interventionist regulatory posture. On June 18, under Section 206 of the Federal Power Act, FERC issued show-cause orders to PJM, MISO, SPP, CAISO, ISO-NE, and NYISO: resource adequacy reports within 30 days, and within 60 days either justify existing tariffs as "just and reasonable" or propose reforms for large loads including data centers and factories — covering faster transmission service, anti-cost-shifting protections, co-location and behind-the-meter generation, and flexible large-load service options. FERC said the action responds to a 2025 request from Energy Secretary Chris Wright, covers roughly 200 million Americans and nearly two-thirds of federally jurisdictional power demand, and requires data centers to pay their own power costs and be flexible in consumption.

### 7. Ore Energy signs 1 GWh iron-air long-duration deal with Dutch Budget Thuis on June 22 (Storage)
**Summary:** Energy-Storage.News reported on June 22, 2026, that Amsterdam-based Ore Energy and Dutch energy and telecoms supplier Budget Thuis agreed to deploy up to 1 GWh of iron-air multi-day storage, with a committed 400 MWh first phase targeted for 2028 delivery. Budget Thuis CEO Annemarie Buitelaar said the deal reduces exposure to volatile fossil fuel prices and offers customers more predictable clean power.
