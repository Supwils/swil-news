## I. Policy & Carbon Markets

### 1. U.S. SEC Moves To Withdraw Biden-Era Climate Disclosure Rules (Regulation)
**Summary:** Multiple outlets reported on **Sunday, May 31, 2026** that the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has begun proceedings to withdraw the corporate climate disclosure framework adopted in **March 2024**. The rule had required reporting on material climate risks, certain severe-weather-related costs, and how management identifies and oversees those risks. The repeal proposal is subject to roughly two months of public comment before a final decision. Meanwhile, California has introduced stricter greenhouse gas and climate financial risk disclosure requirements for large companies doing business in the state — analysts expect many multinationals to face a "federal rollback, state tightening" compliance split.

### 2. Taiwan Collects Carbon Levies From Major Emitters for First Time (Carbon Markets)
**Summary:** Nikkei Asia reported on **May 31, 2026** that Taiwan has begun charging a carbon emissions levy on large emitters under supporting rules of its Climate Change Response Act, with the first payment deadline at the end of May. The standard rate is **NT$300 per metric ton** of CO₂e; entities with approved self-determined reduction plans may qualify for preferential rates of **NT$50 or NT$100 per ton**, and high carbon-leakage-risk industries may apply emission adjustment coefficients. The Ministry of Environment expects roughly **NT$4.5 billion** (~**US$143 million**) in first-round collections, funding a greenhouse gas management fund. About **465 facilities** exceeding **25,000 tonnes** of annual emissions are in the first regulated group, based on verified **2025** emissions.

**Links:**

- [Nikkei Asia — Taiwan collects carbon levies from major emitters for first time](https://asia.nikkei.com/spotlight/environment/climate-change/taiwan-collects-carbon-levies-from-major-emitters-for-first-time)
- [Taipei Times — Carbon fee scheme needs central, local cooperation](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/taiwan/archives/2026/05/28/2003858118)

**Commentary:** Another Asia-Pacific "carbon price goes live" case study — rates are moderate and discount channels are complex; the near-term goal is building payment habits and a fund pool more than instantly reshaping industrial cost structures.

## II. Renewables & Storage

### 3. Chinese Team Reports 33% Perovskite–Silicon Tandem Efficiency, ~90% Retained After 1,000 Hours (Solar Technology)
**Summary:** Industry reports published **May 31, 2026** state that Chinese researchers achieved roughly **33%** power conversion efficiency on a perovskite–silicon tandem cell with an active area of about **one square centimeter**, retaining about **90%** of initial performance after roughly **1,000 hours** of continuous operation. A thin aluminum oxide layer over industrial silicon pyramid microstructures was used to suppress leakage without altering the underlying cell architecture. Commercial mono-silicon modules typically operate between **20% and 24%** efficiency; authors stress the result remains lab-scale, but it offers a verified durability pathway toward pushing beyond silicon's Shockley–Queisser limit.

**Commentary:** Tandem cells are competing on "how long record efficiency lasts," not just headline numbers — durability is what decides whether the lab can reach mass production.

### 4. CNNC "Ultra-Carbon No. 1" sCO₂ Waste-Heat Demo Fully Commissioned in Guizhou (Industrial Efficiency)
**Summary:** National Business Daily cited CNNC on **May 31, 2026** that the second unit of the "Ultra-Carbon No. 1" supercritical carbon dioxide (sCO₂) waste-heat power project in Liupanshui, Guizhou, has synchronized to the grid, marking full completion of the world's first demonstration of this technology class.
