### 1. EU officially launches T-MED cross-Mediterranean renewable and clean-tech initiative in Brussels (Policy)
**Summary:** On **June 9, 2026**, at the 20th European Sustainable Energy Week (EUSEW 2026, **June 9–11**) in Brussels, Mediterranean Commissioner Dubravka Šuica officially launched T-MED (Trans-Mediterranean Renewable Energy and Clean-Tech Cooperation Initiative). ### 2. EU clears **€23 billion** Italian renewable power aid scheme targeting **37.15 GW** of new capacity (Policy)
**Summary:** On **June 8, 2026**, the European Commission approved a **€23 billion** Italian state aid scheme for renewable electricity under the Clean Industrial Deal State Aid Framework (CISAF). Support will flow through **20-year** two-way contracts for difference (CfDs) for onshore wind, solar, hydropower, and sewage gas; projects above **1 MW** must compete in tenders, while smaller projects may access support directly. The scheme is expected to add **37.15 GW** — roughly **48%** of Italy's current renewable capacity — helping reach a **2030** target of **39.4%** of gross final electricity consumption from renewables. Executive Vice-President Teresa Ribera said the measure will reduce fossil-fuel import dependence.

### 3. US federal court vacates IRS notice, restoring **5%** safe harbor for wind and large-scale solar tax credits (Policy)
**Summary:** On **June 6, 2026**, the US District Court for the District of Columbia ruled that the IRS acted in an "arbitrary and capricious" manner in Notice 2025-42 by eliminating the **5%** safe harbor for wind and large solar alone, vacating the notice nationwide. Developers may again use incurred costs reaching **5%** of total project cost to establish construction start and lock in **45Y/48E** credits alongside the physical-work test. The court stressed decade-long industry reliance interests were not adequately considered; as of **June 6**, the government had not appealed. Some projects unable to meet physical-work deadlines before **July 4, 2026** may reassess the **5%** path, though appeal risk remains.

### 4. Fastmarkets launches daily CBAM certificate price assessments, filling real-time carbon-cost gap in cross-border trade (Carbon market)
**Summary:** Price reporting agency Fastmarkets announced on **June 9, 2026** (London) two daily CBAM assessments — the CBAM Certificate Index and CBAM Certificate Builder — both in **€/tCO₂e**. With the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) in its definitive phase since **January 1, 2026**, importers must purchase certificates for embedded emissions, yet official certificate prices are only confirmed after reporting periods close (quarterly in **2026**, weekly from **2027**), leaving steel, aluminium, cement, and other sectors blind to carbon costs at the border. The Index combines EUA auction and spot/forward data for forward signals; the Builder tracks volume-weighted average costs of EUAs auctioned in the current period. The firm said the Index closely anticipated final Q1 **2026** CBAM certificate prices.

**Links:**

- [Fastmarkets — Fastmarkets closes the CBAM pricing gap with daily view of carbon import costs](https://www.fastmarkets.com/insights/fastmarkets-closes-the-cbam-pricing-gap-with-daily-view-of-carbon-import-costs/)

**Commentary:** CBAM is shifting from "pay after the fact" to "price before the trade" — whoever holds daily carbon-price signals gains leverage at the negotiating table.

## II. Clean Power & Storage

### 6. California's Tumbleweed project goes live as first major US grid battery with **8-hour** continuous discharge (Storage)
**Summary:** Canary Media reported on **June 8, 2026** that Rev Renewables' Tumbleweed battery project in Kern County entered service on **June 1**, becoming the first major US installation capable of full-power discharge for **8 hours** — roughly twice typical **4-hour** projects. Adjacent to solar fields, it can push cheap daytime solar onto the grid from roughly **6 p.m. to 2 a.m.**, offering a path toward near-**24-hour** clean power in California. Born from a **2020** community-choice aggregator long-duration storage competition, Tumbleweed delivered even after state regulators pushed mandatory long-duration procurement deadlines to **2031**.

### 10. Strait of Hormuz near standstill; Brookings calls it largest oil supply disruption on record (Energy security)
**Summary:** A Brookings analysis on **June 8, 2026** states that since US-Israeli attacks on Iran began **February 28, 2026**, threats to shipping — drones, missiles, small boats — have made insurance prohibitive and crews unwilling to transit, effectively closing the Strait of Hormuz; pre-war roughly **20%** of global oil (crude ~**15 mbd**, products ~**5 mbd**) flowed through it. The IEA coordinated a historic **400 million barrel** strategic release adding roughly **2.5–3 mbd**, potentially exhausted by **July–August**; Saudi and UAE bypass pipelines run full but cannot offset Iraqi and Kuwaiti shut-ins. US gasoline averaged **$4.31/gal** and diesel **$5.35/gal** as of **June 1**; authors say little short-term policy can ease consumer pain except reopening the strait — electrification and efficiency cushion the next shock but are too slow for today's prices.
