### 2. Study: EU CBAM could raise global emissions cuts ~73% vs EU-only action (carbon border adjustment)
**Summary:** Research from the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research (PIK), reported June 1, 2026, models a $100/ton EU carbon price: without border adjustment, EU cuts ~505 Mt CO₂/year but global net cuts are only ~305 Mt as leakage offsets ~40% of EU gains. With CBAM extended in early 2026, leakage falls to ~15% and global cuts reach ~399 Mt/year; if Canada, Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan adopt domestic carbon pricing to avoid the levy, global reductions rise to ~691 Mt/year—~73% above EU-only policy. Published in the *Journal of the Association of Environmental and Resource Economists*; authors also note finance and technology-transfer gaps for developing countries.

**Links:**

- [Down To Earth — EU carbon tariff could influence climate policies beyond its borders](https://www.downtoearth.org.in/climate-change/eu-carbon-tariff-could-influence-climate-policies-beyond-its-borders-study)
- [Mirage News — EU Carbon Tariff Boosts Global Climate Policies](https://www.miragenews.com/eu-carbon-tariff-boosts-global-climate-policies-1683755/)

**Commentary:** CBAM's game has shifted from leakage prevention to a "Brussels effect"—trade rules are pushing partners to write carbon prices into domestic politics.

### 4. UN Paris Agreement Article 6.4 body adopts N₂O abatement methodology for nitric acid (carbon markets)
**Summary:** The UNFCCC reports that the Article 6.4 Supervisory Body adopted, at its May 2026 Bonn meeting, a methodology for nitrous oxide (N₂O) abatement at nitric acid plants—covering introduction, restart, or enhancement of secondary/tertiary controls. It entered force May 21, 2026, for five years. Roughly 400–600 plants produce ~70 million tonnes of nitric acid annually, much for fertilizer, with abatement uneven in developing countries. The body also adopted lock-in risk tools and revised additionality standards.

### 6. China's new refrigerator energy-efficiency standard takes effect June 1; new Tier 1 uses ~40% less power (efficiency policy)
**Summary:** Chinese media reported June 1, 2026, that GB12021.2—2025 for household refrigerators is now mandatory. New Tier 1 units consume ~40% less than the prior Tier 1; a 500 L side-by-side example drops from ~0.92 kWh/day to ~0.55 kWh/day (~135 kWh/year saved per unit; industry-wide savings potential ~13 billion kWh/year). The standard expands scope, adds volume-utilization metrics, and requires smart-grid response capability; ~20% of high-consuming models may be phased out and ~80% of former Tier 1 products may no longer qualify as Tier 1.

**Links:**
