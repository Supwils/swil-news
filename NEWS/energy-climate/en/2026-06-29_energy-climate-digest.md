### 4. California to Sue Trump Administration Over Termination of Central Coast Offshore Wind Project (Policy · US)

## II. Green Power & Storage

### 5. EU Signs First-Ever Energy Storage Tripartite Agreement, Targeting ~**45 GW** Added in **2026–2028** (Storage)
**Summary:** The European Commission signed the first EU-level energy storage tripartite agreement on **June 26, 2026** on the margins of the Luxembourg energy council; Energy-Storage.News followed up on **June 29**. The pact brings together energy ministers from **22** member states, storage developers and manufacturers, renewable developers, energy-consuming industries and financial institutions, aiming to accelerate roughly **45 GW** of new storage in **2026–2028** (member-state pledges total about **30–35 GW** so far)—at least **20%** faster annual deployment than the roughly **12 GW** installed in **2025**—toward an estimated **200 GW** total need by **2030** (about **55 GW** installed at the start of **2026**). Targets include raising storage's share of peak demand from about **5%** in **2025** to **10%**; storage-tied PPAs from **1.5 GW** to **4.5 GW**; commercial and industrial battery storage from **9 GWh** to **24 GWh**; and co-located storage from **5%** to **20%** of renewable installations.

**Links:**

- [European Commission — Tripartite agreement for energy storage](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/publications/tripartite-agreement-energy-storage_en)
- [Energy-Storage.News — EU tripartite agreement targets 30-35GW of storage deployments by 2028](https://www.energy-storage.news/eu-tripartite-agreement-targets-30-35gw-of-storage-deployments-by-2028/)

**Commentary:** Once renewable capacity is largely planned, the bottleneck is storing and dispatching it—this tripartite pact elevates storage from project level to a systemic tool shared by ministers and banks.

### 6. China's First Northeast CSP Plant and Highest-Latitude Solar Thermal Station Commissions on June 29 (Green Power)
**Summary:** China Economic Net, citing CCTV News, reported on **June 29, 2026** that CGN's Jixi Base **100 MW** concentrated solar power plant in Da'an, Jilin Province, entered commercial operation—the first CSP plant in Northeast China and the highest-latitude CSP project nationwide (latitude **45.36°N**). The plant offers **8 hours** of thermal storage and **24-hour** stable continuous operation; the site sees winter lows of **-37.3°C**, winds up to force **9**, and saline-alkali geology. The build team developed a standardized solution replicable in high-latitude cold regions. The project is expected to generate **180 million kWh** annually, saving about **54,000 tonnes** of standard coal equivalent and cutting CO₂ emissions by roughly **139,000 tonnes**.

**Links:**

- [China Economic Net — Northeast China's first CSP plant, China's highest-latitude solar thermal station, enters operation](http://www.ce.cn/xwzx/gnsz/gdxw/202606/t20260629_3058633.shtml)

**Commentary:** CSP is moving beyond northwest pilot zones into the frigid northeast—dispatchable night-time solar is filling the time-gap that PV alone cannot cover at high latitudes.

**Summary:** The BBC and The Economic Times reported on **June 29, 2026** that WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus said on social media that more than **1,300** excess deaths linked to high temperatures have been recorded in Europe since **June 21**; he warned about **150 million** people are living under extreme heat—"hundreds have died, schools are shut, grids are buckling"—with Europe warming at roughly twice the global average and "once-in-a-generation" heatwaves now occurring nearly every year. World Weather Attribution analysis called this June event the most severe heatwave recorded over the studied region; comparable June extremes would have been "virtually impossible" in **1976** without human-caused climate change.

### 11. WEF Energy Transition Index: Global Transition "Fragmenting," Readiness Scores Turn Down for First Time in Over a Decade (Transition)
**Summary:** The World Economic Forum's **June 2026** Energy Transition Index and related energy roundup finds the global transition is not reversing but becoming "more fragmented and uneven": average scores are broadly flat and momentum has weakened. On transition readiness—which signals where the transition is heading—finance and investment, regulation and political commitment, and innovation have turned downward together for the first time in over a decade, widening the gap between renewable build-out and what systems can absorb. The report lists three priority areas and notes Hormuz Strait conflict and other geopolitical risks are reshaping oil and gas markets, with Gulf exporters discussing pipeline bypass options; energy shocks remind policymakers that imported fossil dependence creates vulnerability while domestic renewables and local supply build resilience.

**Links:**

- [World Economic Forum — Global energy transition is fragmenting, and more energy stories](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/06/energy-transition-index-shows-fragmenting-and-more-top-energy-stories/)

**Commentary:** The ETI alarm is not reversal but fracture—capacity curves still rise, but finance, rules and political will are loosening at once, and systems cannot absorb new clean power fast enough.

Authors say most firms are planning fossil fuel production growth without credible transition pathways backing emission targets.
