## II. U.S. Politics and Economy

### 5. FISA Section 702 Statutory Authority Expires at Midnight June 12 After Congress Fails to Extend
**Summary:** According to TechCrunch, ABC News, and the Austin American-Statesman on June 12, Congress on June 11 failed to pass a short-term extension of Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA): the House rejected a three-week extension 198–218, and three Senate unanimous-consent extension efforts also failed; Congress then recessed until June 23. Section 702 authorizes collection of foreign-target intelligence from electronic communications providers and was set to expire at midnight on Friday, June 12 — the first time the statutory authority faces lapse due to congressional inaction. The impasse centers on Trump's appointment of Federal Housing Finance Agency Director Bill Pulte as acting director of national intelligence; Democrats refuse to extend surveillance powers while he holds the role. Notably, the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court recertified the program in March 2026, and existing court orders may allow operations to continue until approximately March 2027, but statutory expiration still triggers legal and political controversy.

### 7. Senate Defense Bill Would Permit New Robotic and Autonomous Systems Combatant Command
**Summary:** According to Defense One, DefenseScoop, and Air & Space Forces on June 11–12, a summary of the Senate Armed Services Committee's fiscal 2027 National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA), worth approximately $1.14 trillion, would "permit" the Defense Department to establish a new four-star Robotic and Autonomous Systems Combatant Command — the first potential new combatant command since U.S. Space Command was reestablished in 2019. Committee staff said the move was inspired in part by Ukraine's drone-focused military service, aiming to accelerate unmanned systems acquisition, testing, and cross-service integration; the command would have limited acquisition authority and special test-and-evaluation powers. The provision is permissive rather than mandatory and must be reconciled with the House version and passed by both chambers. The committee version passed 18–9; full bill text has not yet been released.

## III. China Policy and Economy

### 8. PBOC Releases May Financial Data: M2 Up 8.6% YoY, Total Social Financing Growth at 7.7%
**Summary:** According to the People's Bank of China, China Finance Information Network, and Securities Times on June 12, the central bank released May 2026 financial statistics. At end-May, broad money (M2) stood at 353.67 trillion yuan, up 8.6% year-on-year; narrow money (M1) was 114.89 trillion yuan, up 5.5%. New total social financing in May was 2.03 trillion yuan, 260.7 billion less than a year earlier; outstanding social financing was 458.81 trillion yuan, growing 7.7%. Financial institutions added 520 billion yuan in new yuan loans in May, 100 billion less year-on-year; outstanding loans were 281.02 trillion yuan, growing 5.5%. Social financing increments for the first five months totaled 17.48 trillion yuan, 1.16 trillion less than the same period last year. Structurally, household medium- and long-term loans remained weak, bill financing showed notable front-loading, and corporate medium- and long-term loans fell 350 billion yuan year-on-year, indicating still-fragile effective credit demand.
