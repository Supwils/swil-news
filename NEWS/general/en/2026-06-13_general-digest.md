## II. U.S. Politics and Economy

### 5. FISA Section 702 Authority Expires at Midnight June 13 — First Lapse in the Program's History
**Summary:** According to the Christian Science Monitor, Washington Examiner, and NBC News around June 13, after Congress failed on June 11–12 to pass a short-term extension of Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act before adjourning, the authority expired at 12:01 a.m. on Saturday, June 13 — the first lapse since the provision was enacted in 2008. The impasse centers on Democratic opposition to President Trump's appointment of Federal Housing Finance Agency head Bill Pulte as acting director of national intelligence; Trump nominated former SEC Chair Jay Clayton as permanent director on June 11, but Democrats still demand a guarantee Pulte will not serve in an interim role. The Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court recertified the program for one year in March 2026, and existing court orders may sustain operations until roughly March 2027; however, statutory expiration raises legal and political questions over telecom compliance, new certifications, and carrier liability without statutory indemnification.

**Links:**

### 6. Trump Signs Order Opening Over 500,000 Square Miles of Pacific Marine Monuments to Commercial Fishing
**Summary:** According to Eastern Herald and Singju Post (White House event transcript) on June 11–13, President Trump signed a proclamation/executive order at the White House on June 11 lifting commercial-fishing prohibitions on three marine national monuments — Papahānaumokuākea off Hawaii, the Mariana Trench off Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands, and Rose Atoll off American Samoa — covering more than 500,000 square miles of Pacific Ocean. Trump said the move aims to lower seafood prices, revitalize U.S. fisheries, and generate income for coastal communities; the protections were originally established by the George W. Bush and Barack Obama administrations and reaffirmed by subsequent presidents. Reports note this is the third major Pacific monument rollback of Trump's second term, following openings of roughly 400,000 square miles of the Pacific Islands Heritage Marine National Monument and the Northeast Canyons and Seamounts Marine National Monument; roughly 900,000 square miles of U.S. protected ocean has been reopened since he took office.
