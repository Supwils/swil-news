### 1. U.S. naval blockade devastates Iranian oil exports: May shipments may fall below 300,000 bpd; ~$6bn revenue loss
**Summary:** Per Al Jazeera on June 5, amid the U.S. port blockade launched April 13 and continued Hormuz tensions, Iran's May crude and condensate exports hit at least a six-year low. Trade-intelligence firm Kpler data show daily exports plunging from nearly 2 million barrels per day to below 300,000 bpd versus a 40-day pre-blockade average; Hormuz-linked oil accounts for roughly 80% of Iran's exports, with China—the top buyer—especially affected. The report estimates nearly $6 billion in lost oil revenue for Tehran, though elevated global energy prices also impose costs on Washington. Iran condemns the blockade as "piracy"; the Trump administration treats it as leverage to force peace terms. Talks continue, but strait transit has not resumed.

### 5. Senate passes $70 billion ICE and Border Patrol funding 52–47 in pre-dawn vote
**Summary:** Per WKBW and The Yeshiva World on June 5, the Senate approved roughly $70 billion in early-morning voting (around 5 a.m.) to fund Immigration and Customs Enforcement and the Border Patrol for three years—through the remainder of Trump's term—on a near party-line 52–47 vote. ### 6. Trump slams House war powers vote as "meaningless" and "unpatriotic"
**Summary:** Per BBC, France 24, and KPBS on June 3–4, the House voted 215–208 on June 3 to pass war powers resolution H.Con.Res. 86 directing an end to U.S. hostilities with Iran, with four Republicans—Tom Barrett, Warren Davidson, Brian Fitzpatrick, and Thomas Massie—joining Democrats. Trump posted June 4 on Truth Social calling it a "meaningless vote" that limits his war powers "right in the middle of my final negotiations," labeling the four Republicans "GRANDSTANDERS." As a concurrent resolution, an identical Senate passage would not require presidential signature—but the administration questions the War Powers Act's constitutionality; Trump would likely veto any joint-resolution version, and Congress lacks two-thirds to override. This was the House's fourth attempt to curb Iran war powers—and the first to pass under Republican control.
