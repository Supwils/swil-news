### 1. U.S. and Iran Announce MOU; Formal Signing Set for June 19 in Switzerland, Hormuz to Reopen
**Summary:** According to Geo TV, NBC News, and The New Indian Express on June 15, the United States and Iran announced a memorandum of understanding (MOU) to end their war, mediated by Pakistan. Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif posted on X that both sides declared the "immediate and permanent" termination of military operations on all fronts, including Lebanon, with an official signing ceremony scheduled for June 19 in Switzerland. Iranian Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi confirmed on television that the deal puts an "immediate end" to the war and that talks on a "final agreement" would continue within two months; nuclear issues remain for a later phase. Oil prices plunged more than 4% at the Tokyo open, and the Nikkei rose about 3%. Analysts note the draft contains roughly 14 points covering ceasefires and maritime access, but core issues — nuclear program, sanctions relief — await phase two, and Israel is not a party to the accord.
