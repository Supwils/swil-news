### 5. ECB Raises Rates 25 Basis Points to 2.25%, First Hike in Three Years on Middle East Energy Shock
**Summary:** According to the ECB and CNBC on June 11, the Governing Council unanimously decided to raise all three key interest rates by 25 basis points: the deposit facility rate to 2.25%, the main refinancing operations rate to 2.40%, and the marginal lending facility rate to 2.65%, effective June 17. President Christine Lagarde said the Middle East war is generating inflation pressures, with energy price inflation at 10.9% and broadening across the euro area economy; the hike is robust across multiple shock scenarios. Eurozone headline inflation was 3.2% and core inflation 2.5% in May. CNBC noted this is the ECB's first rate hike since 2023 and the first among major central banks in response to the Iran war energy shock; Lagarde stressed no pre-commitment to a future rate path, raised the inflation forecast, and downgraded the growth outlook.

## II. U.S. Politics and Economy

### 6. Congress Fails to Extend FISA Section 702; U.S. Foreign Surveillance Authority Set for Historic Lapse
**Summary:** According to ABC News and Roll Call on June 11, the House rejected a three-week extension of Section 702 of the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (FISA) by a 198–218 vote, falling short of the two-thirds majority required; 19 Republicans and some privacy advocates voted against it, while seven Democrats supported it. The Senate also failed to pass an extension. Section 702, which authorizes U.S. collection of intelligence on overseas targets from electronic communication service providers, is set to expire on Friday, June 12; Congress is expected to recess until June 23, meaning the authority could lapse for at least a week for the first time in its history. The impasse centers on President Trump's appointment of Federal Housing Finance Agency head Bill Pulte as acting Director of National Intelligence; Democrats refuse to extend surveillance powers while he is in place, citing his lack of national security, law enforcement, or military experience. House Minority Leader Hakeem Jeffries called Thursday's vote a Republican "show vote" they knew would fail.
