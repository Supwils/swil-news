## I. Global Headlines

### 1. Venezuela earthquake death toll rises to 920; UN says more than 50,000 missing
**Summary:** According to France 24, the BBC, and other reports on June 26, casualties continued to climb after back-to-back magnitude 7.2 and 7.5 earthquakes struck northwestern Venezuela on the evening of June 24. National Assembly head Jorge Rodriguez said on state television that the death toll had reached 920, with at least 3,360 injured and 172 people still trapped; acting President Delcy Rodriguez had earlier in the day put the toll at 589 dead and 2,980 injured, with official figures revised upward several times within hours. The UN aid chief said more than 50,000 people were missing; independent missing-persons sites also registered figures in the tens of thousands. La Guaira state was hardest hit and placed under military control; major ports and power plants were damaged by outages, and Simon Bolivar International Airport remained closed. The United States deployed warships and transport aircraft and pledged $150 million in aid, partially easing sanctions through October 23 to facilitate relief transactions; India, Portugal, Spain, and others sent search teams and supplies. The oil minister said national crude output stood at about 1.2 million bpd and that fuel distribution was guaranteed.

## IV. U.S.–China Relations

### 10. Both sides agree to establish trade council; further talks on reciprocal tariff cuts ahead
**Summary:** According to Xinhua, People's Daily Online, and Asia News Network on June 26, Commerce Ministry spokesperson He Yadong said at a June 25 briefing (published June 26) that China and the United States have agreed to establish a trade council under which the two sides' economic and trade teams will discuss cooperation including reciprocal tariff reductions, with further consultations to follow. Both sides have agreed in principle to discuss a reciprocal tariff-reduction framework covering no less than $30 billion in goods on each side; products of mutual concern are expected to receive most-favored-nation rates or lower. He said China–U.S. cooperation in aircraft and agricultural products is mutually beneficial and that both teams will continue encouraging enterprises to strengthen linkages and expand trade in relevant fields. The mechanism continues the U.S.–China Board of Trade framework agreed during President Trump's May visit; the U.S. Trade Representative is accepting public comments on specific product lists through July 10.
