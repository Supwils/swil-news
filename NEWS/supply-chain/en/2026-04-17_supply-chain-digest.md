### 2. TSMC Warns on Helium Supply; Specialty Chemicals Diversification Underway
**Summary:** TSMC management specifically flagged helium supply as a supply chain concern on the Q1 earnings call, noting that approximately 27–30% of global helium production capacity was offline due to operational issues at key US and Russian source fields. Specialty chemicals for semiconductor manufacturing were also cited as an area where TSMC was actively diversifying its sourcing. Helium is used in semiconductor manufacturing for cooling, etching, and inert gas blanket applications, and its supply concentration creates geopolitical exposure.
**Links:**
- [Semiconductor Engineering — TSMC helium supply risk](https://semiengineering.com/)
**Commentary:** TSMC's helium warning is the most operationally significant supply chain disclosure from the earnings call, even though it received less media attention than the profit numbers. Korean DRAM and HBM manufacturers are similarly exposed to helium price spikes given the concentration of semiconductor-grade helium supply infrastructure.

### 6. Canada Mangrove Lithium: First Commercial Electrochemical Lithium Refinery (BC Delta; ~$21.9M CAD Government Support)
**Summary:** Canada's Mangrove Lithium announced the commissioning of the first commercial electrochemical lithium refinery at its BC Delta facility, with approximately $21.9 million CAD in government support. Electrochemical lithium refining uses electricity rather than evaporation ponds or hydrometallurgical acid processes, producing battery-grade lithium hydroxide at higher purity and with lower water and chemical consumption. The facility positioned Canada as a pioneer in next-generation lithium processing chemistry.
**Links:**
- [Mangrove Lithium — BC Delta refinery](https://www.mangroveli.com/)
- [Natural Resources Canada — Clean technology support](https://www.nrcan.gc.ca/)
**Commentary:** Electrochemical lithium refining's key advantage is geographic flexibility — unlike evaporation-based processing, it doesn't require solar irradiance or vast water resources, enabling refinery siting near mining sources or consumers rather than in climate-specific locations. Commercial-scale validation is the necessary step before the technology can attract mainstream capital.
