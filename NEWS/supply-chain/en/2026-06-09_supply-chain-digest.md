### 2. Section 232 metal tariff adjustments now in effect: farm/HVAC cut to 15%, U.S. metal content threshold lowered to 85%
**Summary:** Per the White House **June 1** proclamation and analyses from C.H. Robinson and Holland & Knight, adjusted Section 232 tariffs took effect at **12:01 a.m. ET on June 8**, running through **December 31, 2027**. Key changes include Annex I-C tiered rates for mobile industrial equipment, agricultural machinery, and residential HVAC (generally **25%**, **15%** cap for framework economies, **10%** for goods with **≥85%** U.S. smelted/cast metal content by weight); steel racks and aluminum lithographic plates newly subject to **25%** derivative duties; and the "entirely U.S. metal" threshold relaxed from **95%** to **85%**. CBP issued ACE filing guidance on **June 5** (CSMS #68855869). Importers must submit metal origin and content certifications at entry—not just apply headline rates.

**Links:**

- [The White House — Further Adjusting the Tariff Regimes for Aluminum, Steel, and Copper (June 2026)](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/further-adjusting-the-tariff-regimes-for-imports-of-aluminum-steel-and-copper-into-the-united-states/)

**Commentary:** Section 232 compliance has entered a three-dimensional matrix of product annexes, country of origin, and metal content—importers who have not updated HTS classification and origin documentation this week face sudden duty spikes and re-entry risk.

## II. Semiconductors & Critical Materials

### 4. Helium "June test" enters operational phase: Qatar force majeure window nears, fab rationing may intensify
**Summary:** Per Startup Fortune and Tantalum Strategy analysis, Qatar accounts for roughly **30%** of global helium production; after Ras Laffan facility damage in **March**, early inventory buffers are largely exhausted. Reuters reported in late March that Korean chipmakers had helium stocks lasting "at least until June"—and **June has arrived**, testing whether U.S. and other replacement supply can arrive in time at required purity. Edison disclosed on **May 25** that QatarEnergy's force majeure may extend to mid-**August** (three months beyond the mid-**June** window announced **May 4**); spot prices have risen from roughly **$500/thousand cubic feet** pre-crisis to the **$1,000–1,200** band. S&P Global sees risk shifting from a short-term logistics problem to a possible structural constraint in **H2 2026**; Samsung and SK hynix are prioritizing scarce helium for AI memory and HBM lines.
