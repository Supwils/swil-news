## I. Semiconductors & Critical Materials

### 1. Micron Signs **16** Strategic Customer Agreements, Locking High-Margin Supply Through **2026–2030**
**Summary:** Per The Register and Channel NewsAsia on **June 25**, Micron Technology disclosed on its **FY2026 Q3** earnings call that it has signed **16** Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs), with **14** carrying roughly **$100 billion** in minimum revenue commitments at floor pricing, plus **$22 billion** in customer cash deposits and related financial commitments (about **$18 billion** in cash). Most agreements run five years from **2026 to 2030** as take-or-pay contracts with floor-and-ceiling pricing bands—the floor guarantees gross margins above any prior cycle peak, while the ceiling is set near **calendar Q2 2026** market prices to cap upside for committed buyers. CEO Sanjay Mehrotra said SCAs will eventually cover about **40%** of revenue; customers including Nvidia increasingly treat Micron as a strategic partner whose fab expansions they must co-fund, not a commodity vendor to play off rivals. Even as industry supply improves gradually in **2028**, memory shortages are unlikely to close quickly. Q3 revenue hit **$41.5 billion** (+**346%** YoY), net income **$28.9 billion**, gross margin **84.9%**; Q4 guidance calls for **$50 billion** revenue and ~**86%** gross margin.

## II. Capacity & Relocation

### 2. Michelin to Close Tuscaloosa BFGoodrich Tire Plant by **End-2028**, Affecting ~**1,200** Workers
**Summary:** Per ABC 3340 on **June 25**, Michelin North America announced a U.S. BFGoodrich manufacturing network reorganization, consolidating nearly all production at its Fort Wayne, Indiana, facility while gradually winding down its Tuscaloosa, Alabama, plant from early **2026** through year-end **2028**. The West Alabama Chamber of Commerce says roughly **1,200** employees will be impacted; Michelin says no immediate separations are planned as transition details are developed. The plant briefly shut down after the announcement for leadership-employee discussions; production is expected to resume **June 29** and continue during the multi-year wind-down. The move concentrates North American tire capacity while marking one of the region's largest recent industrial workforce reductions.

## III. Policy & Geopolitics

### 7. China's MOFCOM Issues and Implements Supply Chain Security Investigation Rules
**Summary:** Per Caixin Global and InKunming (Xinhua) on **June 25**, China's Ministry of Commerce published and put into effect **22-article** rules on industrial and supply chain security investigations, providing operational detail for the State Council's **April 2026** Regulations on Industrial and Supply Chain Security. The rules clarify scope, content, procedures, and remedial actions; MOFCOM may investigate foreign countries, regions, or international organizations that violate international law, impose discriminatory restrictive measures against China, or threaten the security of China's industrial and supply chains. Prior legal analysis from Sidley Austin notes the framework enables monitoring and countermeasures against conduct perceived to threaten supply chain security, with likely focus on strategic technologies and critical industrial inputs including integrated circuits, machine tools, high-end equipment, basic software, advanced materials, and biomanufacturing.

## IV. Logistics & Trade

### 9. Manila NAIA Air Cargo Congestion Forces Global Tech Production Halt; Customs Opens Duty Free Satellite Facility
**Summary:** Per PortCalls, Manila Bulletin, and LogisticsNews.ph on **June 25**, warehouse congestion at Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) is disrupting the Philippines' semiconductor and electronics industry—wafers, storage devices, and other air-only components face sharply slower clearance, and at least one global technology company has suspended a product line. PCCI President and EMS Group CEO Ferdinand Ferrer said clearance within three days fell from **95%** last year to about **76%** this year; electronics are the country's largest trade category, with nearly **$50 billion** in exports in **2025**. BOC Assistant Commissioner Vincent Philip Maronilla announced at a **June 25** summit that Duty Free space will serve as a satellite processing facility with dedicated customs staff to ease Paircargo congestion, though **2,000 sq m** of capacity cannot cover all cargo volume.
