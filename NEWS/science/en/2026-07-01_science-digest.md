## I. Space & Deep Space

### 1. NASA Swift Orbit-Boost Mission Enters July 1 Launch Window After Weather Scrub
**Summary:** Per NASA Science, Eos, and SatNews (June 30–July 1, 2026), unfavorable weather at Kwajalein Atoll scrubbed the June 30 launch of Katalyst Space's LINK robotic servicing spacecraft aboard a Northrop Grumman Pegasus XL air-launched rocket. The next attempt is no earlier than Wednesday, July 1, at 9:43 p.m. UTC+12 (5:43 a.m. EDT). Under NASA's roughly $30 million contract, LINK aims to capture and slowly raise the 22-year-old Neil Gehrels Swift gamma-ray observatory, whose orbit is decaying faster due to increased atmospheric drag from recent solar activity; without intervention, re-entry could occur in 2026. LINK is about 1.5 m tall and 399 kg, with three ion thrusters and robotic arms. After launch, weeks of commissioning and roughly a month of approach precede a multi-month boost from ~360 km to a stable ~600 km altitude. Stargazer and Pegasus XL arrived at Kwajalein on June 18.

- [Eos — NASA Rushes to Buoy a Swiftly Sinking Telescope](https://eos.org/research-and-developments/nasa-rushes-to-buoy-a-swiftly-sinking-telescope)

**Commentary:** The July 1 window is a dual bet—save a flagship telescope and prove commercial on-orbit servicing—where a nine-month contract-to-launch timeline will define feasibility for aging LEO assets.

## II. Fundamental Research & Theoretical Physics

### 2. Nobel Laureate Parisi and Claude "Collaborate" to Prove Decade-Old Jamming Conjecture
**Summary:** Per phys.org (July 1, 2026), Giorgio Parisi (2021 Nobel Physics laureate) and Francesco Zamponi (Sapienza University of Rome) published in the *Journal of Statistical Mechanics: Theory and Experiment* (arXiv: 10.48550/arxiv.2606.03300) a rigorous proof that critical exponents in jamming theory satisfy a+b=1. The relation, numerically evident since Parisi's 2014 work, had resisted proof for a decade and mirrors conclusions from Matthieu Wyart's independent jamming framework (EPFL). Parisi first had Anthropic's Claude reproduce legacy numerical results, then asked whether it could prove why a+b=1; the model supplied a largely correct proof sketch refined over several author rounds. The authors note the answer was simpler than expected—"right there, and we simply hadn't seen it."
**Links:**
- [phys.org — Physicists and AI model Claude 'collaborate' to prove a 10-year-old jamming conjecture](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-physicists-ai-claude-collaborate-year.html)
- [arXiv — A proof of an identity for the critical exponents of jamming](https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2606.03300)

**Commentary:** Crossing from "computed" to "proved" via human–AI collaboration unifies two jamming formalisms and offers a workflow template for complex-systems math where numerics long outpaced rigor.

### 3. *Nature Communications*: Riemann Hypothesis Linked to Dynamical Quantum Phase Transitions
**Summary:** Per a *Nature Communications* paper published July 1, 2026 (Wei et al., DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-74935-8), researchers established a direct correspondence between nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function and dynamical quantum phase transitions (DQPTs) in two complementary engineered quantum many-body systems—one characterized by average accumulated phase factor, the other by Loschmidt amplitude. The mapping recasts the Riemann Hypothesis as phase transitions at a unique temperature and identifies a previously unknown transition mechanism. The team demonstrated the correspondence in a proof-of-principle quantum-processor experiment and proposed a polynomial-resource quantum computational framework, suggesting quantum advantage in probing the hypothesis.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — The Riemann Hypothesis manifested in dynamical quantum phase transitions](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-74935-8)

**Commentary:** Embedding a millennium problem in testable quantum dynamics expands the "physics verifies mathematics" toolkit—regardless of how close this route comes to a full RH proof.

### 4. MIT-Led Team Reports Multiple Magnetic-Field-Enhanced Unconventional Superconducting States in Rhombohedral Graphene
**Summary:** Per Graphene-Info (July 1, 2026), researchers at MIT, the University of Basel, Florida State University, the University of Florida, and Japan's National Institute for Materials Science observed multiple superconducting states in rhombohedral tetra- and pentalayer graphene as a function of carrier density. In pentalayer devices, three phases persist or strengthen under in-plane fields up to ~8.5–9 T—far beyond the conventional Pauli limit where BCS superconductivity is usually suppressed. Some states are stabilized via proximitized spin–orbit coupling. Authors position rhombohedral graphene as a highly tunable clean-limit platform toward topological superconductivity and non-Abelian quasiparticles.
**Links:**
- [Graphene-Info — Rhombohedral graphene hosts multiple magnetic-field-boosted superconducting states](https://www.graphene-info.com/researchers-find-rhombohedral-graphene-can-host-multiple-magnetic-field-boosted)

**Commentary:** Field-enhanced superconductivity appearing systematically in carbon 2D materials could make rhombohedral graphene a crystalline testbed for Majorana physics without heavy-fermion platforms.

## III. Biomedicine & Health

### 5. *Nature*: McMaster Dual-Target GPNMB CAR-T Attacks Glioblastoma and Pro-Tumor Macrophages
**Summary:** Per News-Medical (July 1, 2026), Sheila Singh's team at McMaster University reports in *Nature* a CAR-T strategy targeting GPNMB (Glycoprotein non-metastatic melanoma protein B) on glioblastoma (GBM) cells and tumor-supporting macrophages hijacked by the tumor microenvironment, dismantling both cancer and its immune shield. In multiple preclinical GBM models including patient-derived tumors, therapy eliminated detectable disease and yielded long-term disease-free survival. A University of Calgary team simultaneously published a first-in-human GPNMB CAR-T (GCAR1) trial in *Nature Cancer*; GCAR1 Phase I (NCT07297667) began recruiting in Canada on June 30, 2026. Collaborators include King's College London, Northwestern, the University of Toronto, and SickKids; funders include the Terry Fox Research Institute and Brain Canada.
**Links:**
- [News-Medical — Dual-target CAR-T therapy shows promise against aggressive brain tumors](https://www.news-medical.net/news/20260701/Dual-target-CAR-T-therapy-shows-promise-against-aggressive-brain-tumors.aspx)
- [ClinicalTrials.gov — GCAR1 Phase I (NCT07297667)](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07297667)

**Commentary:** GBM immunotherapy shifts from "kill cancer cells only" to dismantling the tumor–immune ecosystem—GPNMB evidence spanning brain tumors and sarcoma offers a shared target logic beyond blood cancers.

### 6. Orca Bio's TREGZI™ Wins FDA Approval for Precision T-Cell Therapy in Allogeneic Transplant
**Summary:** Per Business Wire (June 30, 2026) and OncLive, the FDA approved Orca Bio's TREGZI™ (allogeneic regulatory T cell immunotherapy with HSPC and T cells-vldq, formerly Orca-T®) for matched-donor hematopoietic stem cell transplantation with myeloablative conditioning to reconstitute hematopoiesis/immunity and improve chronic graft-versus-host disease (cGVHD)-free survival (cGFS) in adults with hematological malignancies. In Phase 3 Precision-T (NCT05316701, n=187), median 12-month cGFS was not estimable with TREGZI versus 7.3 months with unmanipulated graft plus tacrolimus/methotrexate (HR 0.26, P<0.00001); moderate-to-severe cGVHD 12-month cumulative incidence was 12.6% vs 44.0%. The company cites a wholesale acquisition cost of ~$428,000 and expects ordering availability by late July.

### 8. *Nature*: Germline-Targeting HIV Vaccine Reproducibly Elicits Broadly Neutralizing Antibodies in Primates
**Summary:** Per *Nature* (published June 30, 2026; Steichen et al., DOI: 10.1038/s41586-026-10837-5), William R. Schief's team at Scripps Research with IAVI, Emory, and partners tested an adjuvanted protein germline-targeting vaccine in outbred non-transgenic nonhuman primates. ≥50% of animals generated bnAb lineages with up to 67% neutralization breadth versus a reference bnAb; 44% developed serum bnAb activity, with the strongest titers reaching levels expected to confer protection. Induced antibodies structurally mimicked known human bnAb–HIV Env interactions, demonstrating reproducible elicitation of prespecified bnAb classes to prespecified epitopes under endogenous conditions.
**Links:**
- [Nature — Vaccination elicits HIV broadly neutralizing antibodies in primates](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10837-5)

**Commentary:** The closest proof-of-principle yet in a four-decade HIV vaccine quest—crossing from transgenic mice to conventional macaques warrants accelerated preclinical-to-clinical investment in germline-targeting.

## IV. Climate, Environment & Energy

### 10. *Nature Communications*: Svalbard–Barents Ice-Sheet Instability Pulsed Reactive Iron to the Open Ocean
**Summary:** Per *Nature Communications* (July 1, 2026; Tessin et al., DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-75133-2), sediment records from the Yermak Plateau near the Svalbard–Barents Ice Sheet show at least five pulses of reactive iron export over the past 135 ka during ice-sheet advance or retreat. Iron-rich layers preserve high reactive-Fe phases, some likely bioavailable at deposition. Iron may have been delivered via meltwater plumes or remobilization of fjord/shelf sediments, reaching hundreds of kilometers offshore. Results imply modern ice-sheet instability could perturb high-latitude iron cycles and nutrient–carbon burial coupling.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — Svalbard-barents ice sheet instability enhanced delivery of reactive iron to the ocean](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-75133-2)

**Commentary:** Glaciers do more than add freshwater—iron pulses tie ice-sheet dynamics to open-ocean primary productivity, filling a gap in forecasting ecological consequences of Arctic retreat.

### 11. Cambridge Team Demonstrates Square-Meter Outdoor Solar Reactor Converting Plastic Waste to Hydrogen
**Summary:** Per ScienceNet.cn / Science and Technology Daily (July 1, 2026) and a University of Cambridge release, Erwin Reisner's team operated a ~1 m² photocatalytic panel outdoors under natural sunlight outside the Chemistry Department, photoreforming cellulose and real PET bottles into hydrogen plus formate and acetate. The work appears in *Nature Chemical Engineering* (Annuar et al., DOI: 10.1038/s44286-026-00406-y, online June 24, 2026). The device uses Al-doped SrTiO₃ absorbers with single-source-precursor Co–Zr co-catalyst coatings applied by room-temperature spray assembly. A 6-hour outdoor run at ~0.5–0.6 suns yielded ~5.24 mmol/m² H₂ from glucose substrate. Techno-economic analysis accompanies the demo; durability and efficiency must improve before commercialization.
**Links:**
- [ScienceNet.cn — Solar reactor converts plastic waste to fuel outdoors](https://news.sciencenet.cn/htmlnews/2026/7/567467.htm)
- [University of Cambridge — Solar-powered plastic recycling at real-world scale](https://www.cam.ac.uk/research/news/scientists-demonstrate-solar-powered-plastic-recycling-at-real-world-scale)

**Commentary:** Scaling from 25 cm² lab devices to 1 m² outdoor panels—if durability hurdles clear, dual-product photocatalytic "waste-to-hydrogen" could become a low-infrastructure distributed energy option.
