## II. Plasma physics & fusion energy

### 3. **EAST** metal-wall tokamak demonstrates the integrated **DTP** regime: **partial divertor detachment + ELM-free H-mode + improved pedestal performance** (fusion engineering)
**Summary:** An English-language news item from Hefei Institutes of Physical Science, CAS (**Mar 31, 2026**) describes work led by **Guosheng Xu** at the Institute of Plasma Physics, reporting the first minute-scale demonstration in a metal-wall environment that simultaneously achieves **partial divertor detachment**, an **edge-localized-mode (ELM)-free** high-confinement mode, and **enhanced pedestal performance** on **EAST**. The team names the scenario the **Detached divertor and Turbulence-dominated Pedestal (DTP)** regime and emphasizes real-time control of light impurity seeding, with pedestal temperature-gradient-driven turbulence identified as a transport channel that limits pedestal pressure buildup and sustains ELM-free operation. Crossref metadata lists the associated **Physical Review Letters** article as published online **Mar 23, 2026**, **Phys. Rev. Lett. 136 (12)**, article **125101** (registered DOI below).
**Links:**
- [HFIPS / CAS — Researchers Demonstrate New Integrated Plasma Regime (2026-03-31)](http://english.hf.cas.cn/nr/rn/202603/t20260331_1154298.html)
- [DOI.org — Turbulence-Driven Edge-Localized-Mode-Free High-Confinement Mode with Divertor Detachment in a Metal-Wall Tokamak (Phys. Rev. Lett.)](https://doi.org/10.1103/7r3f-dqft)
**Commentary:** The engineering takeaway is to treat divertor heat-load management and edge stability less as a zero-sum trade and more as a jointly optimizable operating window—directly relevant to long-pulse, high-performance scenarios for **ITER** and beyond.

## III. Biomedicine & clinical translation

### 4. **BBB/LRP1**-targeted “supramolecular nanomedicines” clear **Aβ** quickly and improve cognition in Alzheimer’s mouse models (preclinical neurovascular medicine)
**Summary:** A **ScienceDaily** release dated **May 17, 2026** (materials attributed to **IBEC**) summarizes international work led by **IBEC** and **West China Hospital, Sichuan University** (with UK collaborators) on bioactive nanoparticles that act as therapeutics themselves—tuning **LRP1** trafficking at the blood–brain barrier to restore clearance of **amyloid-β (Aβ)** rather than only ferrying small-molecule cargo. ## VI. Chemistry, soft matter & molecular design

### 8. **Cornell**: a “loop-then-bind” conformational-entropy mechanism can accelerate and strengthen molecular pairing—lessons for drugs and nanoparticle assembly (soft matter | statistical mechanics)
**Summary:** A **Cornell Chronicle** story highlights modeling-led insights from **Fernando Escobedo**’s group (with experimental coauthors) on sequence-programmable **oligocarbamates**: one partner can pre-form an intramolecular loop that opens upon binding, increasing accessible conformations and yielding a **positive entropy change** upon association—counterintuitive relative to naive “binding always loses translational entropy” heuristics. The underlying study is published in *PNAS* (DOI **10.1073/pnas.2534579123**), with Cornell noting a **May 5, 2026** publication date.
**Links:**
- [Cornell Chronicle — Entropy gives a ‘marriage’ of molecules just enough freedom](https://news.cornell.edu/stories/2026/05/entropy-gives-marriage-molecules-just-enough-freedom)
- [PNAS — (article landing page)](https://www.pnas.org/doi/abs/10.1073/pnas.2534579123)
**Commentary:** The design principle is that strategic pre-constraint can bank entropic “budget” released upon binding—useful for revisiting affinity-first screening defaults in ligand discovery and programmable assembly.
