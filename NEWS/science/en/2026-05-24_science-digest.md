## I. Spaceflight & Deep Space

### 1. Shenzhou 23 reaches orbit: Hong Kong’s first astronaut, plus China’s first planned one-year station stay
**Summary:** According to the China Manned Space Agency and state media (Xinhua), at 23:08 Beijing time on May 24, 2026, a Long March 2F Y23 rocket lifted off from Jiuquan and inserted the Shenzhou 23 crew spacecraft into its planned orbit, declared a success. The crew is commander Zhu Yangzhu, pilot Zhang Zhiyuan, and payload specialist Lai Ka-ying (Hong Kong). After insertion, the spacecraft followed procedures for autonomous fast rendezvous and docking with the space station complex. The mission is the seventh crewed flight of the station application & development phase and the 40th crewed spaceflight of the program; officials also state one crew member will carry out a roughly one-year on-orbit residency experiment to support long-duration human spaceflight medicine and engineering.

## II. Condensed Matter & Quantum Materials

### 2. *Nature Physics*: angular momentum transfer among lattice modes and a rotational Umklapp-like picture in Bi₂Se₃
**Summary:** An international collaboration (including Helmholtz-Zentrum Dresden-Rossendorf and the Max Planck Fritz Haber Institute) uses intense terahertz pulses to coherently drive the IR-active Eu phonon in bismuth selenide and tracks the coupled Raman-active Eg mode. The experiment resolves a helicity reversal under sum-frequency phononic coupling, interpreted with discrete rotational symmetry as a conserved “crystal angular momentum” story; the authors connect the mechanism to magnetization equilibration, spin–lattice relaxation, and ultrafast control of material responses. The work appears in *Nature Physics*.
**Links:**
- [*Nature Physics* — Observation of angular momentum transfer among crystal lattice modes](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03274-8)
- [ScienceDaily — Angular momentum transfer in a quantum material](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260523103903.htm)
**Commentary:** This is a direct view of phonon angular momentum flow between modes, giving axial nonlinear phononics a concrete, reproducible testbed beyond inferred magneto-optic proxies.

### 3. *Science*: nodeless superconducting gap and electron–boson coupling fingerprints in bilayer nickelate films
**Summary:** A USTC-led team with Southern University of Science and Technology collaborators reports ARPES measurements on Ruddlesden-Popper bilayer nickelate superconducting thin films, evidencing a nodeless superconducting gap and a dispersion kink ~70 meV below the Fermi level consistent with electron–boson coupling. Published online in *Science* on May 21, 2026, the results sharpen constraints on pairing symmetry and pairing mechanisms in nickelate high-Tc superconductivity.
**Links:**
- [*Science* — Nodeless gap and electron–boson coupling in nickelate films](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adw8329)
- [SciTechDaily — Progress on nickelate high-temperature superconductivity](https://scitechdaily.com/scientists-crack-key-mystery-behind-high-temperature-superconductors/)
**Commentary:** The field’s debate shifts from “node or not” toward identifying the bosonic mode set and how nickelates align with—or diverge from—the cuprate playbook.

## III. Biomedicine & Health

### 4. *NEJM*: Phase 3 OCEANIC-STROKE reports asundexian cuts recurrent non-cardioembolic stroke without a clear major-bleeding penalty
**Summary:** In this Bayer-funded, randomized, double-blind trial, 12,327 patients within 72 hours of non-cardioembolic stroke or high-risk TIA received asundexian 50 mg once daily or placebo on top of planned antiplatelet therapy. Ischemic stroke occurred in 6.2% vs 8.4% (cause-specific HR 0.74; 95% CI 0.65–0.84; P<0.001). ISTH major bleeding was 1.9% vs 1.7%, not indicating a prespecified harm signal in the published summary. Authors conclude asundexian reduces recurrent stroke and selected cardiovascular composites in this population.
**Links:**
- [*NEJM* — Asundexian for Secondary Stroke Prevention](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2513880)
- [PubMed — trial record](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41985132/)
**Commentary:** Factor XIa inhibition now has a large Phase 3 “fewer strokes without obvious bleeding tax” story in secondary prevention; next questions are patient selection, durability, and comparative effectiveness.

### 5. Eli Lilly details TRIUMPH-1 Phase 3 weight-loss outcomes for retatrutide (80 weeks)
**Summary:** According to a May 24, 2026 report in *The Week*, Lilly announced Phase 3 TRIUMPH-1 results for the GIP/GLP-1/GCG tri-agonist retatrutide in adults with obesity or overweight and weight-related comorbidities without diabetes: 2,339 participants randomized to placebo or weekly 4 mg, 9 mg, or 12 mg (titrated) for 80 weeks. The article cites company-reported mean weight losses of ~19.0%, ~25.9%, and ~28.3% at 4 mg, 9 mg, and 12 mg, respectively, with ~45.3% of the highest-dose arm reaching ≥30% weight loss; adverse events resemble other incretin-class agents, dominated by GI effects.
**Links:**
- [*The Week* — Lilly retatrutide trial outcomes](https://www.theweek.in/news/health/2026/05/24/how-eli-lillys-new-weight-loss-drug-achieves-up-to-30-per-cent-weight-loss.html)
**Commentary:** Efficacy keeps ratcheting upward in the incretin era; regulators and clinicians will focus on cardiovascular outcomes, durability after withdrawal, tolerability, and access.

## IV. Climate, Energy & Earth Systems

### 6. *Nature Energy*: integrated planning of net-zero power systems “for all” (GISPO-class global model)
**Summary:** Zhu Ziheng (Tsinghua University) and coauthors publish a globally resolved power-system model (~0.25°×0.25°, 8,760 hours) co-optimizing capacity expansion and operations. They report that net-zero systems meeting universal electricity needs for decent living standards appear technically feasible with roughly 15–20 TW of variable renewables, and highlight international transmission expansion, technology trade, demand-side management, and land-use planning as levers for cost and equity. Open code and reproduction assets are advertised on the lead author’s publication page.
**Links:**

**Summary:** *Ars Technica* summarizes a *PLOS One* study digitizing decades-old underwater video from a New York aquarium: belugas Natasha and Maris show exploratory and self-directed behaviors in front of a two-way mirror consistent with mirror self-recognition staging; one animal also shows mark-oriented behavior. ### 9. Theoretical work: charged, radiating black holes may avoid both singularities and Cauchy horizons (preprint / press coverage)
**Summary:** Francesco Di Filippo (Institute for Theoretical Physics, Goethe University Frankfurt) develops a formal model in arXiv:2510.20649 for a charged, spherically symmetric black hole formed by collapse with Hawking radiation, arguing electromagnetic repulsion combined with energy-condition violations from radiation can weaken standard singularity arguments so that neither a curvature singularity nor a Cauchy horizon need form in some regimes; an analogy to astrophysical spin is discussed. Science-news outlets including *Phys.org* summarize the work and note a *Physical Review Letters* publication; the arXiv PDF remains the openly accessible primary text used here.
**Links:**
- [arXiv — Radiating black holes in general relativity need not be singular](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.20649)
- [Phys.org — Charge, Hawking radiation, and interior pathologies](https://phys.org/news/2026-05-black-holes-singularities-hawking-combine.html)
**Commentary:** This is a careful re-examination of theorem boundaries in theory space; empirical leverage still requires independent observational handles on black-hole interiors and evaporation physics.
