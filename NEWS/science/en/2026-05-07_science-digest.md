## II. Climate & Environmental Science

### 5. Regional Aerosol Hygroscopicity and Global Radiative Forcing (*Communications Earth & Environment*)
**Summary:** A multi-institution study published **May 7, 2026**, in *Communications Earth & Environment* argues externally mixed aerosols can exert a stronger influence on **direct radiative forcing** than many models capture, with implications for inferred aerosol cooling—especially at suburban sites. The authors contrast a conventional bulk-chemistry approach (reporting **R² ≈ 0.61** for urban/suburban hygroscopicity prediction) with an interpretable machine-learning framework reaching **R² ≈ 0.97** on region-specific, geographically diverse training.

**Links:**
- [Nature — Regional aerosol hygroscopicity influences radiative forcing globally](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43247-026-03505-z)
**Commentary:** Elevating mixing-state realism is not a niche detail—it shifts how regional pollution controls couple to global temperature projections.

### 6. Colored Microplastics in the Atmosphere: A Non-negligible Radiative Forcing Lever (Climate)
**Summary:** Canada’s *National Observer* (**May 7, 2026**) summarizes a *Nature Climate Change* study (reported in the piece as published **May 4**) showing pigmented airborne micro/nanoplastics absorb far more sunlight than clear particles—about **75×** in the reported comparison—and yield a modeled **global mean radiative forcing around 0.039 W·m⁻²**, described in the article as roughly **16% of black carbon’s** warming effect. The story underscores urgency as plastic production grows.

**Links:**
- [Canada's National Observer — Microplastics warm the atmosphere](https://www.nationalobserver.com/2026/05/07/news/microplastics-warming-climate-change-study)
**Commentary:** If validated in assessment processes, microplastics graduate from marine headline risk to a climate-forcing line item worth monitoring.

**Links:**

## III. Biomedicine

### 8. Solid Biosciences: First Patient Dosed in Phase 3 **IMPACT DUCHENNE** Trial for SGT-003
**Summary:** Per BioSpace’s recap of a **May 7, 2026** release, Solid Biosciences dosed the **first participant** in the multinational, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase 3 **IMPACT DUCHENNE** trial of **SGT-003** for **Duchenne muscular dystrophy**—first dosing occurred in Australia at **The Children’s Hospital at Westmead**. As of a **May 4, 2026**, safety cutoff, **46** participants had been dosed in the Phase 1/2 **INSPIRE DUCHENNE** study under a steroid-only immunomodulation prophylaxis regimen, with no observed cases of drug-induced liver injury, myocarditis, TMA, or atypical HUS per the company statement. A pre-specified primary endpoint includes **18‑month change in time-to-rise (TTR) velocity**.

**Links:**
- [BioSpace — Solid Biosciences Doses First Participant in Phase 3 IMPACT DUCHENNE Clinical Trial Evaluating SGT-003 in Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/solid-biosciences-doses-first-participant-in-phase-3-impact-duchenne-clinical-trial-evaluating-sgt-003-in-duchenne-muscular-dystrophy)
