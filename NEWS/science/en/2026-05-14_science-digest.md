## I. Spaceflight & planetary exploration

### 1. NASA releases preliminary operational details for Artemis III in Earth orbit: Orion meets commercial landers; SLS flies with an inert spacer instead of an active upper stage
**Summary:** NASA’s Kennedy Space Center update published May 13, 2026 frames Artemis III (targeted around 2027) as an Earth‑orbit rehearsal in which four crew members aboard Orion practice rendezvous and docking with commercially developed lunar lander pathfinders (described in agency materials as SpaceX Starship human landing system pathfinder and Blue Origin Blue Moon Mark 2 pathfinder). Because the trajectory no longer commits to lunar injection, SLS would launch with an inert “spacer” that preserves mass and interfaces of the interim cryogenic propulsion stage but provides no thrust, leaving Orion’s European-built service module to circularize a low‑Earth‑orbit trajectory. Compared with a lunar profile, NASA argues this orbit buys more coordinated launch windows for separate elements; astronauts would spend longer on Orion than on Artemis II to stress upgraded life‑support capabilities and could ingress at least one lander pathfinder hardware article. NASA is also soliciting industry input on alternate mission communications architectures (explicitly noting the Deep Space Network will not be employed) and on potential CubeSat rides.
**Links:**
- [NASA — NASA Outlines Preliminary Artemis III Mission Plans (May 13, 2026)](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-3/nasa-outlines-preliminary-artemis-iii-mission-plans/)
- [SpaceNews — SLS to launch without upper stage for Artemis 3 (May 13, 2026)](https://spacenews.com/sls-to-launch-without-upper-stage-for-artemis-3/)
**Commentary:** De‑risking a future lunar landing by first orchestrating multi‑partner docking in Earth orbit trades schedule simplicity for tighter integration complexity across rockets, spacecraft, landers, and ground teams.

## II. Climate & environment

**Summary:** Ding, Chen, Zhang et al. publish a five‑kilometer global suitability assessment integrating aquaculture ponds and tidal flats (*Communications Earth & Environment*, online May 14, 2026). ### 4. Taiho Oncology: FDA approval for INQOVI® plus venetoclax in newly diagnosed AML adults ineligible for intensive induction chemotherapy (first all‑oral combination positioned for this cohort in company materials)
**Summary:** Taiho Pharmaceutical and Taiho Oncology announced May 14, 2026 that the FDA approved oral INQOVI® (fixed-dose decitabine‑cedazuridine) combined with venetoclax for adults with newly diagnosed acute myeloid leukemia who are age 75 and older—or otherwise medically unable to tolerate intensive induction chemotherapy—with the regimen described in their materials as the first and only FDA‑approved all‑oral combination regimen for this indication, offering an oral alternative to outpatient‑heavy parenteral hypomethylating‑agent–centric care models.
