### 3. Texas A&M: FGF2 and BMP2 Combination Triggers Partial Digit Regeneration in Mammals (*Nature Communications*)
**Summary:** Texas A&M researchers published findings in ***Nature Communications*** demonstrating that combined administration of **FGF2** (fibroblast growth factor 2) and **BMP2** (bone morphogenetic protein 2) triggers partial regeneration of amputated digit tips in mammalian models. The work extends prior findings on blastema formation in amphibians to a mammalian context, with potential implications for future regenerative medicine approaches to extremity injuries.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — FGF2/BMP2 digit regeneration (Texas A&M, 2026)](https://www.nature.com/ncomms/)
- [Texas A&M — Digit regeneration research (2026-04-23)](https://today.tamu.edu/digit-regeneration-2026/)
**Commentary:** Digit tip regeneration in mammals remains partial and context-dependent — the results are mechanistically significant but a long way from clinical application. The value of such findings is in establishing that mammalian regenerative capacity is higher than assumed, not in immediate therapeutic translation.
