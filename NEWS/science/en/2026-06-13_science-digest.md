### 2. ESA AI scan of 99.6 million Hubble cutouts confirms 1,300+ anomalies, 800+ previously undocumented
**Summary:** Per ESA/Hubble and science media around June 13, 2026, ESA researchers David O'Ryan and Pablo Gómez used the neural-network tool AnomalyMatch to systematically search ~99.6 million image cutouts from the Hubble Legacy Archive (~7–8 arcseconds each), completing initial screening in ~2.5 days. Manual review of top candidates confirmed 1,300+ true anomalies, more than 800 of which had not appeared in prior scientific literature, including gravitational-lens arcs and interacting galaxies. Results appear in *Astronomy & Astrophysics*; the tool is available via ESA Datalabs and GitHub, offering a template for mining Euclid, Rubin, Roman, and other large surveys.

### 3. JWST plus gravitational lensing weighs dormant SMBH 10 billion light-years away at ~6 billion solar masses
**Summary:** Per Carnegie Science, UCL releases, and Earth.com on June 13, 2026, an international team led by Andrew Newman used JWST stellar kinematics on galaxy MRG-M0138 at redshift ~2 to measure a dormant supermassive black hole of ~6 billion solar masses (6.0 × 10⁹ M☉, with uncertainties). At ~10 billion light-years, it is the most distant dormant black hole weighed via stellar dynamics — ~15× farther than the prior record. A foreground galaxy cluster magnifies the background ~30×, enabling star-speed measurements at cosmological distance. Published in *Science* (DOI: 10.1126/science.adx5816).
**Links:**
- [EurekAlert — JWST measures mass of a dormant black hole from the early universe for the first time](https://www.eurekalert.org/news-releases/1130556)
- [Science — A stellar dynamical mass measurement of an inactive black hole at redshift 2](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adx5816)

**Commentary:** A ~6-billion-solar-mass dormant black hole when the universe was ~3 Gyr old anchors early galaxy–black-hole co-evolution models and validates lensing+JWST dynamical measurements at high redshift.

## II. Cosmology & Fundamental Research

### 4. European and Chinese teams report first working thorium-229 nuclear clocks with feedback-stabilized 148 nm transitions
**Summary:** Per *New Scientist*, *Science News*, and arXiv preprints in June 2026, Thorsten Schumm's team at TU Wien with Germany's PTB embedded thorium-229 in CaF₂ crystals, locked a continuous-wave laser to the 148 nm nuclear transition with a feedback loop, and demonstrated a continuously running solid-state nuclear clock prototype stable for 24 hours without intervention — immediately applied to constrain ultralight dark matter. A Chinese team independently reported 229Th nuclear clock operation (arXiv:2606.08870). Nuclear clocks use nuclear rather than electronic transitions, promising greater robustness and potentially surpassing optical atomic clocks by orders of magnitude.
**Links:**
- [New Scientist — First working nuclear clock heralds a new era in timekeeping](https://www.newscientist.com/article/2530094-first-working-nuclear-clock-heralds-a-new-era-in-timekeeping/)
- [arXiv — A thorium-229 optical nuclear clock with feedback loop](https://arxiv.org/html/2606.04997)

**Commentary:** Two decades of chasing nuclear-transition timekeeping have moved from excitation spectroscopy to closed-loop clocks — metrology and dark-matter searches may soon share the same solid-state nuclear frequency standard.

### 5. Southampton-led reanalysis rebuts 2025 "cosmic slowdown" claim; Type Ia supernova cosmology confirms accelerating expansion
**Summary:** Per ScienceDaily on June 13, 2026, and the Royal Astronomical Society, Phil Wiseman's University of Southampton team (including Adam Riess and Brian Schmidt) in *Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society* (DOI: 10.1093/mnras/stag797) re-examined Type Ia supernova cosmology, finding key errors in a 2025 Korean study claiming accelerating expansion was an illusion: host-galaxy age was mistaken for progenitor-star age, and standard host-galaxy mass corrections were omitted. ### 6. IonQ trapped-ion processor realizes ν = 1/3 fermionic Laughlin state, validating key topological observables
**Summary:** Per *Nature Communications* published June 8, 2026 (DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-72769-y), IonQ and collaborators prepared the ν = 1/3 fermionic Laughlin state on a 16-qubit circuit with 369 two-qubit gates, extracting correlation hole, bulk–edge correspondence, and topological entanglement entropy in strong agreement with exact diagonalization. The Laughlin state is a cornerstone of fractional charge, anyonic excitations, and incompressibility; bosonic analogs existed on simulators, but this is the first fermionic Laughlin demonstration on a quantum processor.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — Realization of fermionic Laughlin state on a quantum processor](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-72769-y)

**Commentary:** From topologically ordered phases hard to probe in materials to programmable hardware — an expandable path toward anyon physics and topological quantum information.

### 7. *Nature Chemistry*: End-capped-free carbyne chains show quasi-ballistic charge transport between Au electrodes
**Summary:** Per *Nature Chemistry* online June 12, 2026 (DOI: 10.1038/s41557-026-02175-w), Morris et al. used transmetallation from Au(I) species to Au(0) electrodes to build one-dimensional carbon chains up to 16 atoms without end-capping groups, characterizing transport via scanning tunneling microscope break junctions. Shorter chains show oligoyne-like conductance decay with length; longer chains exhibit bond-length equalization toward cumulenic structure with markedly enhanced conductance; direct Au|C contact enables quasi-ballistic transport.
**Links:**
- [Nature Chemistry — Charge transport through linear carbon atomic chains](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41557-026-02175-w)

**Commentary:** Linear carbon allotropes move from synthesis challenges to measurable conductance — a junction-stabilization route toward carbon-based 1D nanoelectronics.

**Summary:** Per Intellia Therapeutics on June 13, 2026, and simultaneous *New England Journal of Medicine* publication, the global Phase 3 HAELO trial (80 HAE patients, randomized double-blind placebo-controlled) showed lonvoguran ziclumeran (lonvo-z, NTLA-2002) via single IV infusion reduced attack frequency 87% relative to placebo between weeks 5–28; 62% of treated patients were attack-free without maintenance therapy vs. 11% on placebo. ## IV. Climate, Energy & Environment

### 12. Rahmstorf team: Atlantic "cold blob" is AMOC slowdown signature; system approaching tipping point
**Summary:** Per Phys.org and Eastern Herald on June 13, 2026, Stefan Rahmstorf's Potsdam Institute team in *Geophysical Research Letters* (DOI: 10.1029/2025gl118383) using reanalysis data finds cooling south of Greenland and Iceland is a depth-resolved signature of reduced ocean heat transport, not increased surface heat loss — surface heat loss in the region has actually decreased. AMOC is an IPCC climate tipping element; standard CMIP6 scenarios show tipping crossed around mid-century in a substantial subset of runs; authors say the system is close enough to warrant serious policy concern.
**Links:**
- [Phys.org — Atlantic 'cold blob' caused by weakening ocean current system](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-atlantic-cold-blob-weakening-ocean.html)

**Commentary:** The cold blob upgrades from phenomenon to AMOC-weakening observational fingerprint — long-term risks to European winters and fisheries are re-quantified.
