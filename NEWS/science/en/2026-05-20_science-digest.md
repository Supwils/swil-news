## II. Astronomy & Cosmology

### 3. **TIME** commissioning maps toward **Sgr A**: validating pipelines for extragalactic **CO / [C II]** line-intensity mapping (mm-wave | instrumentation)
**Summary:** Researchers including a **Cornell**-led team report in *The Astrophysical Journal* (**published 2026 May 12**; **DOI: 10.3847/1538-4357/ae606c**) on commissioning observations with the **Tomographic Ionized-carbon Mapping Experiment (TIME)** during the **2021–2022** season at the **Arizona Radio Observatory 12 m** telescope toward the **Sgr A** molecular complex. After correlated-noise mitigation, the analysis recovers strong **12CO(2–1)** and **13CO(2–1)** emission and continuum components; broadband continuum fluxes agree with the **Bolocam Galactic Plane Survey** to roughly **5%** in high-SNR molecular clouds. **CO** line-ratio modelling yields a molecular hydrogen mass around **5.4×10⁵–5.7×10⁵ M⊙**, consistent with prior work, arguing the instrument is ready for harder extragalactic surveys. **Technology.org** summarises the work and its early-galaxy science goals on **2026-05-20**.

## III. Climate, Environment & Energy

### 4. Accelerating **North American heat stress** exposure: **+21%** since the **1940s** for **WBGT > 32 °C**, **+273%** at **2 °C** warming (heat waves | public health)
**Summary:** Authors from **ETH Zürich**, **NSF NCAR**, **Stanford**, **Princeton**, **Purdue**, and **Iowa** publish in *Nature Communications* (**2026-05-19**; **DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-72795-w**) using reanalyses and climate simulations to show U.S. population exposure to extreme heat (wet-bulb globe temperature **> 32 °C**) has risen **21%** since the **1940s**; at **2 °C** global warming, exposure rises **273%** as heat-stress frequency grows roughly exponentially with warming. The paper highlights rising nighttime stress, reduced work capacity, differing northern versus southern heatwave morphologies (including month-long merged events in the south), and the roles of temperature, humidity, and wind for regional adaptation needs.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — Local drivers in accelerating North American heat stress (2026)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-72795-w)
**Commentary:** Population-weighted exposure tied to **WBGT** thresholds gives urban planning and occupational-health policy a more operational heat metric than abstract global-mean warming alone.

## V. Materials, Photonics & Nanotechnology

### 8. Giant **second-harmonic generation** in aligned chiral carbon nanotube films: **Rice** quantifies a long-predicted nonlinear-optical response (nonlinear optics | flexible photonics)
**Summary:** A **Rice University** release dated **19 May 2026** describes work in *ACS Nano* (**DOI: 10.1021/acsnano.6c06017**) led by **Hanyu Zhu** and **Junichiro Kono** with collaborators including chiral-nanotube isolation by **Kazuhiro Yanagi**’s group at **Tokyo Metropolitan University**. The team builds wafer-scale films of single-handedness chiral nanotubes and measures **second-harmonic generation (SHG)** with conversion efficiency **two to three orders of magnitude** higher than conventional materials—quantifying a decades-old prediction that was hard to test before macroscopic chirality and alignment could be controlled. The release discusses routes toward more compact light converters and integration with silicon photonics.
**Links:**
