## II. Quantum Optics & Imaging

### 3. Sunlight‑pumped **SPDC** enables quantum **ghost imaging** at visibility competitive with a lab laser (quantum optics | remote sensing potential)
**Summary:** Researchers including **Wuhong Zhang** and **Lixiang Chen** (**Xiamen University**) report in *Advanced Photonics* (**2026**; DOI **10.1117/1.AP.8.3.036011**) a sun‑tracking system that couples sunlight into a ~**20 m** multimode fiber to pump a **PPKTP** crystal for spontaneous parametric down‑conversion. In ghost‑imaging demonstrations, the sunlight‑driven setup achieved **90.7%** visibility compared with **95.5%** for a conventional **405 nm** laser at matched pump power. An **SPIE** news item (**May 14, 2026**) and a **ScienceDaily** release (**May 17, 2026**) describe the work as a first demonstration combining sunlight‑pumped **SPDC** with ghost imaging, with discussion of remote and space‑relevant deployment where stable lasers are costly.
**Links:**
- [SPIE — Sunlight-powered generation of correlated photon pairs (2026-05-14)](https://spie.org/news/sunlight-powered-generation-of-correlated-photon-pairs)
- [ScienceDaily — Quantum ghost imaging works using only sunlight in stunning new experiment (2026-05-17)](http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260517211424.htm)
**Commentary:** Replacing laser infrastructure with passive solar pumping is a credible engineering path toward fieldable quantum imaging links—if collection optics, crystal engineering, and reconstruction algorithms mature in tandem.

## III. Astrophysics & Cosmology

### 4. Scintillation imaging of **PSR B1508+55**: **Effelsberg** + **FAST** reveal ordered interstellar scattering structures (radio astronomy | interstellar medium)
**Summary:** An international team led by **Tim Sprenger** (**Max Planck Institute for Radio Astronomy**) measured flickering radio emission from pulsar **PSR B1508+55** (~**7,000 light‑years** away in Draco) caused by interstellar scattering; long‑exposure maps show a nearly linear smearing pattern, interpreted as evidence that scattering material is not purely random but can include oriented sheet‑like or filamentary structure. The observing strategy exploits large baselines between **Germany** and **China** combined with Earth’s motion to synthesize imaging resolution difficult to obtain otherwise at the observed frequencies; modeling places a scattering screen near **430 light‑years** from Earth. **Technology.org** summarized the story on **May 18, 2026**, citing **Max Planck Society** material.
**Links:**

**Commentary:** Treating pulsars as “interstellar medium microscopes” turns scintillation from a nuisance into a structural diagnostic—especially when paired with globally distributed, ultra‑sensitive radio facilities.

### 5. Galaxy‑cluster‑scale test of gravity’s distance scaling: **ACT** + galaxy maps favor standard gravity and tighten pressure on **MOND** (cosmology | dark matter)
**Summary:** **Technology.org** (**May 16, 2026**) and **USC Dornsife** (**April 15, 2026**) describe a line of analysis using the **Atacama Cosmology Telescope (ACT)** cosmic microwave background measurements together with large galaxy maps to infer how galaxy clusters move toward one another across hundreds of millions of light‑years. The reported conclusion is that cluster infall is consistent with standard gravity plus unseen mass—strengthening the dark‑matter interpretation—and that certain modified‑gravity alternatives (notably **MOND**) face tighter pressure in this regime. Sources also note limitations: subtle signals, statistical combination across huge samples, and theory‑dependent extensions.

## IV. Biomedicine, Devices & Regulation

### 6. Microneedle sensors track drug clearance for **six days** in rats; signals may precede creatinine thresholds in kidney injury models (*Science Translational Medicine* | wearables)
**Summary:** A **UCLA** news release dated **April 17, 2026** describes a microneedle platform led by **Sam Emaminejad** that continuously monitored drug concentrations in freely moving rats for about **six days**, inferring liver and kidney clearance from pharmacokinetic trajectories of a liver‑metabolized chemotherapeutic and a kidney‑cleared antibiotic. The team reports that during the first week of kidney injury, microneedle‑derived clearance impairment appeared while blood creatinine remained below conventional injury thresholds—an early‑warning signal in a preclinical setting. The peer‑reviewed article is linked as DOI **10.1126/scitranslmed.adr5493**; **Technology.org** published a synthesis on **May 18, 2026**.

## V. Climate, Energy & Earth Systems

### 8. **IRENA**: firm solar/wind‑plus‑storage can undercut new fossil generation on cost in high‑resource regions (energy transition | economics)
**Summary:** The **International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA)** issued a **May 6, 2026** press release alongside the report *24/7 renewables: The economics of firm solar and wind*, arguing that hybrid solar/wind with storage can deliver round‑the‑clock (“firm”) electricity at competitive costs. The release cites indicative firm levelized costs of roughly **USD 54–82/MWh** in premium resource regions, compared with about **USD 70–85/MWh** for new coal in **China** and more than **USD 100/MWh** for new gas globally in the agency’s framing, alongside long‑term cost‑decline statistics since **2010** for photovoltaics, onshore wind, and batteries, and notes demand from **AI** data centers for highly reliable clean power.
