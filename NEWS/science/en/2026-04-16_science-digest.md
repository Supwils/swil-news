## II. Biomedical & Clinical Research

### 4. Eli Lilly Foundayo (orforglipron) ACHIEVE-4 Phase 3 Positive: CV Non-Inferiority Met; NDA Planned Q2 2026
**Summary:** Eli Lilly published primary results from the event-driven, open-label Phase 3 ACHIEVE-4 trial (NCT05803421): in adults with type 2 diabetes plus obesity/overweight and high cardiovascular risk, Foundayo met the pre-specified non-inferiority framework vs. insulin glargine for the MACE-4 composite primary endpoint (CV death, MI, stroke, or unstable angina hospitalization). The reported hazard ratio was approximately 0.84 (95% CI approximately 0.59–1.20); a pre-specified analysis showed a statistically significant all-cause mortality reduction (HR approximately 0.43). At 52 weeks, Foundayo showed superiority over insulin in HbA1c reduction and weight change. More than 2,700 patients from 15 countries were enrolled. Lilly announced plans to file a US FDA NDA for the type 2 diabetes indication before end of Q2 2026.

### 9. Nature Communications: China Net-Zero Pathway Requires Explicit Sub-Hourly Flexibility Modeling
**Summary:** Zhang and Chen developed an energy-environment-economy sub-annual model coupling hourly power balancing with demand curve evolution, comparing feasibility domains for China's net-zero transition under coarse vs. fine time resolution. Results showed that ignoring temporal detail systematically understates ramping and evening peak price impacts in high-variable-renewable, high-electrification scenarios by more than 9% in marginal abatement cost estimates, while changing the optimal mix of coal, nuclear, hydrogen production, and storage. The paper quantified the demand-side response substitution effect on investment structure.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — Exploring the feasible net-zero transition pathway in China considering energy system flexibility](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-71410-2)
**Commentary:** National models that still use "a few annual time slices" create the policy illusion of "enough renewables but the system can't accommodate them" — this paper moves flexibility economics from verbal correctness to a calibratable constraint set.

### 10. Nature Communications: Afforestation and Onshore Wind Compete for the Same Atmospheric Boundary Layer
**Summary:** Zhang, Gou, Zhu et al. used coupled land-atmosphere simulation to compare global onshore wind technical potential under different afforestation area scenarios. In the maximum ~1,508 Mha afforestation scenario, biomass carbon sinks could reach approximately 90.7 GtC, but global onshore wind technical potential would decline by approximately 19.5% on average — with marked regional variation. Afforestation expansion in Europe and the continental US could not only reduce future wind resource potential but also erode output from existing wind farms.
**Links:**
