### 3. Janux Therapeutics: JANX014 Phase 1 First Patient Dosed (PSMA Dual-Masked TCE; mCRPC)
**Summary:** Janux Therapeutics announced that the first patient had been dosed in a Phase 1 clinical trial of JANX014 — a PSMA-targeting dual-masked T-cell engager (TCE) designed for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC). The "dual-masking" design is intended to activate the bispecific antibody selectively in the tumor microenvironment rather than systemically, with the goal of reducing the cytokine release syndrome and off-target toxicity that have limited earlier T-cell engager approaches in prostate cancer.
**Links:**
- [Janux Therapeutics — JANX014 Phase 1 initiation](https://www.januxrx.com/)
**Commentary:** Tumor-selective unmasking of T-cell engagers is one of the most active areas in oncology engineering — the clinical question is whether the masking/unmasking selectivity translates from preclinical models to the heterogeneous tumor microenvironment of human mCRPC. First-patient dosing initiates the safety observation period rather than providing efficacy signals.

### 4. Chengda Bio: Simplified 4-Shot Rabies PEP Schedule Approved by China NMPA
**Summary:** Chengda Biopharmaceuticals received China NMPA approval for a simplified 4-shot post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) rabies vaccination schedule, reducing the standard course from 5 shots over 28 days to 4 shots over a shorter window. The approval was significant for public health in China, where approximately 3,000–5,000 animal bite exposures requiring rabies PEP are reported daily, and schedule completion rates are a known challenge in rural settings.
**Links:**
- [Chengda Bio — NMPA approval announcement](http://www.chengda.com/)
**Commentary:** Reducing a PEP schedule from 5 to 4 doses has disproportionate impact in high-exposure rural areas where the incremental logistic barrier of each additional clinic visit meaningfully reduces completion rates. Non-completion of rabies PEP is fatal — so schedule simplification is a genuine public health intervention, not a cosmetic change.

## III. Earth Science & Environmental Research

### 5. PNAS: Ocean Methane Emission Governed by Phosphate Scarcity in Global Open Ocean
**Summary:** A paper published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences argued that phosphate availability is the primary governing factor for methane production in the global open ocean, explaining the "ocean methane paradox" — methane supersaturation in oxygenated surface waters. The proposed mechanism involved microbial phosphonate degradation as a pathway for methane production in phosphate-limited regions. DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2521235123.
**Links:**
- [PNAS — Ocean methane phosphate scarcity](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2521235123)
**Commentary:** The ocean methane paradox has been debated for decades — a phosphate-limitation mechanism adds a new regulatory lever for understanding how ocean methane emissions might respond to climate-driven changes in nutrient cycling, with implications for climate models that treat ocean methane as a fixed background source.

### 6. Colombia's Cerros de la Plaza Glacier Completely Disappeared (IDEAM Satellite Monitoring)
**Summary:** Colombia's national environmental institute IDEAM confirmed via satellite monitoring that the Cerros de la Plaza glacier — one of Colombia's remaining Andean glaciers — had completely disappeared. The glacier joins a growing list of small high-altitude glaciers in tropical South America that have been lost entirely since systematic monitoring began. IDEAM estimated that Colombia had lost approximately 80% of its total glacier area since 1850, with the remaining glaciers projected to disappear within decades.
**Links:**
- [IDEAM — Cerros de la Plaza glacier loss confirmation](https://www.ideam.gov.co/)
**Commentary:** The complete loss of an individual glacier is a documentable endpoint in a process that otherwise unfolds too slowly for public engagement. The IDEAM monitoring program provides the longitudinal data needed to move from "concerning trend" to "documented endpoint" — which has different policy and legal implications for climate litigation and adaptation planning.

## IV. Fundamental Science & Materials

### 8. Nature Chemistry: MonBI-MonBII Paired Polyether Epoxide Hydrolases Reveal Biosynthetic Logic
**Summary:** A paper published in Nature Chemistry characterized a previously undescribed pair of polyether epoxide hydrolases designated MonBI and MonBII, revealing a paired enzymatic mechanism for building polyether natural product scaffolds. The structural and biochemical analysis showed how the two enzymes work in sequential coordination to control stereochemistry during epoxide ring opening — a biosynthetic problem that synthetic chemists have struggled to solve efficiently. DOI: 10.1038/s41557-026-02122-9.
**Links:**
- [Nature Chemistry — MonBI-MonBII paired polyether epoxide hydrolases](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41557-026-02122-9)
**Commentary:** Understanding the enzymatic logic for controlling polyether stereocenters has direct relevance for biosynthetic and semi-synthetic routes to complex natural products with antiparasitic, antifungal, or ion channel modulation activity — categories with significant pharmaceutical interest.

### 9. Rydberg Dipolar Chain Vector Electrometry: Frontiers of Optoelectronics Paper
**Summary:** Researchers published results in Frontiers of Optoelectronics demonstrating vector electric field sensing using Rydberg atoms in a dipolar chain configuration. The approach exploited the highly polarizable nature of Rydberg states and dipolar interactions between atoms in the chain to achieve multi-axis (vector) electric field measurement with high sensitivity. The technique was proposed as a platform for precision microwave electrometry and potentially for sensing near-field interactions. DOI: 10.2738/foe.2026.0006.
**Links:**
- [Frontiers of Optoelectronics — Rydberg dipolar chain electrometry](https://doi.org/10.2738/foe.2026.0006)
**Commentary:** Rydberg atom electrometry is a rapidly maturing quantum sensing platform — extending it to vector measurement substantially increases its practical utility for antenna characterization, near-field communication analysis, and field mapping in complex electromagnetic environments.
