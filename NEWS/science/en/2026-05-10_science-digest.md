## I. Space, deep space, and international cooperation

### 1. ESA and JAXA formalize Ramses planetary-defense cooperation ahead of Apophis’ 2029 Earth flyby (spaceflight)
**Summary:** On May 7, 2026, the European Space Agency and the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency signed a planetary-defense memorandum of cooperation and a dedicated agreement for the Rapid Apophis Mission for Space Safety (Ramses), with the Italian Space Agency (ASI) noting the step implements expanded ESA–JAXA intent dating to a November 2024 joint statement. Ramses is targeted to launch in 2028 and rendezvous with near-Earth asteroid (99942) Apophis ahead of its rare close approach in April 2029. ESA leads spacecraft design, integration, and operations; JAXA is set to contribute elements such as lightweight solar arrays and an infrared imager, with launch on Japan’s H3 rocket described in agency materials. Apophis is described as roughly 375 metres across, with institutional materials citing a closest-approach altitude near 32,000 km above Earth’s surface on April 13, 2029.

- [ASI — ESA and JAXA team up on planetary defence, Ramses mission to asteroid Apophis](https://www.asi.it/en/2026/05/esa-and-jaxa-team-up-on-planetary-defence-ramses-mission-to-asteroid-apophis/)
**Commentary:** Near-Earth-object risk is inherently multinational: agreements matter because they convert political commitments into auditable engineering roles and launch-window accountability.

### 2. Europe’s first reusable orbital platform, Space Rider: thermal protection and full-scale landing demo hardware advance (spaceflight)
**Summary:** Reporting on May 6, 2026 (Space.com) alongside ESA technical notes, engineers for the uncrewed reusable vehicle Space Rider have advanced end-to-end return verification: plasma wind-tunnel testing at Italy’s CIRA subjected belly tiles and control flaps to conditions described as on the order of 1,600 °C and Mach-10-class flows, and a full-scale drop-test article has been assembled for upcoming helicopter release trials in Sardinia aimed at a guided parafoil terminal descent. The lifting-body design is meant to support runway-class recoveries after roughly two months in low Earth orbit with an experiment bay for returned payloads.
**Links:**
- [Space.com — Europe's 1st reusable spacecraft 'Space Rider' clears key hurdles on the road to launch](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/launches-spacecraft/europes-1st-reusable-spacecraft-space-rider-clears-key-hurdles-on-the-road-to-launch)

**Commentary:** Reusable spaceplanes often fail on the last integration problems—thermal protection, guidance, and controllability through the final descent; parallel plasma-ground testing and drop campaigns closes the loop from orbit to rapid retrieval.

### 4. NASA’s TESS uses eclipse timing in binaries to find exoplanet candidates beyond classical transits (astrophysics)
**Summary:** A NASA Science release dated May 4, 2026 describes an analysis of 1,590 eclipsing binaries with at least two years of TESS coverage, reporting 27 systems with planet candidates inferred from eclipse-timing variations—helpful where planetary orbits are not favorably aligned for transits. The article situates the result within TESS’s cumulative tally (885 confirmed planets and more than 7,900 candidates in the text) and links to a May 4, 2026 *Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society* paper (DOI path stag515).
**Links:**
- [NASA Science — For NASA’s TESS, Stellar Eclipses Shed Light on Possible New Worlds](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/tess/for-nasas-tess-stellar-eclipses-shed-light-on-possible-new-worlds/)
- [MNRAS — advance article DOI landing page (stag515)](https://academic.oup.com/mnras/advance-article/doi/10.1093/mnras/stag515/8524019)
**Commentary:** TESS was optimized for transits; squeezing eclipse-timing planet science out of the same survey is a durable way to multiply discovery yield without launching new hardware.

## II. Planetary science

### 5. Venus’ vast cloud disturbance linked to a planetary-scale hydraulic jump connecting super-rotation and cloud fronts (planetary atmospheres)
**Summary:** A May 10, 2026 Astrobiology.com synthesis of a University of Tokyo–led explanation for Akatsuki-era imagery of a ~6,000-km cloud disturbance: numerical fluid modeling argues the feature arises from a hydraulic jump—an abrupt transition from fast, shallow flow to slower, deeper flow—when an eastward Kelvin-wave mode in Venus’s lower-to-middle clouds goes unstable, producing a localized updraft that loft sulfuric acid vapor to condense into a sweeping cloud front. The peer-reviewed article is in the *Journal of Geophysical Research: Planets*, posted April 24, 2026, with DOI 10.1029/2026JE009672.
**Links:**
- [Astrobiology.com — Venus’ Atmosphere Jumps And Waves](https://astrobiology.com/2026/05/venus-atmosphere-jumps-and-waves.html)
- [JGR: Planets — A planetary-scale hydraulic jump driving Venus’ cloud front (Wiley)](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1029/2026JE009672)
**Commentary:** Importing a textbook fluid phenomenon into Venus’s super-rotating acid-cloud regime gives mission planners a concrete dynamical target for global models and future in situ probes.

## III. Particle physics, materials, and engineering frontiers

### 6. RHIC–STAR reports Λ–Λ̄ spin correlations in *Nature*, connecting strange-quark vacuum structure to hadronization observables (particles | QCD)
**Summary:** The *Nature* article “Measuring spin correlation between quarks during QCD confinement” reports STAR measurements in proton–proton collisions at RHIC (Brookhaven National Laboratory) in which short-range Λ–Λ̄ pairs show a relative polarization correlation on the order of (18 ± 4)%, with correlations approaching zero at larger rapidity/azimuth separation; the authors interpret the pattern in terms of spin-triplet strange quark–antiquark pairs materializing from the QCD vacuum and hadronizing, and discuss implications for nonperturbative hadronization and decoherence. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-025-09920-0.
**Links:**
- [Nature — Measuring spin correlation between quarks during QCD confinement](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-025-09920-0)
- [arXiv — 2506.05499](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05499)
**Commentary:** The result threads “vacuum structure → parton materialization → spin observables” into one experimental narrative; cross-checks across beam energies and systems will test how generic the hadronization-preserves-correlation story is.

**Summary:** *New Scientist* covers Texas A&M University work on ~0.01-mm metasurface “metajets” whose micro-pillar texturing steers refracted light and the reaction momentum; in a water tank laser demonstration the devices both levitate and translate horizontally, with reported peak speeds around 0.07 mm s⁻¹, and the researchers discuss extending wavelength coverage toward sunlight for sail concepts and potential biomedical micro-propulsion uses.
**Links:**
- [New Scientist — Tiny 'metajets' could use light to steer sails for interstellar travel](https://www.newscientist.com/article/2525802-tiny-metajets-could-use-light-to-steer-sails-for-interstellar-travel/)
**Commentary:** Light sails need vectorable thrust, not just scalar radiation pressure; tabletop demonstrations of directionally engineered momentum transfer are a necessary precursor to credible mission architectures.
