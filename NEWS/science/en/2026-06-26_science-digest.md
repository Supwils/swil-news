## I. Climate & Environment

### 1. World Weather Attribution: Europe's record June heatwave was "virtually impossible" in 1976, hundreds of times more likely than in 2003
**Summary:** Per a World Weather Attribution (WWA) rapid analysis released June 26, 2026, and CNN and phys.org coverage the same day, researchers assessed the extreme heatwave that began June 18 across Western Europe, focusing on the three hottest days and nights over the most affected region. They found that comparable June heat would have been statistically "virtually impossible" in 1976; versus the deadly 2003 European heatwave, today's nighttime extremes are more than 100 times more likely and daytime highs about 10 times more common. At roughly 1.4°C of global warming, a similar circulation pattern (southerly flow) now runs about 3.5°C hotter by day and 2.4°C hotter at night than in June 1976. The study covered 19 national capitals and 854 European cities; about 45% broke indoor wet-bulb globe temperature (WBGT) records. France has reported at least 40 heat-related deaths; schools, rail, and power grids are under strain across multiple countries.
**Links:**
- [World Weather Attribution — Fossil fuel emissions have rapidly worsened European heatwaves](https://www.worldweatherattribution.org/fossil-fuel-emissions-have-rapidly-worsened-european-heatwaves-in-just-a-few-decades/)
- [CNN — Europe's record-shattering heat wave would have been 'virtually impossible' just a few decades ago](https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/26/climate/european-heat-wave-impossible-global-warming)

**Commentary:** Attribution turns abstract warming into probability shifts within living memory — extreme heat is no longer a freak event but an expected stress test for infrastructure at 1.4°C.

### 4. NASA TESS finds "puffiest" exoplanet pair TOI-791 b/c: Jupiter-sized, less dense than cotton candy
**Summary:** Per NASA Science, phys.org, and Engadget on June 26, 2026, an international team led by Georgina Dransfield (University of Oxford) confirmed two super-puff exoplanets, TOI-791 b and TOI-791 c, orbiting F-type dwarf star TOI-791 about 1,110–1,113 light-years away in the southern constellation Volans. Both are near or larger than Jupiter in size but hold only about 3% and 5.9% of Jupiter's mass, with densities of 0.038 and 0.047 g/cm³ — below cotton candy (~0.05 g/cm³). Orbital periods are 139 and 232 days in a 5:3 mean-motion resonance; TESS needed 1,122 days of observations to confirm the long-period transits. Only five systems are known to host multiple super-puffs; TOI-791 is a rare laboratory for giant-planet formation and evolution. Published in *Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society*.
**Links:**
- [NASA Science — NASA's TESS Mission Reveals the "Puffiest" Planets Ever Found](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/tess/nasas-tess-mission-reveals-the-puffiest-planets-ever-found/)
- [phys.org — 'Super-puff' planets less dense than cotton candy discovered](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-super-puff-planets-lighter-candy.html)

**Commentary:** Same volume, cotton-candy mass — super-puffs strain standard accretion models; this long-period resonant pair may be the natural experiment needed to explain the "puff."

## III. Biomedicine & Health

### 5. *Science* genomic study traces invasive malaria vector *Anopheles stephensi* from South Asia via Djibouti into Africa
**Summary:** Per Liverpool School of Tropical Medicine (LSTM) on June 26, 2026, and Gavi coverage June 25–26, an LSTM-led team published a genomic surveillance study in *Science* sequencing 645 *An. stephensi* mosquitoes from Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia. The urban-adapted vector likely originated in South Asia (coastal Pakistan or northern India), established a "bridgehead" population in Djibouti around 2012, then spread to Yemen, Ethiopia, and possibly Sudan; Sudan's flat, windy terrain aided spread while the Ethiopian Highlands slowed southward movement, creating genetically distinct subpopulations. Most insecticide-resistance genes were imported from Asia rather than evolving locally in Africa — the mosquito may have arrived pre-equipped to evade common insecticides, threatening roughly 126 million urban residents. DOI: 10.1126/science.adx6925.
**Links:**

### 6. McMaster *Nature* paper: Streptomyces "megacluster" encodes four synergistic biotin-targeting antibiotics
**Summary:** Per Ars Technica on June 26, 2026, and McMaster news releases, Eric Brown's team discovered a rare DNA "megacluster" in Streptomyces encoding four natural-product families — stravidins, acidomycin, dapamycins, and α-Me-KAPA — that jointly attack biotin (vitamin B7) synthesis, uptake, and use, an essential nutrient for most bacterial growth and division. Dapamycins is entirely new to science; acidomycin was recognized as an antibiotic for the first time. The strategy is conserved across Streptomyces species; two compounds were highly effective against multidrug-resistant *E. coli* in animal infection models. Published in *Nature* June 24, 2026; DOI: 10.1038/s41586-026-10647-9.
**Links:**

**Commentary:** Bacteria evolved combination therapy long before pharma — antibiotic discovery may shift from single-molecule screens to reconstructing native synergistic systems to raise the resistance bar.

## IV. Basic Research & Physical Chemistry

### 12. *Nature*: Light-induced quantum friction slows single-walled carbon nanotubes in water under near-infrared excitation
**Summary:** Per a *Nature* paper published June 26, 2026, researchers observed that functionalized single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs) in aqueous solution show an excitation-power-dependent linear decrease of about 50% in diffusion constants under near-infrared excitation, attributed to quantum friction between excitons and water. The effect vanishes when excitons are localized (e.g., quantum-defect SWCNTs) and can be modulated up to 2× by chemically tuning fluorescence. Optical pump–terahertz probe spectroscopy shows an ~30 cm⁻¹ instantaneous response (exciton–water Debye-mode coupling) and a >100 ps response in hydrogen-bond translational modes. Classical molecular dynamics support fluctuating-dipole friction. DOI: 10.1038/s41586-026-10632-2.
**Links:**
- [Nature — Light-induced quantum friction of carbon nanotubes in water](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10632-2)

**Commentary:** Light is not just illumination or heating — electronic excitation can directly "slow" nanoscale motion, offering a quantum lever to control fluids and nanomachines.

### 13. Columbia and Indiana University team magneto-optically traps calcium monohydride (CaH) below 1 mK
**Summary:** Per phys.org on June 26, 2026, and a *Physical Review Letters* paper (Dai et al.), researchers effectively cooled and trapped about 230 calcium monohydride (CaH) molecules in a magneto-optical trap (MOT) below 1 millikelvin (mK), showing metal hydrides can be laser-cooled despite predissociative loss channels. The platform could enable photodissociation toward ultracold atomic hydrogen for high-precision Standard Model tests and fundamental-constant measurements; work toward higher phase-space density and dissociation spectroscopy is underway. DOI: 10.1103/xy6y-kyhc.
**Links:**

**Commentary:** Ultracold chemistry expands beyond alkali and alkaline-earth dimers into metal hydrides — paving an experimental path to ultracold hydrogen as an ideal probe atom.
