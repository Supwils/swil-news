## I. Space & Deep Space

### 1. NASA confirms natural meteor breakup over New England; ~300 tons TNT equivalent sonic boom
**Summary:** Per The Verge and Phys.org (May 31, 2026), at roughly 2:06 p.m. ET on Saturday, May 31, a natural meteor about 0.9 m (~3 ft) wide entered the atmosphere at ~120,700 km/h (~75,000 mph) and fragmented near 64 km (40 miles) altitude over northeastern Massachusetts and southeastern New Hampshire. NASA estimates energy release equivalent to ~300 tons of TNT, producing a widely heard double sonic boom and building shaking across New England; GOES-19 also captured the fireball. USGS and NASA ruled out earthquakes and satellite/debris re-entry; the event was not tied to an active meteor shower. The American Meteor Society received reports from Delaware to Montreal.

### 2. “Phoebe”: lunar-mass microlensing candidate in the Milky Way halo may be a primordial black hole
**Summary:** Per ScienceAlert and Phys.org (around May 31, 2026), Swinburne University–led researchers re-analyzed a ~1-hour microlensing brightening of an LMC star recorded on Dec. 18, 2019, by DECam high-cadence observations, nicknaming the lens “Phoebe.” The arXiv preprint (AMPM II) infers a mass of ~3 lunar masses at ~59,630 light-years, with membership in the Milky Way dark-matter halo favored over known stellar populations by ~five orders of magnitude; after excluding instrumental glitches and stellar variability, a primordial black hole (PBH) is the preferred interpretation among alternatives including halo rogue planets. The work is not yet peer-reviewed and intersects ongoing debate over Andromeda microlensing candidates reported in early 2026.
**Links:**
- [arXiv — AMPM II: A Lunar-Mass Primordial Black Hole Microlensing Candidate in the Milky Way Halo](https://arxiv.org/html/2605.19375v1)
- [ScienceAlert — Strange Event Might Have Been A Primordial Black Hole Winking At Us](https://www.sciencealert.com/strange-event-might-have-been-a-primordial-black-hole-winking-at-us)

**Commentary:** High-cadence follow-up from Roman or Vera C. Rubin could turn lunar-mass PBHs into rare probes of dark-matter composition and early-universe inflation physics.

### 4. RASolute 302 in NEJM and ASCO plenary: daraxonrasib median OS 13.2 vs 6.7 months in second-line metastatic pancreatic cancer
**Summary:** Per Dana-Farber, Revolution Medicines, and Science News (May 31, 2026), global randomized open-label Phase 3 RASolute 302 (NCT06625320) results were presented at ASCO (LBA5) by Brian Wolpin and published simultaneously in the New England Journal of Medicine. Among 500 previously treated metastatic pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma patients (91.8% RAS G12), oral RAS(ON) multiselective inhibitor daraxonrasib vs investigator-choice chemotherapy yielded median overall survival 13.2 vs 6.7 months (HR=0.40, P<0.001) and median PFS 7.2 vs 3.6 months; treatment-related discontinuation 1.2% vs 11.2%. FDA expanded access was authorized May 1; the sponsor cites planned expedited review.
**Links:**
- [NEJM — Daraxonrasib or Chemotherapy in Previously Treated Metastatic Pancreatic Cancer](https://www.nejm.org/doi/10.1056/NEJMoa2605555)

## III. Climate & Environment

### 8. Communications Earth & Environment: Antarctic intermediate water ventilation spiked when AMOC weakened ~39,400 years ago
**Summary:** Nadar et al. (Communications Earth & Environment, published May 24, 2026; DOI 10.1038/s43247-026-03659-w) reconstructed Antarctic Intermediate Water variability at ODP Site 1233 off Chile during Antarctic Isotope Maximum 8 (~37–39.4 ka). Around 39.4 ka, bottom waters warmed, oxygen rose sharply, and carbon isotopes increased—signaling stronger ventilation and nutrient redistribution consistent with modeled AMOC weakening, thermocline deepening, and remote Southern Ocean response. Authors warn that if today’s AMOC slowdown continues, similar levers could shift oxygen, carbon, and nutrients globally and test CO₂ rise hypotheses tied to past Atlantic slowdowns.
**Links:**
- [Communications Earth & Environment — Rapid increase in Antarctic intermediate water ventilation related to past AMOC weakening](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43247-026-03659-w)
- [Earth.com — A deep ocean change near Antarctica may follow Atlantic slowdowns](https://www.earth.com/news/a-deep-ocean-change-near-antarctica-may-follow-atlantic-slowdowns-and-it-could-reshape-climate-patterns/)

**Commentary:** Sediments turn the modeled North Atlantic–Southern Ocean coupling into a detectable, rapid paleo fact—an anchor for assessing modern AMOC decline.

### 9. Nature Communications: anthropogenic warming advances global flood timing by ~0.43 days per 0.5°C
**Summary:** Qi et al. (Nature Communications, published May 29, 2026; DOI 10.1038/s41467-026-73839-x) used multi-model ensembles for 1.5°C–4.0°C warming. Global flood timing advances ~0.43±0.25 days per 0.5°C anthropogenic warming, with regional divergence (early-flood regions earlier, late-flood regions later). At 1.5°C, ~50.73±4.23% of global land sees flood-timing shifts >7 days, rising to ~52.85±3.20% at 2.0°C; exposed populations are projected to grow in countries including China, India, and the United States. The authors urge treating timing alongside magnitude and frequency in adaptation planning.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — Anthropogenic climate change accelerates the onset of global flood timing](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-73839-x)

**Commentary:** Flood risk is not only “bigger and more often” but “sooner or later”—agriculture, urban drainage, and insurance must add a temporal dimension.

### 10. Nature: temporary CDR can physically offset methane, not long-lived CO₂
**Summary:** He et al. (Nature, published May 27, 2026; DOI 10.1038/s41586-026-10607-3) propose a physics-based “two-basket” framework: because methane and temporary carbon storage both act on decadal-to-centennial climate, temporary CDR (e.g., ~20-year bioplastics, ~100-year durable wood) can compensate methane; neutralizing 1 kg CH₄ requires storing ~498 kg CO₂ for 20 years or ~101 kg for 100 years. Temporary CDR cannot fully offset centennial CO₂; continuous agricultural methane needs continuously matched CDR, with safeguards against reversal and moral hazard.
**Links:**
- [Nature — Temporary carbon dioxide removal to offset short-lived climate forcers](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10607-3)
- [Phys.org — Temporary carbon removal could help support climate goals, if used correctly](https://phys.org/news/2026-05-temporary-carbon-climate-goals.html)

**Commentary:** The study draws scientific boundaries for livestock-heavy economies while closing loopholes that would use short-lived storage credits against fossil CO₂.

### 12. Cortical Labs trains ~200,000 human neurons on CL1 chip to play Doom
**Summary:** Per Phys.org and AFP/Straits Times (May 31, 2026), Melbourne-based Cortical Labs cultured ~200,000 human neurons from blood-derived stem cells on a multi-electrode CL1 chip, encoding Doom’s 3D environment as electrical stimulation; closed-loop feedback yielded adaptive navigation, aiming, and firing within about a week. The firm launched a commercial “biological computer” in March 2025 after earlier Pong demos; cells live ~six months, outputs remain unstable and not fully programmable, but the team cites far lower energy than comparable silicon workloads and drug-screening use cases.
