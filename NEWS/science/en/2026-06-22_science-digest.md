## I. Space & Deep Space

### 1. Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope arrives at Kennedy Space Center: final ~70-day campaign before late-August launch
**Summary:** Per Spaceflight Now on June 22, 2026, NASA's Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope reached Florida's Kennedy Space Center aboard the Pegasus barge on the evening of June 21 and was transferred to the Payload Hazardous Servicing Facility. The ~13 m (43 ft) observatory will undergo roughly 70 days of checkouts, fueling, and fairing encapsulation before launching no earlier than August 30 on a SpaceX Falcon Heavy from Pad 39A — about one month ahead of the original September target. Roman carries a 300-megapixel Wide Field Instrument (18 detectors) plus a coronagraph, will operate from Sun–Earth L2 for at least five years (propellant may support 10+), and is designed to survey billions of galaxies and exoplanets roughly 100× wider and ~1,000× faster than Hubble while probing dark matter and dark energy.

## II. Climate & Energy

### 3. KIT and DLR release PAINT, the first open operational database for a solar power tower: 849 GB from Jülich
**Summary:** Per pv magazine on June 22, 2026, and a *Nature Energy* paper published June 16 (Phipps, Kuhl, et al.), Karlsruhe Institute of Technology and the German Aerospace Center launched PAINT (Power Plant Artificial Intelligence and Networked Technology) — the first FAIR-compliant open database of concentrating solar power tower operations. It contains ~849 GB from Germany's Jülich Solar Tower (Dec 2020–Jun 2024), including kinematics for 2,014 heliostats, >218,000 calibration images, mirror warping measurements, and 1-second on-site weather plus German Weather Service neighbor-station records. The team aims to enable digital twins, AI calibration, and predictive maintenance while building multi-plant data standards.
**Links:**
- [pv magazine — KIT, DLR publish dataset from German solar power tower](https://www.pv-magazine.com/2026/06/22/researchers-publish-dataset-from-german-solar-power-tower/)
- [Nature Energy — The PAINT database for operational concentrating solar power plant data following FAIR data principles](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41560-026-02070-1)

**Commentary:** Solar towers have long been data-starved — a shared operational corpus could finally let AI heliostat optimization graduate from simulation to reproducible engineering.

### 10. ETH Zurich team identifies self-feeding GRK2 "protein trap" in Alzheimer's; Compound Ten breaks the loop in mice
**Summary:** Per Earth.com on June 22, 2026, and a *Cell Reports Medicine* paper, Professor Ursula Quitterer's team at ETH Zurich reports that inactive, chemically switched-off GRK2 enzyme variants accumulate in Alzheimer's brains, clump onto mitochondria, choke pores, drain energy, and further raise amyloid-beta — a self-reinforcing loop distinct from plaque-centric therapies. Screening yielded Compound Ten, which blocks inactive GRK2 aggregation, restores mitochondrial function, reduces Aβ deposits, and improves neuron survival; treated mice lived longer with better heart function. Compound Ten is patented; the group is seeking industry partners for human trials.

## IV. Basic Research & Life Sciences

### 11. *Nature*: developing neurons routinely incur DNA double-strand breaks during confined migration — repaired within ~24 hours
**Summary:** Per *Nature* online June 22, 2026 (Kyoto University WPI-iCeMS Mineko Kengaku et al.) and Asia Research News, newborn neurons squeezing through dense tissue during cortical development undergo widespread DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) from mechanical stress, often without nuclear envelope rupture. Microchannel experiments show DSBs forming in narrow passages and clearing within ~24 hours via topoisomerase IIβ–linked lesions and non-homologous end joining. Deleting ligase IV causes persistent DSBs in cerebellar granule neurons and mild motor deficits in adulthood, suggesting unrepaired developmental damage may pose later neurological risk.
**Links:**
- [Nature — Confined migration induces non-lethal DNA damage in developing neurons](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10648-8)
- [Asia Research News — DNA in neurons is damaged and repaired during brain cortex formation](https://www.asiaresearchnews.com/content/dna-neurons-damaged-and-repaired-during-brain-cortex-formation)

**Commentary:** Brain development runs a genome stress test during migration — if repairable DSBs are physiological, genome-instability syndromes gain a mechanical developmental explanation for neuronal vulnerability.

### 12. *Nature Communications*: anion-exchange fluorination enables 5.0 V all-solid-state Li batteries retaining >77.5% capacity after 2,000 cycles
**Summary:** Per *Nature Communications* published June 22, 2026 (Li, Yao, Wang, et al.), a solid-state anion-exchange strategy produces core–shell Li-fluoride/LiCl nanocomposite precursors yielding heavily fluorinated lithium-halide and lithium-sulfide electrolytes with high ionic conductivity and oxidative stability against high-voltage oxide cathodes. All-solid-state cells with Li-rich oxide cathodes retain >77.5% capacity after 2,000 cycles at 3 C (1 C = 275 mA/g) and 5.0 V cutoff, attributed to a self-limiting LiF-rich interphase at the cathode. Authors say the approach generalizes to other fluorinated material systems.
**Links:**
- [Nature Communications — Anion-exchange fluorinated ion conductors for stable high-voltage lithium battery](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-74773-8)

**Commentary:** Solid-state bottlenecks often sit at high-voltage interfaces, not bulk conductivity — anion-exchange fluorination couples interphase chemistry and bulk synthesis in one reproducible 5 V pathway.

### 13. "Ballista spider" discovered in Australia: conical snare catapults green ants at ~130 g
**Summary:** Per New Scientist on June 22, 2026, and a *Current Biology* paper (Macquarie University's Ajay Narendra et al.), a newly described Australian spider builds a species-specific conical tension web targeting green tree ants (Oecophylla smaragdina). After up to four hours setting 15–60 tension lines post-dusk and applying chemicals that trigger ant attacks, struggling ants release an anchor and are flung ~30 cm upward at accelerations up to ~1,367 m/s² (~130 g) into the main web — likely avoiding ground-level colony counterattacks. DOI: 10.1016/j.cub.2026.04.066.
**Links:**
