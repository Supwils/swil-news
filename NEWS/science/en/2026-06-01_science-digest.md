## II. Biomedicine & Health

### 2. RASolute 302 keeps dominating headlines: daraxonrasib median OS 13.2 vs 6.7 months in second-line mPDAC
**Summary:** Per ScienceAlert (AP), ABC News, and Revolution Medicines (June 1, 2026), global randomized Phase 3 RASolute 302 (NCT06625320) results continue to reverberate after an ASCO plenary and simultaneous NEJM publication. Among 500 patients with previously treated metastatic pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma randomized 1:1 to oral RAS(ON) multi-selective inhibitor daraxonrasib or investigator’s-choice chemotherapy, intent-to-treat median overall survival (OS) was 13.2 vs 6.7 months (hazard ratio ~0.40), median progression-free survival (PFS) 7.2 vs 3.6 months, and treatment-related discontinuation 1.2% vs 11.2%. Dana-Farber’s Brian Wolpin called for a new second-line standard and exploration in earlier disease and surgery windows; FDA expanded access was authorized May 1, with expedited review planned.
**Links:**
- [ScienceAlert — Breakthrough Pill Nearly Doubles Survival Time For One of The Deadliest Cancers](https://www.sciencealert.com/breakthrough-pill-nearly-doubles-survival-time-for-one-of-the-deadliest-cancers)
- [NEJM — Daraxonrasib or Chemotherapy in Previously Treated Metastatic Pancreatic Cancer](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2605555)

**Commentary:** With >90% of PDAC RAS-driven and long “undruggable,” the first Phase 3 RAS(ON) inhibitor to beat chemotherapy in second line could reset standards and force earlier-line and perioperative trials.

### 3. HARMONi-6 plenary: ivonescimab (PD-1/VEGF bispecific) plus chemo improves first-line sq-NSCLC OS (HR=0.66)
**Summary:** Per BioSpace, Akeso, and PRNewswire (June 1, 2026), Phase III HARMONi-6/AK112-306 OS data were presented at the 2026 ASCO plenary: 532 treatment-naïve advanced squamous NSCLC patients received ivonescimab plus chemotherapy vs tislelizumab plus chemotherapy. Prespecified interim analysis showed a 34% reduction in death risk (HR=0.66, 95% CI 0.50–0.87, P=0.0017), median OS 27.9 vs 23.7 months, 24-month OS rates 64.7% vs 48.6%, median follow-up ~21.4 months. Prior PFS analysis showed medians 11.1 vs 6.9 months (HR=0.60). The trial is described as the first Phase III in lung cancer to show statistically significant OS and PFS benefits versus PD-1 plus chemotherapy.

### 4. LIBRETTO-432 at ASCO plenary and in NEJM: adjuvant selpercatinib cuts early RET fusion-positive NSCLC EFS risk by 83%
**Summary:** Per Lilly, BioSpace, and Newswise (June 1, 2026), global Phase III LIBRETTO-432 was presented at ASCO and published in NEJM: 151 stage IB–IIIA RET fusion-positive NSCLC patients after definitive locoregional therapy were randomized to selpercatinib or placebo for up to three years. In the primary population (stage II–IIIA, n=109), investigator-assessed event-free survival (EFS) improved sharply, with an 83% lower risk of recurrence or death (HR=0.17, P<0.001); 24-month EFS rates were 92% vs 61%, median EFS not reached vs 31.8 months. Results were consistent in the overall IB–IIIA population. RET fusions account for roughly 1%–2% of NSCLC; no approved adjuvant targeted therapy existed previously.

### 5. ETH Zurich and University of Basel: phage-steered triple therapy clears gut E. coli K1 in pregnancy, cutting neonatal meningitis transmission
**Summary:** Per ETH Zurich (June 1, 2026), neonatal meningitis pathogen E. coli K1 often colonizes adult intestines and can pass to preterm infants at birth. Because the K1 capsule resembles human cells, conventional vaccines are difficult. Researchers combined phages isolated from Lucerne wastewater treatment effluent (forcing bacteria to shed the K1 capsule), oral vaccination to raise intestinal antibodies, and a benign probiotic to occupy the niche. In infected pregnant mice, transmission fell from 83% of offspring in controls to 23% with triple therapy. Results appear in Nature Communications (2026, 17:4363, DOI 10.1038/s41467-026-70808-2); a human swallowable capsule is planned, with extension to other resistant gut pathogens.
**Links:**
- [ETH Zurich — Protection for newborns: new treatment aims to prevent meningitis without antibiotics](https://ethz.ch/en/news-and-events/eth-news/news/2026/06/protection-for-newborns-new-treatment-aims-to-prevent-meningitis-without-antibiotics.html)
- [Nature Communications — Phage-steering permits antibody-mediated clearance of E. coli K1 from the gut](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-70808-2)

**Commentary:** Linking phage-driven decapsulation, mucosal immunity, and ecological displacement offers an engineerable, antibiotic-sparing path for neonatal infection control.

## IV. Fundamental Research

### 8. ASKAP J1745−5051: PhD-led team confirms long-period radio transients from accreting white dwarf binary
**Summary:** Per Phys.org and the University of Sydney (June 1, 2026), an international team led by PhD student Kovi Rose using CSIRO’s ASKAP radio telescope identified rare binary ASKAP J1745−5051: an Earth-sized, near-solar-mass white dwarf accreting from a ~0.1 solar-mass red dwarf, 1.4-hour orbit, producing periodic radio and X-ray bursts. This is the clearest evidence yet that some long-period radio transients (LPRTs)—only about a dozen known—come from cataclysmic variables rather than slow neutron-star pulsars. It is the second LPRT with regular X-rays and the first with both stellar components and accretion clearly resolved as the periodicity driver. Published in Nature Astronomy (2026, DOI 10.1038/s41550-026-02882-x).
**Links:**

**Commentary:** A Rosetta Stone for decoding LPRTs—and a repeatable window into extreme plasma and magnetic interactions beyond Earth-bound labs.

### 9. TransCODE Consortium in Nature defines “peptideins,” surfacing 1,700+ dark-proteome candidates
**Summary:** Per Nature, EMBL, and EurekAlert, the international TransCODE Consortium integrated 95,520 proteomics experiments, finding protein-level evidence for ~25% of 7,264 non-canonical open reading frames (ncORFs). It introduces “peptideins”—translated products of indeterminate function between known proteins and non-coding sequence—with 1,785 ncORFs supported in HLA-I immunopeptidomics builds. CRISPR screens show an OLMALINC-derived peptidein essential in ~85% of 485 cancer cell lines tested. Data are public via PeptideAtlas with GENCODE/UniProt annotation pathways. Published in Nature (2026, DOI 10.1038/s41586-026-10459-x).
