## I. Cosmology & gravity

### 1. Testing the gravitational force law on cosmological scales: ACT pairwise kSZ + SDSS galaxies favors an inverse-square-like scaling and tightens constraints on MOND-like alternatives (cosmology | dark matter)
**Summary:** A large international team combines **Atacama Cosmology Telescope (ACT)** cosmic microwave background intensity maps with **Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS)** galaxies to estimate mean pairwise velocities via the **kinematic Sunyaev–Zeldovich (kSZ)** effect, constraining how gravitational acceleration scales with separation over roughly **30–230 Mpc**. The analysis reports a power-law index **n = 2.1 ± 0.3 (68% credible interval)**, consistent with Newtonian gravity embedded in a standard **ΛCDM** dark-matter picture, and discusses exclusion pressure on **MOND**-like scenarios, with forecasts for stronger tests as galaxy samples grow. The work is posted as **arXiv:2604.14327**; a popular summary appeared on **May 16, 2026**.
**Links:**
- [arXiv — The Atacama Cosmology Telescope: A Test of the Gravitational Force Law on Cosmological Scales Using the Kinematic Sunyaev-Zeldovich Effect](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14327)
- [Technology.org — Scientists test a fundamental rule of gravity on cosmic scales — and it holds up (2026-05-16)](https://www.technology.org/2026/05/16/scientists-test-a-fundamental-rule-of-gravity-on-cosmic-scales-and-it-holds-up/)
**Commentary:** Pairwise-velocity probes are unusually direct about “does the acceleration field match the matter field?”—but the scientific payoff still hinges on how well systematics, tracer bias, and alternative-gravity nuances (including external-field subtleties) are modeled.

## II. Earth science (earthquakes & plate boundaries)

### 2. *Science*: structural “rupture barriers” on the Gofar oceanic transform fault stop ~M6 ruptures quasi-periodically via damage-enhanced porosity and dilatancy strengthening (marine seismology)
**Summary:** By analyzing ocean-bottom seismometer experiments from **2008** and **2019–2022**, the authors show two barrier regions along the **Gofar** transform hosted intense microseismicity before major **~M6** earthquakes, then quieted abruptly afterward. The barriers coincide with multistrand fault architecture and **100–400 m** transtensional stepovers, favoring **dilatancy strengthening** as a repeatable rupture terminator—helping explain why this fault produces similar-sized earthquakes every **~5–6 years** in nearly the same places. The research article is in *Science* (**DOI: 10.1126/science.ady6190**); **WHOI** and partners issued a press explainer on **May 14, 2026**.
**Links:**
- [Science — Predictable seismic cycles result from structural rupture barriers on oceanic transform faults](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.ady6190)
- [Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution — Researchers help solve mystery of clockwork-like earthquake system deep beneath the Pacific (2026-05-14)](https://www.whoi.edu/press-room/news-release/gofar-earthquakes-copy/)
**Commentary:** Turning “why do many oceanic transform earthquakes stay smaller than you’d guess?” into a testable structural–fluid mechanism matters for how tsunami and rupture-length models treat poorly instrumented offshore faults.

## III. Space & astronomical instrumentation

### 3. NASA: an ultra-thin, ultra-black coating stack for **starshade** edges and membranes could suppress solar glint enough to enable future exoplanet imaging (astrobiology | stray light)
**Summary:** A **May 12, 2026** NASA Science highlight describes a **ZeCoat** ultra-black thin-film architecture that uses resonant absorption in nanoscale metal/dielectric stacks—roughly **two orders of magnitude thinner** than some prior candidates—and reports **JPL** scatterometer tests on **50 cm** amorphous-metal blades showing scattered light reduced by about **20×**. The article frames the technology as part of maturing **starshade** edge and membrane treatments for extremely high star–planet contrast, with connections to future mission concepts such as the **Habitable Worlds Observatory**.
**Links:**
- [NASA Science — New Ultra-Black Coating Could Enable the Search for Life on Exoplanets (2026-05-12)](https://science.nasa.gov/science-research/science-enabling-technology/technology-highlights/new-ultra-black-coating-could-enable-the-search-for-life-on-exoplanets/)

**Commentary:** Direct imaging is often won or lost in nanometers of edge scatter; blackening that stays thin preserves sharp petal geometry while buying contrast in the starlight budget.

### 4. Biogen: Phase 2 **CELIA** topline for tau-targeting ASO **diranersen (BIIB080)**—strong tau/PET signals and cognitive analyses, but the primary dose-response endpoint missed (Alzheimer’s disease)
**Summary:** On **May 14, 2026**, Biogen announced **CELIA** results: a **76-week**, **416-participant** randomized, double-blind, dose-ranging study in early Alzheimer’s disease that **did not meet its primary endpoint** assessing dose response on **CDR-SB** at week **76**. ### 5. *Science* news: a large **Bundibugyo ebolavirus** outbreak in northern DRC raises alarm—field tests can miss the species, and no licensed vaccines/therapies exist (public health)
**Summary:** A **May 16, 2026** *Science* article reports the DRC Ministry of Health announcement of an **Ituri** outbreak caused by **Ebola Bundibugyo**, with **246** suspected cases and **80** deaths at the time of reporting, suggesting weeks of undetected spread. **Links:**

## VI. Climate, energy & environment

### 6. IRENA: new analysis argues firm **24/7 renewable** hybrids are already cost-competitive with new fossil generation in high-quality wind/solar resource regions (energy transition)
**Summary:** An IRENA press release dated **May 6, 2026** (Abu Dhabi) accompanies the report **“24/7 renewables: The economics of firm solar and wind”**, claiming firm hybrid **solar/wind + storage** levelized costs of roughly **USD 54–82/MWh** in premium resource regions versus about **USD 70–85/MWh** for new coal in China and **> USD 100/MWh** for new gas in many markets, with projected further declines toward **2030** and **2035**. The messaging emphasizes reliability for demanding loads (including data centers) and clean fuels where high utilization matters.
**Links:**
- [IRENA — 24/7 Renewables Outcompete Fossil Fuels on Costs (Press release, 2026-05-06)](https://www.irena.org/News/pressreleases/2026/May/24-7-Renewables-Outcompete-Fossil-Fuels-on-Firm-Costs)

**Commentary:** LCOE competitiveness is necessary but not sufficient—grid rules, interconnection, and mineral supply chains still decide whether “firm renewables” become firm in practice.

### 7. *Nature Sustainability*: coal-related aerosols co-located with PV fleets systematically suppress global solar output—**~5.8% (111 TWh)** aerosol-induced loss in **2023** (energy–environment coupling)
**Summary:** Using a facility-level global PV dataset (**140,945** sites) integrated with reanalysis and a validated PV performance model, the authors partition losses from clouds versus aerosols, reporting **~5.8%** global generation loss from aerosols in **2023** (**111 TWh**), with **China** showing **~7.7%** national loss from aerosols and large “loss-to-growth” ratios in several years. The paper argues slow coal phase-out creates a persistent headwind that can bias upward estimates of solar’s climate and air-quality dividends unless co-emissions and co-location are accounted for.
**Links:**
- [Nature Sustainability — Coal plants persist as a large barrier to the global solar energy transition](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41893-026-01836-5)
**Commentary:** This reframes “coal vs solar” from a carbon accounting story into an irradiance-and-revenue engineering story—cleaner air and better capacity factors can move together, but not automatically.

## VII. Chemistry & molecular science

### 8. MIT: synthesis and isolation of **dioxaborirane**, a strained boron–oxygen three-membered peroxide ring formed rapidly at room temperature (boron chemistry)
**Summary:** A **May 13, 2026** MIT News feature describes a new boron–oxygen ring made of **one boron and two oxygens**, long considered too unstable to isolate, now generated almost instantaneously at **room temperature** from an engineered boron precursor and **O₂**. The open-access *Nature Chemistry* paper (**DOI: 10.1038/s41557-026-02120-x**) highlights dual reactivity depending on charge state—oxygen donation for synthesis and reactivity with **CO₂**—with downstream implications for oxidation toolkit design.
