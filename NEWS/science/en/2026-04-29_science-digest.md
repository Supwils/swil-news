## I. Spaceflight & Advanced Propulsion

### 1. **NASA / JPL:** Lithium-fed **MPD** electric thruster hits **120 kW** on the ground — stepping toward Mars-class nuclear–electric bundles (deep-space propulsion)
**Summary:** On **April 28, 2026**, **NASA** announced that engineers at **JPL** repeatedly ignited a **lithium-vapor-fed magnetoplasmadynamic (MPD)** prototype inside the **CoMeT** vacuum facility in Southern California at up to **~120 kW**—more than **25×** the electric-thruster power now flying on **Psyche**, currently NASA’s highest-power electric mission. Testing on **Feb. 24, 2026**, pushed central electrodes beyond **5,000 °F**, part of a path toward **0.5–1 MW per unit** and, eventually, **multi-megawatt** duty cycles for crewed Mars stacks. Administrator **Jared Isaacman** called it the **first U.S. demonstration near that class**, though pairing with **nuclear electric power** remains essential for large human Mars payloads.

**Commentary:** The headline is moving **kilowatt-class electric propulsion experience** into the **same design space as human Mars mass budgets and cumulative operating hours**; the next bottleneck is **long-duration reliability, materials, and nuclear–electric interfaces**.
