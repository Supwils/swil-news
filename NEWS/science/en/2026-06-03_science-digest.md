## I. Space & Deep Space

### 1. Sun emits X1.0 flare; NASA warns of comms, grid, and spaceflight impacts
**Summary:** NASA Science reported on June 3, 2026, that the Sun produced a strong flare peaking at 7:28 a.m. ET. The Solar Dynamics Observatory captured the event in extreme ultraviolet. The burst is classified as X1.0—the most intense X-class category. Flares and related eruptions can disrupt radio communications, electric power grids, navigation signals, and increase radiation risk to spacecraft and astronauts. Operational forecasts are issued by NOAA’s Space Weather Prediction Center while NASA maintains continuous solar monitoring.

- [NASA Science — Strong Flare Erupts from Sun](https://science.nasa.gov/blogs/solar-cycle-25/2026/06/03/strong-flare-erupts-from-sun-10/)

**Commentary:** With crewed deep-space missions and large LEO constellations expanding, each X-class event stress-tests space-weather alerting and on-orbit protection strategies.

### 3. Accreting white dwarf binary confirmed as a “Rosetta stone” for long-period radio transients
**Summary:** Space.com and Nature Astronomy (June 1–2, 2026) report that Kovi Rose’s University of Sydney–led team used CSIRO’s ASKAP telescope to tie long-period radio transient ASKAP J1745−5051 to a magnetic cataclysmic variable: a Sun-mass white dwarf accreting from a red-dwarf companion on a ~1.3-hour orbit, with modulated X-rays and ~1.4-hour repeating polarized radio bursts. The match provides the strongest evidence yet that at least some LPTs originate in white-dwarf binaries rather than slow magnetars. Published in Nature Astronomy (DOI: 10.1038/s41550-026-02882-x).
**Links:**
- [Space.com — Scientists locate source of mysterious radio signals: a vampire star and its victim](https://www.space.com/astronomy/stars/scientists-locate-source-of-mysterious-radio-signals-after-20-year-search-a-vampire-star-and-its-victim)
- [Nature Astronomy — Periodic radio and X-ray emission from an accreting white dwarf binary](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41550-026-02882-x)

**Commentary:** Anchoring “mystery repeating radio” to observable binary accretion physics gives astronomers a reusable template to classify other transient populations.

## II. Biomedicine & Health

### 4. Shionogi’s ensitrelvir (Xocova) wins FDA nod as first U.S. oral COVID post-exposure prophylaxis
**Summary:** Shionogi announced on June 1, 2026 (widely covered through June 3) that the FDA approved Xocova (ensitrelvir) for post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) in people aged 12+ after contact with someone who has COVID-19—the first and only U.S. oral option in that setting, ahead of the June 16 PDUFA date. In Phase 3 SCORPIO-PEP, starting within 72 hours of household exposure, symptomatic infection risk fell 67% through Day 10 versus placebo (ensitrelvir n=1,030; placebo n=1,011) with similar adverse-event rates; results appear in the New England Journal of Medicine. U.S. approval is for PEP only, not treatment of active infection.
**Links:**

### 7. First genetically modified human hookworm secretes anti-tetrodotoxin antibody in hosts
**Summary:** Phys.org (June 3, 2026) reports Washington University School of Medicine researchers achieved stable transgenesis of human hookworm to secrete a human anti-tetrodotoxin (TTX) single-chain antibody during long-term gut colonization; blood from infected hamsters partially neutralized TTX versus controls. Hookworms infect hundreds of millions worldwide and naturally persist for years while secreting immunomodulatory molecules—this proof-of-concept points to “living biofactories” for remote toxin exposure or continuous biologics. Published in Nature Communications (DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-73447-9).

## III. Climate, Energy & Environment

### 11. Mesoscale-eddy hotspot drives ~1.5 °C per century warming in high-latitude Southern Ocean sector
**Summary:** Nature Climate Change (published June 3, 2026) combines observations and reanalysis to show accelerated surface warming of ~1.5 °C per century during 1982–2023 in 50°S–61°S, 80°E–130°E—about three times the high-latitude Southern Ocean mean (~0.5 °C/century) and near the global ~1.6 °C/century rate. Analyses link the hotspot to stronger Antarctic Circumpolar Current and enhanced upward heat transport by mesoscale eddies; models that under-resolve eddies may underestimate regional warming and ice-shelf risks.
**Links:**
- [Nature Climate Change — High-latitude Southern Ocean warming hotspot induced by ocean mesoscale eddies](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41558-026-02652-7)

**Commentary:** “Delayed Southern Ocean warming” is not uniform—local eddy-driven hotspots demand finer-scale climate risk accounting.

### 13. Bean inceptin receptor detects caterpillar spit and recruits predatory wasps
**Summary:** Ars Technica and Science Advances (June 2026) describe University of Washington Adam Steinbrenner’s team, with field work in Oaxaca, Mexico, showing common bean INR senses the In11 peptide in caterpillar oral secretions, triggers herbivore-specific defenses, and emits a volatile blend attracting Polybia and Mischocyttarus wasps. Plants lacking INR saw ~40% fewer wasp attacks and >70% faster caterpillar growth. Published in Science Advances (DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.aec3229).
