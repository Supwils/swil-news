**Summary:** The Orion capsule carrying Wiseman, Glover, Koch, and Hansen splashed down safely off San Diego at approximately 5:07 PM Pacific on April 10, 2026, completing humanity's first crewed lunar return since Apollo 17 in 1972. ### 3. Northwestern: Printable "Artificial Neurons" Directly Activate Real Mouse Brain Cells Ex Vivo
**Summary:** Mark C. Hersam's team at Northwestern fabricated devices using molybdenum disulfide and graphene inks via aerosol jet printing on flexible substrates, generating spike and burst patterns matching biological neuron timescales through controlled polymer degradation forming narrow conductive channels. In mouse brain tissue experiments, the artificial unit's electrical output could induce responses in adjacent live neurons. Reported work was published in Nature Nanotechnology, with discussion of applications in brain-machine interfaces, neural prosthetics, and low-power neuromorphic hardware.
**Links:**
- [Interesting Engineering — New printed artificial neurons can directly activate real brain cells](https://interestingengineering.com/innovation/printed-artificial-neurons-activate-brain-cells)
**Commentary:** The critical advance is not "resembling a neuron" but "able to communicate with tissue." If long-term biocompatibility and packaging problems can be solved, the flexible printed route may conform to irregular cortical geometry more readily than silicon-based arrays.

## III. Climate, Energy, and Environment

### 5. Nature Energy: PROLONG Model Gives Probabilistic Global Wind and Solar Deployment Pathways — Close to 2°C Scenarios but 1.5°C Still Tight
**Summary:** A research team built the probabilistic model PROLONG based on multi-country historical capacity installation trajectories and "growth pulse" patterns, projecting global onshore wind and solar generation share through 2050. In the central scenario, onshore wind would reach approximately 13.4% (IQR ~12.6-14.3%) of global generation by 2030 and approximately 26% by 2050; solar approximately 12.3% by 2030 and approximately 21% by 2050 — faster than most current-policies scenarios and approaching IPCC 2°C-compatible pathways, but still below many 1.5°C trajectories. The COP28 "triple renewables" commitment falls at approximately the 95th percentile of the distribution, requiring several times faster wind-solar growth rates from major economies than recent years.
**Links:**
- [Nature Energy — Probabilistic projections of global wind and solar power growth based on historical national experience](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41560-026-02021-w)
**Commentary:** Converting "political rhetoric" into a probabilistic statement about "which growth curve is needed" is the most lacking public good for the next round of NDC revisions and industry financing stress tests.

### 6. Nature Energy: Incorporating PV Rebound Effect into European Power System Optimization Reveals Additional Investment Needs and Cost Pass-Through
**Summary:** Researchers embedded empirical solar rebound effect (SRE) intensity values from rooftop photovoltaics into the open-source European power market model E2M2s, generating time-varying load curves. Results showed rebounds not only raise electricity consumption but also increase renewable investment and flexibility needs plus system costs through temporal mismatch — and may be regressive (rebound-driven households may not bear the full system cost). The authors called for explicit SRE modeling in decarbonization scenarios and grid planning, using load-shifting incentives and efficiency policies as offsetting tools.
**Links:**
- [Nature Energy — Implications of the solar rebound effect for the European energy transition](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41560-026-02031-8)
**Commentary:** "Marginal electricity prices near zero" does not automatically mean the system is cheap — if behavioral feedbacks are ignored, policymakers will systematically underestimate distribution network and peak resource bills.

### 9. LHC CMS Collaboration Achieves Ultra-Precise W Boson Mass Measurement: 80360.2 ± 9.9 MeV — Consistent with Standard Model
**Summary:** The international CMS collaboration analyzed over 1 billion proton-proton collision events, identifying approximately 100 million W→μν events, giving a new W boson mass measurement of 80360.2 ± 9.9 MeV. MIT highlighted contributions from Kenneth Long, Christoph Paus, and others in the analysis pipeline; the paper appeared in Nature.

### 10. IISc and Japan NIMS: Quantum Critical "Dirac Fluid" Charge-Heat Transport in Ultra-Clean Graphene — Deviates >200x from Wiedemann-Franz Law (Nature Physics DOI: 10.1038/s41567-025-02972-z)
**Summary:** Majumdar and collaborators prepared extremely low-defect graphene samples and simultaneously measured electrical and thermal conductance near the Dirac point, finding them changing inversely with tuning — systematically violating the Wiedemann-Franz law governing proportional electrical-thermal transport in metals, with low-temperature deviation exceeding 200-fold. Electron microscopy and transport interpretation pointed toward collective electron behavior, a nearly inviscid fluid picture, correlated with universal conductance quantum scaling. Results appeared in Nature Physics, with IISc/ScienceDaily science communication releases around April 15.
**Links:**
- [ScienceDaily — Graphene just defied a fundamental law of physics](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260415042152.htm)
- [Nature Physics — Universality in quantum critical flow of charge and heat in ultraclean graphene](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-025-02972-z)
**Commentary:** Desktop material reproducing near-"perfect fluid" limit quantum transport provides a controllable sandbox for strongly correlated electron gas and relativistic fluid analogies, and may inspire thermal management redesigns for low-dissipation quantum devices.
