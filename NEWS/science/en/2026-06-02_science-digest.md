## II. Biomedicine & Health

### 3. Rice INTACT platform: blood draw tracks targeted gene transcription in living brain
**Summary:** Per News-Medical and EurekAlert on June 2, 2026, Jerzy Szablowski’s Rice team combined Released Markers of Activity (RMAs) with RNA transcription sensors in the IN-vivo Tracking of ACtive Transcription (INTACT) platform: when target mRNA is expressed, sensors trigger RMA release into blood for simple assays. The group calls it the first nondestructive measurement of selected transcription dynamics in living tissue, enabling longitudinal tracking in the same animal before and during disease. Animal work tracked three brain regions simultaneously with a path to highly multiplexed monitoring. Published in Nature Communications (DOI: 10.1038/s41467-026-73486-2).
**Links:**
- [News-Medical — New blood test tracks real-time gene expression inside living brain tissue](https://www.news-medical.net/news/20260602/New-blood-test-tracks-real-time-gene-expression-inside-living-brain-tissue.aspx)
- [Nature Communications — Monitoring in vivo transcription with synthetic serum markers](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-73486-2)

**Commentary:** It brings NGS/qPCR-style population readouts into living longitudinal monitoring—a deployable interface for neuro drug response and circuit-activation studies.

## III. Climate, Energy & Environment

### 9. ISS EMIT mineral maps cut iron-oxide dust radiative uncertainty by more than sixfold
**Summary:** Per Phys.org, Cornell Chronicle, and BSC on June 1–2, 2026, a Cornell-led team using NASA’s EMIT instrument aboard the ISS and four Earth-system models (including BSC MONARCH) reported in Nature Geoscience that uncertainty in dust shortwave effects from iron oxides fell from 0.62 to 0.10 W/m²—over sixfold—with up to ~80% error reduction over the Sahara. Work shifts focus toward dust transport, particle size, and source changes under warming, linking to ocean fertilization, snow darkening, and clouds.
**Links:**
- [Phys.org — Space station dust maps slash climate uncertainty over iron-rich particles](https://phys.org/news/2026-06-space-station-slash-climate-uncertainty.html)
- [Nature Geoscience — Global mineral constraints on dust shortwave radiative effects](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41561-026-01996-1)

**Commentary:** Replacing model assumptions with orbital mineral constraints hardens estimates of radiative forcing from arid regions and Earth’s energy budget.

### 10. University of Birmingham: perovskite thermochemical water splitting at 150–500 °C with lower regeneration heat
**Summary:** Per ScienceDaily and the university on June 2, 2026 (paper published in May), Yulong Ding’s team used barium–niobium–calcium–iron (BNCF) perovskite catalysts to produce substantial hydrogen at 150–500 °C and regenerate at 700–1000 °C—roughly 500 °C lower than conventional thermochemical cycles (splitting 700–1000 °C; regeneration 1300–1500 °C). Preliminary techno-economics suggest costs below green (electrolysis) and blue (methane CCS) hydrogen, especially where industrial waste heat from steel, cement, and glass is available. Published in International Journal of Hydrogen Energy (DOI: 10.1016/j.ijhydene.2025.152637); patent filed.
**Links:**

## IV. Fundamental Research

### 11. Seven hot Jupiters show slower winds when hotter; Nature Astronomy points to magnetic braking
**Summary:** Per ScienceAlert and The Independent on June 2, 2026, Julia Seidel (Côte d’Azur Observatory) used Gemini North (MAROON-X) and ESO’s VLT (ESPRESSO) to measure iron-line Doppler winds (~2–7 km/s) on seven hot Jupiters—finding higher equilibrium temperature correlates with slower winds, opposite classical heat-driven circulation. Magnetic drag on ionized atmospheres best explains the trend, with inferred fields of a few gauss, comparable to Jupiter—the strongest indirect evidence yet for exoplanet magnetism. Published in Nature Astronomy.
**Links:**
- [ScienceAlert — Astronomers Have Uncovered a Strange Pattern in The Winds of Alien Worlds](https://www.sciencealert.com/astronomers-have-uncovered-a-strange-pattern-in-the-winds-of-alien-worlds)

**Commentary:** Population-level statistics rather than single-planet anecdotes give observable proxies for whether worlds retain atmospheres and water.
