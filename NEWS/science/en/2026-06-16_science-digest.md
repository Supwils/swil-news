**Summary:** Per Ars Technica and The Register on June 16, 2026, cracks in the Russian Zvezda service module transfer tunnel (PrK) have caused atmosphere leaks since 2019; in early June the leak rate rose to about two pounds per day. ## II. Climate & Environment

### 4. UCL Earth's Future: megaconstellation rocket soot lingers aloft with ~540× ground-soot climate forcing
**Summary:** Per Physics World on June 16, 2026, and a UCL-led study in Earth's Future (Connor Barker, Eloise Marais, et al.), black carbon from rocket launches and satellite reentries in 2020–2022 can persist in the upper atmosphere, with per-unit radiative forcing roughly 540 times that of ground-based soot from cars and power plants. Megaconstellation missions since 2019 accounted for about 35% of the space sector's climate impact in 2020, projected to reach 42% by 2029; at current launch cadence, annual soot emissions could reach about 870 tonnes by 2029—comparable to the UK passenger-car fleet. Soot absorbs sunlight and can produce slight surface cooling, but researchers warn of ozone loss and other poorly understood consequences—calling space-industry pollution an "unregulated geoengineering experiment."
**Links:**

**Commentary:** The "climate bill" for LEO internet constellations is now quantified at national-fleet scale—launch-frequency policy must include atmospheric chemistry and radiative forcing.

## III. Fundamental Physics & Quantum

### 6. TU Wien quantifies high multipartite entanglement in centimeter-scale strange-metal crystal
**Summary:** Per TU Wien on June 16, 2026, and Nature Physics (DOI: 10.1038/s41567-026-03298-0), Federico Mazza et al. used neutron scattering at the ILL on a centimeter CePdSi strange-metal single crystal; quantum Fisher information analysis showed neutron energy transfer cannot be explained by independent particles—at least nine entangled entities respond collectively, providing direct evidence of high multipartite entanglement in a macroscopic solid. Strange metals, with linear-in-temperature resistivity and strong electron correlations, are central to high-temperature superconductivity and quantum criticality research.
**Links:**

### 7. Oxford builds new family of Schrödinger's cat states, sculpting nonclassical motional superpositions in trapped ions
**Summary:** Per Gizmodo on June 16, 2026, and the University of Oxford Department of Physics, Sebastian Saner's team reports in Physical Review X (June 3, 2026; DOI: 10.1103/k1xk-yt42): using a single strontium-88 ion, they entangle internal spin with trap harmonic motion and project motion into coherent superpositions of squeezed, trisqueezed, and quadsqueezed nonclassical components via mid-circuit measurement; Wigner negativity confirms nonclassicality. The method controls superposition size, orientation, and geometric symmetry, expanding state space for quantum computing, sensing, and error correction.
