## I. Spaceflight & Deep Space

### 1. NASA’s Psyche probe gears up for a Mars gravity assist ahead of a metal-rich asteroid rendezvous (Spaceflight)
**Summary:** News outlets report that NASA’s Psyche spacecraft is scheduled to fly past Mars on **May 15, 2026**, passing roughly **4,500 km (~2,800 miles)** above the surface to gain a trajectory boost and conserve xenon propellant on its way to main-belt asteroid **16 Psyche** (launch **October 2023**; arrival expected around **2029**). A NASA mission blog post says the team executed a trajectory-correction maneuver and loaded May flyby sequences into the flight computer. During the encounter, the multispectral imager is expected to collect extensive Mars observations for instrument calibration; mission scientists also hope to test searches for a faint dust torus tied to Phobos/Deimos impacts and to hunt for tiny satellites near Mars as practice for later searches near Psyche.

### 2. An ultra-thin, durable “ultra-black” coating advances starshade concepts for exoplanet imaging (Astro engineering)
**Summary:** A **May 12, 2026** NASA Science article describes a ZeCoat coating built from ultrathin partially transparent metal layers separated by dielectric glass layers that form nanoscale absorbing cavities—about **100× thinner** than coatings previously evaluated for starshade edges. Under a NASA/JPL starshade partnership, coated ~**50 cm** amorphous-metal blade prototypes were measured with a custom laser scatterometer; NASA reports reflected light reduced by about **a factor of ~20**, described as sufficient to enable exoplanet imaging in that test context. The piece also notes roll-to-roll coating approaches for large polyimide membranes and potential thermal-control applications for future observatories.
**Links:**
- [NASA Science — New Ultra-Black Coating Could Enable the Search for Life on Exoplanets (2026-05-12)](https://science.nasa.gov/science-research/science-enabling-technology/technology-highlights/new-ultra-black-coating-could-enable-the-search-for-life-on-exoplanets/)

**Commentary:** Starshade performance is tightly coupled to edge stray-light suppression; pushing coatings toward thinner profiles with demonstrated scatter reductions is a key step from concept studies toward mission-ready engineering.

## II. Earth & Planetary Science

### 4. Mantle-linked helium signatures in Zambia’s Kafue Rift hot springs fuel debate over an emerging rift system (Tectonics)
**Summary:** Researchers led by **University of Oxford** geologists analyzed gases from geothermal springs in Zambia: **six** sites inside the Kafue Rift showed helium isotope ratios interpreted as consistent with **mantle-derived fluids**, while **two** sites outside the rift appeared crust-dominated. Authors argue the pattern fits early-stage lithospheric extension with an active fault network; institutional news dated **May 12, 2026** quotes scientists cautioning the study is geographically limited and that similar anomalies along other segments would be needed for stronger claims about a continent-scale emerging plate boundary.
**Links:**
- [Frontiers — New tectonic plate boundary could be forming in Zambia (2026-05-12)](https://www.frontiersin.org/news/2026/05/12/new-tectonic-plate-boundary-could-be-forming-in-zambia-frontiers-earth-sciences)
- [Frontiers in Earth Science — Original article (Karolytė et al., 2026)](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/earth-science/articles/10.3389/feart.2026.1799564/full)
**Commentary:** Gas geochemistry offers a sensitive—if interpretationally delicate—probe of whether rifting has linked surface fluids to deeper Earth reservoirs; results naturally intersect geothermal exploration priorities.

## III. Astrophysics & Fundamental Physics

### 5. A gravitational-wave waveform screen finds GW190728 marginally “prefers” a dense dark-matter environment model (Gravitational waves | Dark matter)
**Summary:** MIT News / MIT Physics (**May 11, 2026**) describes a waveform model for binary black-hole mergers occurring inside an environment of ultralight scalar dark matter, applied to **28** high-quality signals from LVK’s first three observing runs. The team reports **27** events match vacuum-merger expectations, while **GW190728** (**July 28, 2019**) shows a statistical preference for the dark-matter-environment waveform relative to their vacuum baseline. Authors emphasize this is **not** a dark-matter detection—significance is insufficient—and independent checks are needed as more data accumulate.
**Links:**

## IV. Biomedicine & Public Health

### 6. FDA approves Bizengri (zenocutuzumab-zbco) for NRG1 fusion–positive advanced cholangiocarcinoma (Oncology | Regulatory)
**Summary:** FDA labeling materials state **May 8, 2026** approval of **zenocutuzumab-zbco** (Bizengri) for adults with advanced unresectable/metastatic cholangiocarcinoma harboring **NRG1 fusions** after progression on prior systemic therapy. Efficacy was supported by cohort data from trial **eNRGy (NCT02912949)**: **22** enrolled patients with NRG1 fusion–positive cholangiocarcinoma (**19** efficacy-evaluable), blinded independent central review showed confirmed ORR **36.8%** (**95% CI: 16.3–61.6**) and duration of response roughly **2.8–12.9 months**; recommended dosing is **750 mg IV every two weeks**.
**Links:**

## VI. Exoplanet Survey Methods

### 9. TESS eclipse-timing survey finds **27** planet candidates among **1,590** eclipsing binaries (Astronomy)
**Summary:** A **May 4, 2026** NASA Science article summarizes a **UNSW Sydney–led** analysis of **≥2 years** of **TESS** data on **1,590** eclipsing binaries: eclipse-timing variations yielded **27** candidate planetary systems. Estimated candidate masses span a wide range (team-quoted lower bound around **~12 Earth masses** up to roughly **~10× Jupiter**), with confirmation requiring precise ground-based radial velocities. The underlying paper appears in *Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society* with an advance article timestamp **May 4, 2026**.
**Links:**
