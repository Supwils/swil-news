### 2. **NASA Psyche**: Mars gravity assist completed; trajectory confirmed toward metal‑rich asteroid **Psyche**
**Summary:** In a **May 19, 2026** mission update, **NASA** reports that the **Psyche** spacecraft executed a close Mars flyby on **May 15**, passing within about **2,864 miles (4,609 km)** of the surface to gain a gravity assist that adjusts orbital plane and speed without expending onboard chemical propellant. Mission operators subsequently verified trajectory via the **Deep Space Network (DSN)** and plan to resume solar‑electric propulsion toward main‑belt arrival and orbital operations at asteroid **Psyche** in **summer 2029**.
**Links:**
- [NASA — NASA’s Psyche Mission Aces Mars Flyby, Targets Metal-Rich Asteroid (2026-05-19)](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/psyche-mission/nasas-psyche-mission-aces-mars-flyby-targets-metal-rich-asteroid/)
**Commentary:** Mars assists are textbook deep‑space “time for Δv” trades; a clean flyback signal tightens propulsion and thermal margins for cruise, letting science teams focus on approach navigation and compositional mapping strategies.

### 8. **Nature**: Oldest eukaryote fossils cluster in **oxic, benthic** settings—hinting long **nearshore oxygen oases** before planktonic expansion
**Summary:** **Lechte, M.A., Riedman, L.A., Porter, S.M. et al.** publish **“Early fossil eukaryotes were benthic aerobes”** in *Nature* (**DOI: 10.1038/s41586‑026‑10533‑4**, **May 20, 2026**), integrating micropaleontology and sedimentary–geochemical analyses of **~1.75–1.4 billion‑year‑old** rocks from Australia’s **McArthur** and **Birrindudu** basins. They argue the oldest accepted eukaryote fossils are almost restricted to **oxygenated bottom waters**, consistent with aerobic benthic lifestyles and a delayed Neoproterozoic expansion into planktonic habitats—offering a geological explanation for mismatches between body fossils and some molecular clocks. **McGill University** issued a companion news story the same day.
