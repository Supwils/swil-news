### 2. CLARREO Pathfinder installed on the ISS: a hyperspectral calibration benchmark for Earth and Moon reflectance
**Summary:** A NASA science blog dated May 26, 2026 reports that the CLARREO Pathfinder instrument was installed on the International Space Station’s ExPRESS Logistics Carrier-1 on May 22, following launch May 15 aboard SpaceX’s 34th Commercial Resupply Services mission and arrival May 17. The payload is described as a hyperspectral imaging spectrometer intended to measure reflected sunlight from Earth and the Moon with roughly 5–10× improved accuracy versus many existing sensors, using a two-axis gimbal to point at the Sun, Moon, and Earth. After robotic extraction from the Dragon trunk, operators completed launch-lock release and initial pointing-motion tests; commissioning and science checks are expected over the coming months to assess its viability for cross-calibrating government and commercial satellite fleets.

**Links:**

- [NASA Science — NASA Calibration Instrument Installed on International Space Station](https://science.nasa.gov/blogs/clarreo-pathfinder/2026/05/26/nasa-calibration-instrument-installed-on-international-space-station/)

**Commentary:** An in-orbit radiometric “ruler,” if validated operationally, could reduce structural biases when stitching multi-decadal Earth observation records—foundational for climate monitoring and commercial constellation interoperability.

### 3. NASA’s Psyche spacecraft completes a Mars flyby, shaping trajectory toward a metal-rich asteroid
**Summary:** In a May 19, 2026 mission update, NASA states the Psyche spacecraft executed a Mars gravity assist on May 15, passing within about 2,864 miles (4,609 km) of the surface to gain speed and adjust orbital inclination without large propellant expenditure, with trajectory confirmed afterward via the Deep Space Network. The spacecraft is returning to solar-electric propulsion toward a planned arrival at the asteroid Psyche in August 2029 for orbital science, investigating a body thought to preserve clues about early planetary differentiation.

**Links:**

- [NASA — NASA’s Psyche Mission Aces Mars Flyby, Targets Metal-Rich Asteroid](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/psyche-mission/nasas-psyche-mission-aces-mars-flyby-targets-metal-rich-asteroid/)

**Commentary:** Gravity assists are orbital-mechanics “free lunch” maneuvers—trading calendar time for propellant margin that pays off at the science-intensive endgame.

### 6. FDA grants accelerated approval to Gilead’s Hepcludex (bulevirtide-gmod) for chronic hepatitis delta virus (HDV)
**Summary:** Gilead announced on May 22, 2026 that the FDA granted accelerated approval to Hepcludex 8.5 mg once daily (subcutaneous) for adults with chronic HDV infection—the first FDA-approved therapy specifically for HDV in the United States. The company states approval relied primarily on reductions in HDV RNA and ALT normalization from the pivotal Phase 3 MYR301 trial, while noting clinical outcome benefit has not yet been established and continued approval may depend on verification in confirmatory trial(s); Gilead reports a confirmatory long-term outcomes study is underway.
