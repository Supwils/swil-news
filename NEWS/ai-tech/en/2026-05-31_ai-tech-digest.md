## I. Policy & Regulation

### 1. U.S. Commerce Department Closes Overseas-Subsidiary Loophole for Advanced AI Chip Exports to China-HQ Firms (Export Controls)
**Summary:** CNBC, Reuters, and others reported on **Sunday, May 31, 2026** that the U.S. Department of Commerce's Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS) posted unusual weekend guidance clarifying that export licenses are required when selling advanced AI processors—including **Nvidia Blackwell/Rubin** and **AMD MI350x** chips—to entities **headquartered in China**, even if delivery occurs outside China (e.g., Malaysia or Singapore). The move aims to close a potential loophole created when the Trump administration announced in **May 2025** that it would not enforce the Biden-era **AI Diffusion** rule. Industry sources estimate **hundreds of thousands** of high-end chips may have reached Chinese-affiliated buyers via overseas subsidiaries over the past year. The guidance does **not** require data centers to stop using already-installed chips or cut off servicing; Nvidia and AMD had not immediately commented at time of reporting.

- [CryptoBriefing — US Commerce Department closes loophole on Nvidia chip exports to China](https://cryptobriefing.com/commerce-department-closes-nvidia-chip-export-loophole/)

### 2. Illinois SB 315 Passes: First U.S. State Law Mandating Third-Party Frontier AI Safety Audits, Backed by OpenAI and Anthropic (Regulation)
**Summary:** Capitol News Illinois, byteiota, and others reported **May 27–31** that Illinois **SB 315** (Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act) passed the House **110–0** and heads to Governor **J.B. Pritzker**, who has pledged to sign. The bill targets developers with over **$500 million** in annual revenue deploying frontier models, requiring public safety transparency frameworks, pre-deployment catastrophic-risk assessments, reporting of critical safety incidents within **72 hours**, and **annual independent third-party audits**—with penalties up to **$3 million** per violation. OpenAI's Chris Lehane and Anthropic publicly supported the bill. Effective date: **January 1, 2028**. This contrasts with the White House **May 21** postponement of a federal AI model review executive order—state-level regulation is filling the federal vacuum.

**Links:**
