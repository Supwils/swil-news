## I. AI Regulation & Platform Competition

### 1. EU DMA: Commission Proposes Google Share Search Rankings, Queries, Clicks, and Impressions with AI Chatbots
**Summary:** The European Commission published its preliminary remediation direction for Google under the Digital Markets Act, proposing that Google share search-related data — including rankings, queries, clicks, and impression data — with third-party search engines and AI chatbots on fair, reasonable, and non-discriminatory (FRAND) terms. The proposal explicitly included AI chatbots with search capability as eligible data recipients, aimed at reducing Google Search's competitive advantage by lowering the data cold-start cost for emerging AI search products. The measure was in the public consultation phase.
**Links:**
- [European Commission — Commission proposes measures to Google on sharing search engine data with third parties under Digital Markets Act](https://digital-markets-act.ec.europa.eu/commission-proposes-measures-google-sharing-search-engine-data-third-parties-under-digital-markets-2026-04-16_en)
**Commentary:** Forcing data sharing from an incumbent's core product to challengers is a standard structural competition remedy — the implementation battle will be in determining what data, at what latency, with what privacy constraints. The AI chatbot inclusion signals that regulators are treating LLM-based search as a direct competitive threat to Google's query monopoly, not a separate product market.

### 2. EU Commission Advances WhatsApp AI Access Antitrust Action Against Meta; Interim Measures Proposed
**Summary:** The European Commission issued a supplementary statement of objections in the WhatsApp third-party AI assistant access investigation (AT.41034), preliminarily finding that Meta's March 2026 fee framework for third-party AI assistant access was equivalent in effect to the prior exclusion, and proposing interim measures to restore access conditions to those prevailing before the October 15, 2025 policy change. The investigation was also announced to be expanded in cooperation with Italy's competition authority. Meta responded with concerns about "having small companies pay for large competitors."
**Links:**
- [Reuters — EU warns Meta WhatsApp AI fee breaches antitrust rules, orders rollback](https://www.reuters.com/world/eu-warns-meta-whatsapp-ai-fee-breaches-antitrust-rules-orders-rollback-2026-04-15/)

**Commentary:** Instant messaging is becoming an "invisible app store" for AI distribution — Brussels is betting with rare interim tools that whoever controls the default entry point for AI assistants gains a structural advantage in the assistant market that compounds over time.
