### 2. EU institutions reach a provisional political deal to simplify the AI Act and push back high-risk timelines (regulation)
**Summary:** According to Computerworld, citing European Parliament and Council readouts, negotiators reached a provisional agreement on **May 7, 2026** to adjust the EU AI Act package: standalone high-risk AI systems would face a new compliance timeline of **Dec. 2, 2027**, while AI embedded in products already covered by existing EU sectoral safety law would move to **Aug. 2, 2028** (against an earlier high-risk milestone tied to **Aug. 2, 2026**). The deal also covers reducing overlap with machinery rules, narrowing “safety component” triggers, sandbox timing, and advancing some watermarking obligations to **Dec. 2, 2026**; formal adoption by Parliament and Council is still required.
**Links:**
- [Computerworld — EU lawmakers strike provisional deal to soften AI Act](https://www.computerworld.com/article/4168467/eu-lawmakers-strike-provisional-deal-to-soften-ai-act.html)
- [Council of the EU — Council and Parliament agree to simplify and streamline rules on artificial intelligence](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/)
**Commentary:** This is a political pressure-release valve before a hard deadline: enterprises gain runway, but watermarking and prohibitions still move on accelerated clocks—implementation pressure does not disappear.

### 5. YouTube expands AI likeness detection to all users aged 18+ (platform safety)
**Summary:** The Verge reports YouTube is expanding its selfie-based “likeness detection” program—previously rolled out to creators, public officials, and entertainment figures—to **any user 18 or older** with a YouTube account, to hunt for potential deepfake-style misuse of a person’s face. Users can request removals after matches; YouTube says it evaluates factors like realism, AI labeling, and unique identifiability, with carveouts such as parody, and notes the tool covers facial likeness—not voice or other traits.
**Links:**
- [The Verge — YouTube is expanding its AI deepfake detection tool to all adult users](https://www.theverge.com/news/931884/youtube-likeness-detection-ai-deepfake-expansion-all-adults)
