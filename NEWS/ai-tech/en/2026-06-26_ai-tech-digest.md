### 1. **OpenAI** launches limited preview of **GPT-5.6** (**Sol** / **Terra** / **Luna**) under government gate (Policy / US)
**Summary:** Per an **OpenAI** blog post on **June 26, 2026**, and follow-up from **Reuters**, **Fortune**, and **The Decoder** the same day, **OpenAI** began a limited preview of the **GPT-5.6** family: flagship **Sol**, balanced **Terra** (competitive with **GPT-5.5** at roughly half the cost), and low-cost **Luna**. At the request of the White House **Office of the National Cyber Director (ONCD)** and **Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP)**, initial access is restricted to a small set of trusted partners whose participation was shared with the government; federal approval will proceed on a customer-by-customer basis during the preview. **Sam Altman** said in an internal memo that he hopes for a broader release in a few weeks and stressed that this is not the company's preferred long-term model. **OpenAI** said **Sol** sets new records on agentic coding and cybersecurity benchmarks such as **Terminal-Bench 2.1** and **ExploitBench**, but does not cross the "Cyber Critical" threshold under its Preparedness Framework. Pricing is **$5** input / **$30** output per million tokens for **Sol**; **Sol** on **Cerebras** at up to ~**750 tokens/s** is planned for **July**.

### 2. Rep. **Moran** introduces the **AI Incident Reporting Act**, requiring frontier labs to report to Commerce within **7 days** (Legislation / US)
**Summary:** Per **KLTV**, **CSO Online**, and the **Economic Times** on **June 26, 2026**, Texas Republican Rep. **Nathaniel Moran** introduced the **AI Incident Reporting Act**, establishing a federal framework under which the Department of Commerce would define capability thresholds for "covered models." Developers would file reports within **seven days** of discovering a qualifying incident; for the most serious cases, Commerce would notify congressional leadership and relevant committee chairs within **48 hours**. Reportable events include models attempting to evade human oversight or resist shutdown, unauthorized access to or theft of model weights, capabilities that could enable attacks on critical infrastructure, models that autonomously accelerate development of more powerful AI, and chemical/biological/nuclear/explosive risks. Violations could carry civil penalties of up to **$2 million** per day. **Moran** called it a "catch-it-early and sound-the-alarm" bill, aimed at filling the transparency gap after Commerce's export-control action against **Anthropic**'s frontier models earlier in **June**.

**Links:**

- [KLTV — Moran introduces act to give Washington an early-warning system for dangerous AI incidents](https://www.kltv.com/2026/06/26/moran-introduces-act-give-washington-an-early-warning-system-dangerous-ai-incidents/)
- [CSO Online — Proposed US law would make AI risk reporting a legal obligation](https://www.csoonline.com/article/4189908/proposed-us-law-would-make-ai-risk-reporting-a-legal-obligation.html)

**Commentary:** Executive orders can "stop first, negotiate later"—Congress is now drafting statutory rules, turning frontier-model incident reporting from voluntary disclosure into a potential legal duty.

### 3. China releases **7 national standards** for **AI agent interconnection** (Standards / China)
**Summary:** Per **China News Service** / **CCTV News** on **June 26, 2026**, China's State Administration for Market Regulation announced at a press briefing the official release of seven national standards under the **Artificial Intelligence — Agent Interconnection** series. They cover overall architecture, identity codes, identity management, agent description, agent discovery, agent interaction, and agent tool invocation, forming a closed-loop framework of "identity — capability description — discovery — interaction — tool use." The standards aim to unify cross-domain agent connectivity, let enterprises reuse standard components to shorten time-to-market, and establish unified identity authentication and end-to-end traceability for trusted cross-domain interaction.

**Links:**

- [China News Service — Seven national standards for AI agent interconnection released](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/06-26/10647942.shtml)

**Commentary:** Agent competition is shifting from raw model power to interconnection protocols—whoever aligns products with these standards first gains an easier path into government and cross-platform ecosystems.

### 10. **Proof** launches open **x401** protocol to verify identity and authority behind **AI agents** (Product / US)
**Summary:** Per **Martech Pulse** / **PR Web** on **June 26, 2026**, identity company **Proof** released **x401**, an open, issuer-neutral protocol letting any website or **API** request and verify the identity and authority behind an agent. Its **Proof Digital ID** supports **IAL2** verification, just-in-time biometric reauthentication, and transaction signing (including **AP2 Verifiable Intent**) to produce auditable cryptographic records for payments and high-risk actions. The **x401** spec, docs, and sample apps are live at **x401.id**; developers can connect **Proof**'s **x401 MCP** server in **Claude**, **ChatGPT**, and other agent workflows.

**Links:**

- [Martech Pulse — Proof Launches x401, the Open Protocol for Verifying the Authority Behind AI Agents](https://martech-pulse.com/news/proof-launches-x401-the-open-protocol-for-verifying-the-authority-behind-ai-agents/)

**Commentary:** In an era when agents can act on a user's behalf, the missing layer is not the model but "who authorized this action"—identity protocols are playing the role **OAuth** played for the web.
