## I. Policy & Regulation

### 1. EU Emergency Order: **Meta** Must Restore Free **WhatsApp** Access for Rival AI Assistants by **June 15** (Regulation / Antitrust)
**Summary:** On **Tuesday, June 10, 2026**, the **European Commission** issued a rare interim measure requiring **Meta** to restore **free WhatsApp access** for AI chatbots from rivals such as **OpenAI** while an antitrust probe continues. **Politico** notes this is only the **second time in 20+ years** the EU has used such emergency power; the investigation began in **December 2025** over whether Meta abused **WhatsApp** dominance to block third-party AI assistants. After effectively banning access in **October 2025**, Meta restored it **for a fee in March 2026** — which the Commission says amounts to the same outcome as a ban. Competition Commissioner **Teresa Ribera** said the step prevents "serious and irreparable damage" to the general-purpose AI assistant market. Meta must comply by **June 15** or face fines of up to roughly **$20 billion** (10% of annual revenue); the company plans to appeal, calling it "regulatory overreach" that subsidizes giant AI rivals at European businesses' expense.
