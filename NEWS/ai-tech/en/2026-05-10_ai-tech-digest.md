## I. Policy & Regulation (EU, US, and China)

### 1. EU strikes AI Act compromise: high-risk timelines slip; industry-facing rules largely shift
**Summary:** POLITICO and other outlets report that European Parliament and Council negotiators finalized a compromise after overnight talks, postponing key high-risk AI restrictions by more than a year—with a new focal deadline around December 2027—and largely routing industrial AI uses into existing product-safety frameworks (notably machinery), addressing German-led industry concerns about duplicated compliance. The direction also adjusts grace periods for watermarking-style duties (shorter than earlier proposals in press accounts) and adds prohibitions targeting abusive sexualized deepfakes and AI-generated child sexual abuse material, among other elements; the Commission publicly frames the package as simpler and more innovation-friendly while promising strengthened citizen protections.
