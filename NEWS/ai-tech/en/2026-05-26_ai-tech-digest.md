## II. Science, Evaluation, and Trust

### 3. UCSD work in *PNAS*: frontier LLMs can be mistaken for humans in a classic three-party Turing test (research)
**Summary:** Peer-reviewed experiments from UC San Diego, published in the *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, used preregistered, randomized three-party Turing tests (judges chat with one human and one system, then pick the human). With persona-style prompting, GPT-4.5 was chosen as the “human” about **73%** of the time—well above the rate for real humans—while Llama-3.1-405B was near **56%**, statistically hard to separate from humans. Without persona prompting, pass rates fell sharply, reviving debate about whether the test measures intelligence or sociolinguistic mimicry.

**Links:**

- [UC San Diego — AI can seem more human than real humans in a classic Turing test, study finds](https://today.ucsd.edu/story/ai-can-seem-more-human-than-real-humans-in-a-classic-turing-test-study-finds)
- [Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences — Large language models pass a standard three-party Turing test](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2524472123)

**Commentary:** For platforms, the actionable implication is identity, fraud, and bot mitigation—not a philosophical “AGI has arrived” banner; cheap humanlikeness scales abuse.
