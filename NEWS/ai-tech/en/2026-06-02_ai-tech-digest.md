### 2. Draft EU Cloud and AI Development Act Could Sideline U.S. Hyperscalers in Sensitive Public Tenders (Regulation)
**Summary:** A **June 1** Reuters exclusive, followed by outlets on **June 2**, says the European Commission plans a **Cloud and AI Development Act** under tech chief **Henna Virkkunen** (reported unveiling **June 4**) as part of a sovereignty package. Draft rules for banking, energy, healthcare, and defense cloud procurement would add non-price criteria—EU-developed software/hardware, third-country control assessments, market reciprocity—that could effectively exclude **AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud** from some highly critical contracts, with the Commission also acting as a central buyer for data-center and AI services. The three hyperscalers together hold **60%+** global cloud share and have launched EU-local ventures to address sovereignty concerns.
**Links:**
