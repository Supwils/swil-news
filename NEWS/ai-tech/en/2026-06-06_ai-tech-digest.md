### 1. "Great American AI Act" Draft Faces Union and Democratic Commission Backlash: Three-Year State Preemption Sparks Revolt (Regulation)
**Summary:** After bipartisan lawmakers released the **269-page** **Great American Artificial Intelligence Act of 2026** discussion draft on **June 4, 2026**, coverage on **June 6** highlighted mounting opposition: **TechTimes** reported the House Commission on AI and the Innovation Economy rejected the draft as inadequate on release day, while the **AFL-CIO** and consumer groups issued hard "no" votes. Led by **Jay Obernolte (R-CA)**, **Lori Trahan (D-MA)**, and others, the draft would require frontier developers with more than **$500 million** in gross revenue to undergo semi-annual **NIST CAISI**-licensed **IVO** audits and report critical safety incidents, with civil penalties up to **$1 million** per violation, while preempting state laws that "specifically regulate the development" of AI models for **three years**—but preserving state authority over **deployment, consumer harms, elections, child safety**, and sector-specific risks. **FedScoop** notes the draft would codify **CAISI** and authorize **$100 million** per year for fiscal **2027–2029**; the Senate voted **99–1** against a similar preemption provision in **July 2025**. The draft has not been formally introduced and has no floor timeline.

- [FedScoop — Bipartisan 'Great American AI Act' draft proposes new federal AI governance framework](https://fedscoop.com/bipartisan-great-american-ai-act-draft-proposes-new-federal-ai-governance-framework/)

### 6. OpenAI Expands **Lockdown Mode** to Personal ChatGPT Accounts: Deterministically Disables Network Tools Against Prompt-Injection Exfiltration (Product / Security)
**Summary:** An **OpenAI** update on **June 4, 2026** says **Lockdown Mode** is rolling out to personal **ChatGPT** and self-serve **ChatGPT Business** accounts (previously limited to Enterprise/Edu plans).
