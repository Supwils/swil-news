## I. Platform Governance & Search

### 1. Google explicitly treats manipulation of generative AI answers as web-search spam (Search / GEO)
**Summary:** Outlets including The Times of India summarize a recent update to Google’s web spam policies: attempts to manipulate generative AI outputs in Google Search (including AI Overviews and AI Mode) are treated similarly to traditional ranking manipulation. Reporting cites tactics such as “recommendation poisoning,” where hidden on-page content tries to make a domain look like an authoritative source to a language model. Google’s documentation states that violating sites may rank lower or be removed from results, using automated systems and, when needed, human review.
**Links:**
- [The Times of India — Google is making changes to how results are shown in AI Overview or AI Mode in Search](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/technology/tech-news/google-is-making-changes-to-how-results-are-shown-in-ai-overview-or-ai-mode-in-search/articleshow/131153864.cms)
- [Google Search Central — Spam policies for Google web search](https://support.google.com/webmasters/answer/66353)
**Commentary:** When models “read the web for you,” SEO and generative-engine optimization stop being keyword games and start becoming a fight over which sources are quotable—so policy and detection have to harden in parallel.
