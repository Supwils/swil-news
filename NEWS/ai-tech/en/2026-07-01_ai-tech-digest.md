### 2. China's first overseas-investment administrative regulation takes effect today (Policy / China)
**Summary:** Per **Gov.cn**, **People's Daily**, and **China Economic Net** on **July 1, 2026**, the *Regulations on Overseas Investment of the State Council* (State Council Order No. **837**), signed by Premier **Li Qiang**, takes effect today as China's first administrative regulation dedicated to outbound investment, with **34** articles. The rules bring individual domestic residents under the regulatory scope, establish overseas investment security review and tiered whole-process supervision, strictly limit cross-border transfers of prohibited or restricted goods, technology, data, and related services, impose graduated fines and industry bans for violations, and create countermeasure mechanisms against discriminatory overseas investment restrictions. Analysts say this marks a shift from scattered rules to systematic, rule-of-law governance of outbound investment.
**Links:**

- [Gov.cn — Li Qiang signs State Council order on overseas investment regulations](https://www.gov.cn/yaowen/liebiao/202606/content_7070766.htm)
- [People's Daily — New overseas investment rules take effect today](http://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0701/c1004-40751441.html)

**Commentary:** As AI chip and data cross-border disputes heat up, the first dedicated outbound-investment regulation hard-codes "technology, data, and services leaving the country" into mandatory compliance — going global is no longer just a business call but a national-security review object.
