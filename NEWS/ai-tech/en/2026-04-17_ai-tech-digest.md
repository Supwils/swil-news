## I. AI Regulation & Policy

### 1. EU DMA: Commission Proposes Google Share Search Data with AI Chatbots and Third Parties
**Summary:** The European Commission advanced a proposal under the Digital Markets Act requiring Google to share search data — including rankings, queries, clicks, and impressions — with third parties including AI chatbots and search competitors. The proposal set a July timeline for implementation and was framed as a structural remedy to give emerging AI search products access to the scale of behavioral data that Google's dominant position has accumulated. Google was expected to contest key aspects of the implementation scope.
**Links:**
- [Financial Times — EU DMA Google search data ruling](https://www.ft.com/)
- [European Commission — DMA enforcement update](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/)
**Commentary:** Forcing data sharing between an incumbent's core product and its challengers is a classic structural remedy in competition law — the devil is in what data, at what latency, with what privacy constraints. The AI chatbot inclusion signals that regulators are treating LLM-based search as a direct competitive threat to Google's query monopoly.
