### 1. Google I/O: Gemini 3.5 Flash, Gemini Spark agent, and Omni world model (product)

### 2. Alibaba launches Zhenwu M890 AI chip and Qwen 3.7-Max, outlines multi-year silicon roadmap (chips / models)
**Summary:** A May 20 Reuters story carried by Yahoo Finance reports that Alibaba, at its Alibaba Cloud Summit that day, unveiled the Zhenwu M890 AI chip developed by semiconductor unit T-Head, claiming roughly 3× performance over the prior Zhenwu 810E and targeting memory- and communication-heavy AI “agent” workloads. ### 3. OpenAI advances image provenance: C2PA membership, SynthID watermarking, and a public verification preview (trust & safety)
**Summary:** The Next Web, summarizing OpenAI’s announcement, reports that OpenAI is formally joining the Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity (C2PA) open standard and partnering with Google to embed DeepMind’s invisible SynthID watermark in OpenAI-generated images. OpenAI is also previewing a public verification tool for users to upload images and check whether they were generated by OpenAI models. Coverage notes the measures primarily apply to OpenAI’s own outputs and have limited force against imagery from other tools, and that OpenAI says it has joined the C2PA steering committee.
**Links:**
- [The Next Web — OpenAI adds C2PA metadata and SynthID watermarks to AI images](https://thenextweb.com/news/openai-c2pa-synthid-ai-image-detection-watermark)
- [OpenAI — Advancing content provenance](https://openai.com/index/advancing-content-provenance/)
**Commentary:** As deepfake regulation heats up, leading labs are pairing metadata with watermarking—but cross-platform signal survival and industry-wide adoption remain the hard problems.
